# The Sunsword
**Category:** Artifact (Legendary Weapon)  
**Type:** Longsword  
**Acquired:** Month 4, Week 2 — Amber Temple (Vampyr's sarcophagus), empowered by summoning Sergei von Zarovich  
**Foretold by:** Madam Eva's Tarokka reading (Card 3)

---

## Description
A longsword with a hilt of black iron and gold filigree. When inactive (hilt only), it looks like the handle of a broken sword. When Sergei's spirit empowers it, a blade of pure sunlight extends from the hilt — warm, brilliant, and humming with contained energy.

The light it produces is real sunlight. It smells like morning.

---

## Properties (Full, After Sergei's Empowerment)
- **Damage:** 1d8 slashing + **2d8 radiant** bonus damage (against all creatures, doubled effect vs undead)
- **Sunlight Radius:** Sheds bright sunlight in a **15-foot radius** and dim light for an additional 15 feet
  - Vampires and creatures with Sunlight Sensitivity have disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks while in the bright light
  - Strahd's **Regeneration** trait does not function while he is in the sunlight radius
- **Suppress Light (Bonus Action):** The wielder can suppress or restore the light as a bonus action
- **Finesse:** Can use Dexterity for attack and damage rolls
- **+3 to attack and damage rolls**

---

## Properties (Hilt Only — Before Empowerment)
The hilt alone still has faint magic:
- **+1 to attack and damage rolls**
- **Detect Undead (Bonus Action, 3/day):** The hilt pulses warmly when undead are within 30 feet
- No light; no radiant damage

---

## Acquiring the Sword
**Step 1 — Retrieve the hilt:** Inside the Vampyr's amber sarcophagus at the Amber Temple. Patrina Velikovna's spirit guards it. Exethanter can create a protection circle to prevent Vampyr interference during the fight.

**Step 2 — Summon Sergei:** Using the summoning circle Exethanter draws, call Sergei von Zarovich from the Mists. He needs to hear the party's genuine intention:
- **Wrong answer (weakened result):** "To kill Strahd" — sword functions but no sunlight, +2d8 reduced to +1d8
- **Right answer (full empowerment):** Any sincere version of "to free Barovia / end the suffering / give them their lives back"

**Sergei's final words:** "Tell my brother I forgive him. Whatever good that does either of us."

---

## In Combat Against Strahd (Final Battle)
The Sunsword is the single most important tactical asset of the final fight:

| Strahd Ability | Sunsword Effect |
|---------------|----------------|
| Regeneration (20 HP/turn) | **SUPPRESSED** while in the blade's 15-ft light |
| Darkness spell | Light cuts through magical darkness |
| Misty Escape | Strahd cannot escape into mist while the blade touches him |
| Legendary Resistances | Unaffected — he still has 3 |
| Coffin regeneration | If Strahd reaches his coffin, the Sunsword's light prevents reformation |

## Lore
The Sunsword was forged for Sergei von Zarovich as a wedding gift — a blade of sunlight for Barovia's golden prince. When Strahd murdered Sergei, the sword shattered. The hilt fell into the Mists. Patrina Velikovna, hiding in the Amber Temple, found it and placed it inside the Vampyr's sarcophagus as a hidden phylactery for her own spirit — protecting it while using it as an anchor.

It has been waiting 300 years for someone to ask Sergei for it the right way.

---

## Who Should Wield It
Any martial class benefits most (Fighter, Paladin, Ranger). A Paladin adds their Divine Smite for devastating single-target burst damage. 

If a spellcaster must wield it: they can still use the light-suppression and the attack bonuses. The Sunsword's light alone (as a torch that hurts Strahd) is valuable even if the wielder isn't making attack rolls with it.
