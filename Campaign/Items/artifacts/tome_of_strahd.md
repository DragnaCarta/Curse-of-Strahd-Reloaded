# Tome of Strahd
**Category:** Artifact (Quest Item / Lore Object)  
**Acquired:** Tarokka-dependent location (set before Session 1)  
**Foretold by:** Madam Eva's Tarokka reading (Card 1)

---

## Description
A thick book bound in cracked black leather. The cover bears no title — only the embossed silhouette of a bat in flight. The pages are dense with cramped writing in a cipher. Some pages are water-stained. Some pages are stained with something darker. The last entry is dated approximately 80 years ago.

The book smells of old paper, candlewax, and something that might be blood.

---

## What It Contains
The Tome of Strahd is Strahd's personal journal spanning several centuries. Written in his private cipher (taught to him by Exethanter at the Amber Temple — a fact Exethanter will remember with guilt when his memory is restored).

**Readable only after decoding.** Exethanter can decode it in one hour if given the book (Month 4, Week 2).

### Contents (After Decoding)
**Section 1 — His History:**
Strahd's own account of his conquest of the valley, his first sight of Tatyana, his pact with the Dark Powers, the wedding, the murder of Sergei, Tatyana's death. Written with the detachment of someone describing events that happened to a different person. The detachment is more disturbing than rage would be.

**Section 2 — Centuries of Observation:**
Records of Tatyana's reincarnations across dozens of generations. Names, descriptions, dates of birth and death. Each entry ends the same way: "She would not stay." The repetition is devastating.

**Section 3 — The Grand Conjunction Plan:**
The book's most dangerous section. Strahd's complete plan:
> *"The Conjunction requires four elements. First: the Fanes, desecrated and bound to me — done. Second: the divine vessel, the Seeker's blood — Arabelle, the girl of the Vistani. Third: the souls of this valley, harvested in sufficient quantity by the Heart. Fourth: a willing sacrifice to satisfy the Powers — Rahadin will not know. He has never asked what his loyalty costs him."*

**What this reveals to the party:**
- Arabelle's specific importance (she must be protected, not just returned to Luvash)
- Rahadin is to be sacrificed without his knowledge
- The Heart of Sorrow is the soul-harvesting device
- All three Fanes must be reconsecrated to break Strahd's land-bond

**Section 4 — Personal Notes:**
Observations on individual Barovians across centuries. Brief, clinical. And then, scattered throughout, moments that crack the facade: a sketch of Tatyana's face. A pressed flower from a century-old garden. The sentence: "I am told that monsters cannot love. I wonder if they are wrong, or if I am simply not what they think."

## Telling the Party What It Says
When decoded, read the Grand Conjunction section aloud. This is the campaign's "mission briefing" moment — players realize:
1. They need to protect Arabelle specifically (not just return her)
2. Rahadin doesn't know he's meant to be sacrificed
3. The Heart of Sorrow is the physical object they need to destroy
4. The Fane reconsecration they planned is exactly right

**The personal section:** Let players read excerpts at their own pace. The sketch and the pressed flower are more unsettling than any monster. Don't force it — let the players choose to engage with Strahd's interiority or not.

---

## Combat Properties
The Tome has no combat stats. It is not a weapon. Its power is entirely informational — but that information wins the final battle.
