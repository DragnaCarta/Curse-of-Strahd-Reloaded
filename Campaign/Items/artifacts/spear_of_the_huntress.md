# Spear of the Huntress
**Category:** Artifact (Legendary Weapon / Quest Item)  
**Acquired:** Month 3, Week 4 — King Dostron's crypt, Castle Ravenloft (Ravenloft heist)  
**Required For:** Reconsecrating the Forest Fane at Yester Hill (Month 4, Week 3)

---

## Description
A spear roughly six feet long, silver-headed with a shaft of blackened ashwood. The head is engraved with a wolf's head and a crescent moon. It does not look old — it looks timeless, which is different. When held, the wielder occasionally smells pine forests and open sky, and hears (very faintly, not quite audible) the sound of a hunt in full cry.

The Huntress's divine energy is still present in it. It is paying attention.

---

## Properties (As a Weapon)
- **+2 spear** (+2 to attack and damage rolls)
- **Damage:** 1d6+2 piercing (1d8+2 two-handed)
- **Return (Bonus Action):** If thrown, the spear returns to the wielder's hand at the start of their next turn
- **Hunter's Mark (3/day):** Cast Hunter's Mark without expending a spell slot (concentration, 1 hour)
- **Lycanthrope Bane:** Deals +2d6 piercing damage against lycanthropes (in addition to the +2 bonus)
- **Nature's Call:** Beasts and plants within 30 feet recognize the spear's divine nature — neutral beasts will not attack the wielder while the spear is held openly

---

## Primary Purpose: The Forest Fane
The spear is specifically the key to reconsecrating the Forest Fane at Yester Hill. When driven into the earth at the Fane's stone circle:

> "The spear drives into the earth and does not stop at bedrock — it sinks further, and from it, roots spread. Silver-green light traces along the carved sigil. The hill shudders. Every tree around the circle straightens slightly."

After this: the spear is consumed by the earth. It is gone. The Fane is consecrated. **Do not let the party forget it can only be used once this way** — if they spend it in combat before Month 4, Week 3, the Forest Fane cannot be reconsecrated.

**DM note:** The party may be attached to the spear as a weapon by Month 4. The sacrifice of it to the Fane should feel meaningful. Let them mourn it briefly. The effect — removing Strahd's forest lair actions — is worth more than the weapon.

---

## Acquiring It (Ravenloft Heist)
King Dostron's crypt is in the dungeon level of Castle Ravenloft, two rooms past the cells where Emil Toranescu is held. The crypt is sealed with a simple ward (DC 14 Arcana to identify and suppress).

Inside: the preserved body of an ancient Barovian king, pre-Strahd era, hands folded around the spear. When the spear is taken, the body crumbles to dust. The room fills with the smell of pine and rain.

**No combat required** — the crypt is passive. The king's spirit left long ago. He left the spear for exactly this purpose.

---

## Lore
The Huntress (one of the Ladies Three, goddess of summer, war, hunting, and passion) gifted lycanthropy to the wolfir — the first werewolves — as a form of power and communion with the natural world. The Spear of the Huntress was her physical connection to the Forest Fane, driven into the earth as an anchor of her divine presence.

Strahd removed it from the Fane during his desecration. King Dostron, the last king before Strahd's conquest, took it from the battlefield and placed it in his tomb — knowing someone would eventually need it.

King Dostron has been dead 400 years. He was right.
