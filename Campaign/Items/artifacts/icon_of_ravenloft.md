# Icon of Ravenloft (Icon of Dawn's Grace)
**Category:** Artifact (Legendary Wondrous Item / Quest Item)  
**Acquired:** Month 3, Week 4 — St. Markovia's crypt, Castle Ravenloft (Ravenloft heist)  
**Required For:** Redeeming the Abbot (Arc K resolution, Month 3, Week 4)

---

## Description
A golden sunburst about eight inches across, mounted on a short stand. It radiates warmth. Not metaphorical warmth — physical, measurable warmth, like holding a lamp that doesn't burn. In the darkness of Ravenloft's crypts it glows faintly on its own.

Saint Markovia consecrated it herself. It has been in the crypt since before Strahd took the castle — he locked the crypt and never entered it. The Icon has been alone in the dark for four hundred years. It is still warm.

---

## Properties
**Passive — Emanate Grace (always active while held):**
- Undead within 15 feet have disadvantage on attack rolls against the holder
- The holder cannot be charmed by undead while holding the Icon

**Active — Radiant Burst (Action, 1/day):**
- 20-foot radius burst of radiant light
- All undead in range: DC 15 Constitution or take 4d10 radiant damage and are pushed 10 feet away
- Half damage, no push on a save

**Active — Consecrate (Ritual, 10 minutes, 1/week):**
- Consecrates a 30-foot radius area as holy ground
- Undead cannot willingly enter the area for 24 hours
- Sacred Flame and Guiding Bolt spells cast within the area deal maximum damage

**Special — Redemption:**
When presented to a fallen celestial (specifically the Abbot), the Icon channels radiant energy directly into the creature:
- DC 18 Wisdom save (the Abbot automatically fails — his corruption makes him unable to resist the Morninglord's grace)
- On failure: 200 years of corruption are stripped away; the creature returns to its original celestial alignment
- This use does not consume the Icon — it can still be used normally afterward

---

## Primary Purpose: Redeeming the Abbot
The Icon is THE key to the Abbot's redemption arc. Without it, the Abbot must be fought (CR 10 deva fight) or left corrupt. With it, two centuries of sincere misguided service are instantly, painfully redeemed.

**The moment:**
> "The Abbot holds the Icon for a long time. Then the golden light intensifies — pouring into him, through him, stripping away the grey calcification of two centuries. He falls to his knees. When he looks up, his eyes are clear. 'What have I done. What have I *done*.'"

The Abbot then destroys Vasilka himself — gently, grieving — and becomes a genuine healer and ally.

---

## Saint Markovia
The Icon belonged to Saint Markovia, a cleric of the Morninglord who fought Strahd directly when he first rose as a vampire. She died in the assault on Castle Ravenloft. She failed to destroy him. She did not stop trying until she was dead.

Her crypt in Castle Ravenloft is sealed with a ward against evil — meaning none of Strahd's undead can enter it. He sealed the crypt from the outside centuries ago and has not thought about it since. He finds her memory irritating.

The ward on the crypt is visible to Detect Magic: a warm golden light around the door frame. The seal is not hostile to the party (they are not undead). It simply opens for them.

---

## After Redemption — Secondary Uses
Post-Abbot-arc, the Icon retains its properties and can be used in the final battle or for consecrating defensive positions during the war council (Month 4, Week 4).

**War council use:** Consecrate the town square of Vallaki as a fallback defensive position — undead cannot enter for 24 hours, buying the civilians time.
