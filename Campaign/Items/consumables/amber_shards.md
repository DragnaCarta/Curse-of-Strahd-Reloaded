# Amber Shards — Vestige Dark Gifts
**Category:** Consumable (Cursed — Dark Gift Delivery)  
**Source:** Amber Temple sarcophagi (Month 4, Week 2)  
**Warning:** These are intentional temptations. Track carefully.

---

## Description
Translucent fragments of amber, each roughly the size of a finger bone. Some are warm. Some are cold. Some vibrate slightly. Each one carries a whisper — not loud enough to make out words until held in a closed fist.

They are splinters of the sarcophagi that hold the vestiges of dead evil gods. They carry fractions of the vestiges' power and, crucially, fractions of their corruption.

---

## How They Work
A character who holds an amber shard and *chooses to accept it* receives a Dark Gift:
1. A mechanical benefit (powerful, immediately useful)
2. A physical change (subtle at first, escalating)
3. A personality corruption (slow, cumulative)

**DC 14 Wisdom saving throw to resist the whisper** when first picking up a shard. On a failure, the shard "feels right." On a success: the character can still accept it voluntarily — but they know what they're doing.

---

## Sample Dark Gifts (by Vestige)

### The Vampyr's Gift — *Vampire's Resilience*
**Benefit:** Resistance to necrotic damage; regain 5 HP at the start of each turn if you have at least 1 HP  
**Physical change:** Skin becomes slightly paler each week; eyes shift toward red in low light  
**Personality corruption:** Growing hunger — not for food, but for life energy. Prolonged contact with dying creatures becomes... interesting.  
**Escalation:** If 3+ gifts are accepted, the character begins to need blood to maintain HP regeneration (1 HD worth of blood per long rest or the regeneration stops)

### The Tenebrous's Gift — *Shadow Step*
**Benefit:** Bonus action to teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space in dim light or darkness  
**Physical change:** The character casts no shadow in bright light  
**Personality corruption:** Aversion to direct sunlight (not mechanical at first — just preference; escalates to disadvantage on attack rolls in direct sunlight after 3 sessions)  

### The Dahlver-Nar's Gift — *Fearlessness*
**Benefit:** Immunity to the Frightened condition; advantage on saving throws against magical fear  
**Physical change:** Eyes become completely black (no iris, no white) when in dim light  
**Personality corruption:** Inability to feel fear translates, slowly, into inability to feel danger — the character begins taking risks they shouldn't

### The Zantras' Gift — *Compelling Presence*
**Benefit:** Advantage on all Charisma checks; can cast *Charm Person* 3/day without spell slots (DC 14)  
**Physical change:** Attractiveness shifts toward unsettling perfection  
**Personality corruption:** Growing belief that the character's wants are inherently more important than others'

---

## Stacking Dark Gifts
Each additional gift accepted after the first:
- Adds its mechanical benefit
- Accelerates all physical changes (visible within days instead of weeks)
- **DC increases by 2** for all personality corruption checks
- At 3 gifts: the character's alignment shifts one step toward Evil
- At 4+ gifts: the character is effectively under the influence of the original vestige — they may receive visions, urges, or eventually direct communication

---

## Removing Dark Gifts
- *Greater Restoration:* Removes one dark gift (all effects, all corruption)
- *Wish:* Removes all dark gifts
- Willpower: A character can resist the corruption at the DM's discretion with consistent strong roleplay — but cannot remove the mechanical changes without magic

---

## DM Guidance
**These are meant to be tempting.** The party is at Level 14+ when they reach the Amber Temple. They may genuinely want the mechanical benefit. That's the point.

**Don't force them.** But don't hide the appeal either. Present the offer as the vestiges do: genuinely useful, clearly costly, and phrased in the language of the character's exact desires.

**The Vampyr specifically:** It whispers in Strahd's voice. This is intentional and deeply unsettling. "I accepted this once. Look what it gave me. Look what it cost."

**Tracking corruption:** Use a private note to each player with a "corruption level" (1–4). Be transparent about what Level 1 feels like vs. Level 3 — players should make informed choices.
