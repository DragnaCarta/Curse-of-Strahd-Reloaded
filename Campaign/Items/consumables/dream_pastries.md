# Dream Pastries
**Category:** Consumable (Narcotic)  
**Source:** Morgantha; Old Bonegrinder windmill  
**Price:** 2 copper pieces each  
**Session Relevance:** Introduced Month 1, Week 4; full significance Month 2, Week 4

---

## Description
Small pastries — individually wrapped in cloth — that smell extraordinary. Not just food-good. The smell is every comfort you've ever known, layered: the kitchen at home, a specific autumn afternoon, something you've been missing without knowing it.

They taste even better than they smell. The flavor is different for every person who eats one — it tastes like whatever that person most needs to feel safe.

---

## Mechanical Effects
**On consumption:** The eater falls into a deep sleep within 10 minutes (Constitution DC 14 to resist the sleep; on a success, they still feel drowsy for 1 hour with -2 to Perception). The sleep lasts 8 hours and cannot be interrupted by noise or physical movement (shaking, cold water, etc. — magical means still work).

**The Dream:** While asleep, the eater experiences a perfect, personalized dream — whatever they most want to experience. A home they've lost. A person they miss. A version of their life that went differently. The dream is not interactive (they can't change it) but it is completely real while it lasts.

**Upon waking:** The eater feels hollow. Not terrible — just empty in a specific way, like something was taken out to make room for the dream. Their maximum HP is reduced by **1d4** until they complete a long rest *without* eating a dream pastry.

**Addiction:** After eating 3 or more dream pastries (cumulative, even across days), the character must succeed on **DC 15 Constitution** each day without a pastry or gain one level of Exhaustion from withdrawal. This persists until a *Greater Restoration* or *Remove Curse* is cast on them.

---

## The Children at Old Bonegrinder
Three children sit in the mud at the windmill's base, eating pastries with dull eyes. They are alive — their bodies are fine. Their spirits are slowly being hollowed out. They have been eating pastries for weeks.

**Freeing them from addiction:**
- Victor Vallakovich can break the narcotic hold (DC 14 Arcana per child, consuming a spell slot each)
- *Remove Curse* works (DC 14 Con save, advantage if cast gently and the child isn't frightened)
- Separated from the supply for 7 days with care: Con DC 14 per day (each success reduces hold by one stage)

---

## What's Actually in Them
Morgantha's dream pastries contain a distillation of trapped souls — not full souls, but fragments of the dreams and memories of people who have already been hollowed out by addiction. The eater literally consumes another person's happiest memories, replacing their own capacity for happiness with a borrowed echo.

This is knowable via *Detect Magic* (conjuration school, with a necrotic thread) or via a DC 15 Arcana check after eating one and examining the effect.

---

## Morgantha's Route
Morgantha travels from Old Bonegrinder to Vallaki twice a week, selling to her regular customers. She has approximately 20 regular customers in Vallaki — mostly the poorest residents, the ones who have least hope. She considers this a fair transaction. She provides exactly what they asked for.

She has never asked whether they want to stop.

---

## Notes for DM
- The dream pastries are a slow-moving social horror element, not a combat element
- If a party member eats one (voluntarily): give them their dream. Make it beautiful. Make the hollowness after feel exactly right.
- The addiction mechanic should not punish players who engage with the story — it should feel like the cost the narrative demands
- The children at the windmill should be the party's motivation to do something about Morgantha, not the combat
