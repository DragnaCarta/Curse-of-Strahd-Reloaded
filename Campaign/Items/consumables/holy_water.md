# Holy Water
**Category:** Consumable (Sacred Weapon)  
**Sources:** Father Lucian (Vallaki), Father Donavich (Barovia), The Abbot (post-redemption), any cleric with *Create or Destroy Water* + consecration  
**Price:** 25 gp per flask (standard flask = 1 use)

---

## Description
Water in a small sealed glass vial, sealed with a wax stamp bearing the Morninglord's sun. Indistinguishable from regular water until used. Doesn't glow, doesn't feel special. It has been prayed over. That's all. That turns out to be enough.

---

## Combat Use
**Splash attack (Action):** Make a ranged attack (range 20/60 ft) against a creature:
- On hit vs undead or fiend: **2d6 radiant damage**
- On hit vs other creatures: no damage (mundane water)
- Can douse an area instead: 5-foot square with a pour, no attack roll

**For clerics / paladins:** Holy water as a weapon is always available, but casting *Sacred Flame* or *Divine Smite* deals more damage — holy water is for non-casters to contribute radiant damage

---

## Non-Combat Use

**Consecrate terrain:** Pour holy water in a 10-foot circle; that circle is consecrated for 1 hour. Undead crossing it take 1d4 radiant damage per round they occupy it.

**Detect undead disguises:** Splash on a suspected vampire or undead — vampires must succeed on DC 14 Charisma or visibly recoil. The reaction is involuntary. It is very difficult to fake not reacting to holy water.

**Bless wounds:** Applied to a wound sustained from an undead attack (shadow's Strength drain, ghoul's paralysis, etc.): advantage on the next saving throw to end the effect.

**Anti-vampire entrance:** A line of holy water across a threshold acts as a ward — vampires cannot cross the line willingly (DC 18 Wisdom to resist the aversion; they can force themselves with the check).

---

## Supply in Barovia

**Father Lucian Petrovich (Vallaki):**
- Always has 2d6 flasks available
- Will give free to anyone who asks and seems genuine
- Will give extra to the party once they return the Bones of St. Andral
- Can consecrate a new batch in 1 hour given clean water

**Father Donavich (Village of Barovia):**
- Has 1d4 flasks; in poor condition but still functional
- If Doru is resolved peacefully, he becomes more active and keeps better supply (1d8 flasks)

**The Abbot (post-redemption, Month 3, Week 4+):**
- An extremely powerful source — his consecrations are more potent
- His holy water deals 2d6+2 radiant instead of 2d6
- He can create 10 flasks per hour

**Party clerics/paladins:**
- Any cleric or paladin with *Create or Destroy Water* can create holy water: the spell + 1 hour of prayer + the character must be genuinely attuned to a divine power
- Cost: One 1st-level spell slot; yields 1 flask

---

## Notes for DM
- Holy water is one of the most consistently useful tools in the campaign — keep it available
- At high levels (12+) it deals relatively little damage; its greatest value is the consecration/detection uses
- In the final battle: a perimeter of holy water around the party's defensive position slows vampire spawn significantly
- The Abbot's more potent holy water is a good reward for completing his redemption arc
