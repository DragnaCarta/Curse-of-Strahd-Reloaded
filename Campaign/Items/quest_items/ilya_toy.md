# Ilya's Carved Wooden Horse
**Category:** Quest Item (Personal Effect)  
**Source:** Baron Krezkov (given when the party mentions Baba Lysaga's demands)  
**Required For:** Baba Lysaga's scrying ritual — she uses it to watch Ilya from Berez  
**Session:** Month 2, Week 4 / Month 3 crossover (Krezk arc)

---

## Description
A small carved horse, roughly four inches long, made of pale birch wood. Crudely carved — a child's toy, not a craftsman's work. One ear is slightly chipped. The mane is scratched with rough parallel lines. It looks exactly like what it is: something a father made for his son with a knife and an afternoon.

Baron Krezkov carries it on his person at all times. He does not explain why. He does not need to.

---

## The Situation
Baba Lysaga holds **Ilya Krezkov**, the baron's young son, captive in Berez. The arrangement is this: Ilya is alive and relatively unharmed as long as Baron Krezkov cooperates with whatever Lysaga asks (primarily: information about travelers entering Krezk, and silence about certain activities in the swamp). Lysaga uses Ilya's toy — which she took when she took the boy — to scry on Ilya and confirm he is behaving.

Krezkov gave the party a copy of the toy he carved from memory, because the original is in Lysaga's possession. **The copy doesn't function for scrying.** Lysaga will know the difference if she examines it.

---

## Why the Party Has It
The party needs to understand Krezkov's constraint — why a burgomaster who is clearly uncomfortable about something refuses to talk openly, and why he cannot simply send for help. The toy is how he shows them what words would put Ilya at risk to say aloud.

When he hands it to a party member: "Baba Lysaga has his." He says nothing else. He doesn't have to.

---

## Resolving the Arc
When the party defeats or deals with Baba Lysaga in Act IV (Month 4, Week 3), Ilya can be recovered from Berez. His original toy is in Lysaga's hut — on a shelf, near her scrying bowl.

Returning both Ilya and the original toy to Baron Krezkov is one of the campaign's quieter victories. He takes the boy, then takes the toy, holds them both for a moment.

He doesn't say anything. He doesn't need to.

---

## Notes
- The toy has no combat use, no magic, no mechanical properties
- Its function is to show Krezkov's helplessness and explain his cooperation with Lysaga
- Track who is carrying it — it should not be lost or sold
- If the party tries to use it as leverage with Lysaga: she knows it's a copy immediately (DC 15 Insight or she simply tells them) — she has had the original for months
