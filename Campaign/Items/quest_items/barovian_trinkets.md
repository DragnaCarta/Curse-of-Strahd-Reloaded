# Barovian Trinkets — The Hooks Into the Mists
**Category:** Quest Item (One per player character)  
**Session:** Month 1, Week 1 — given before or at the start of Session 1  
**Purpose:** The magical draw that pulls each character individually toward Barovia

---

## What They Are

Each party member has come into possession of a small, seemingly mundane object that has recently begun behaving strangely — tugging at them, warming in their pocket, appearing back after being discarded. They don't know why. They may not even realize all of them have one until they compare notes.

These are not powerful magic items. They are not weapons or tools. They are **anchors** — personal objects tied to something in Barovia that called out across the Mists and latched onto the nearest available soul. Once in Barovia, they lose their pull. They become keepsakes.

**Key rule:** They cannot be permanently discarded. If thrown away, they reappear in a pocket within the hour. If destroyed, they reform by morning. This stops being relevant once the party crosses into Barovia — the Mists have what they wanted.

---

## Suggested Trinkets (One Per Player — Customize to Each PC)

Assign one before Session 1. Ideally flavor it to something in the character's backstory — a lost family member, a hometown, a specific fear or longing. The trinket should feel *personal*.

| Trinket | Description | The Pull |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Tarnished silver locket** | Contains a tiny painted portrait — but the face is no one the owner recognizes | Grows warm and tugs eastward when held; the portrait occasionally blinks |
| **Carved bone charm** | A small figure of a wolf, crudely carved, worn smooth | Rattles softly at night; the owner dreams of fog and a road through dark trees |
| **Old copper coin** | Stamped with a castle on one side, a sun on the other — neither design matches any known mint | Rolls toward the east if set on a flat surface; cannot be spent (merchants refuse it without knowing why) |
| **Child's music box** | Plays three notes of a waltz when wound — but it hasn't been wound in years, and it still plays | Plays on its own at dusk; the melody is somehow familiar |
| **Dried black rose** | Preserved perfectly despite being clearly decades old; faintly sweet smell | Found tucked into the character's belongings; they don't remember acquiring it; it reappears if thrown away |
| **Cracked hand mirror** | The reflection is always slightly delayed — half a second behind | Shows the owner standing in fog in the reflection, even in a bright room |
| **Iron key** | Ornate, old, opens nothing the character has ever tried | Grows uncomfortably hot when pointed west; cool and still when pointed east |
| **Letter with no signature** | Written in a flowing hand: *"Come to Barovia. You are needed. Do not delay."* No address, no sender | Has appeared in the character's pocket three times after being burned or buried |

---

## How to Use Them at the Table

**Before Session 1 (or at Session Zero):**
Tell each player privately: *"Your character has had this object for about two weeks. It appeared out of nowhere and you can't get rid of it. It's been tugging you east. You don't know why."*

Don't over-explain. Let them ask questions during the session.

**During the Daggerford opening:**
When the fog rolls in and the trinkets activate, describe each one reacting:
- The locket snaps open on its own
- The coin rolls off the table and points east
- The music box starts playing
- The rose's scent suddenly sharpens

**In the moment before the children appear:**
> *"Whatever you're carrying — it goes still. For the first time in weeks, the pull stops. You've arrived somewhere. You just don't know where yet."*

---

## After Barovia — What Happens to Them

Once the party crosses into Barovia, the trinkets stop pulling. They become ordinary objects — or as ordinary as they were before. The characters may choose to keep them as mementos or discard them freely.

**One optional use later in the campaign:** If a character holds their trinket during a significant emotional moment — confronting Strahd, meeting Sergei's ghost, standing at the final battle — you can describe it warming slightly. The Mists are paying attention. They always were.

---

## DM Notes

- Customize these to your specific players if you know their backstories before Session 1
- The trinkets are a hook device, not a mystery to be solved — don't let players spend session time trying to identify the magic. If they try: *"The magic feels old. Purposeful. It brought you here. That's all it was for."*
- If a player wants their trinket to have deeper personal significance as the campaign goes on, lean into it — this is good roleplay fodder
