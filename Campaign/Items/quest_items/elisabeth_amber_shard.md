# Elisabeth's Amber Shard (Death House)
**Category:** Quest item — cursed key / focus  

**Found:** Floor 2, Death House — master bedroom jewelry box  
**Carrier (current):** **Scott (Sorcerer)** — gifted by **Jake (Monk)** after Floor 2 (see *Custody* below)

---

## What It Is

A jagged splinter of **deep amber**, warm to the touch, set in a broken silver fitting (the rest of the pendant frame was torn or rotted away). In dim light it seems to hold a **slow pulse** — like a vein of honey lit from inside.

It is **not** (yet) a full Amber Temple vestige shard like those in `consumables/amber_shards.md`, but it is **kin** to that power: a fragment Strahd's cultists and sycophants sometimes carried — a **loan** of hunger from the same well that feeds the Dark Powers. Elisabeth wore it as **proof of her bargain** and as a **leash** on the thing the family called Walter.

---

## Backstory (for the table)

- **Elisabeth Durst** wore this at the height of the Death House cult. It keyed hidden doors and **named** the Flesh Mound — pairing the shard with the word **Walter** forced the abomination to obey a single command, as a master might jerk a chain.
- **Strahd** knew her for what she was. His letter in the library secret room calls her a **pathetic servant** and mocks her curse — the shard did not make her his equal; it made her **useful** and **contemptible**.
- The shard **remembers** Elisabeth: occasional whispers (Wisdom save DC 12 when you long rest with it under your pillow — fail: unsettling dream of a woman at a harpsichord, amber eyes). It does not speak clearly; it **pulls** toward niches in the house and toward the **basement**.
- **Same mystery as the party's trinkets:** Anyone who holds both their Barovian trinket and this shard feels a **harmonic buzz**, as if two tuning forks struck the same awful note. The house wanted those objects **here**.

---

## Mechanics (Death House)

1. **Library (Floor 3):** Fits the niche behind the moved bookshelf; opens the secret room (Strahd's letter, skeleton).
2. **Attic / secret stairs (Floor 4):** Fits the niche on the hidden door; opens the spiral stair to the basement.
3. **Flesh Mound:** Within 30 ft., holder can brandish the shard and speak the name **Walter** aloud — **DC 13 Charisma (Intimidation)**. On a success, the Mound obeys **one** command that does not directly harm it (e.g. stop, move aside, release a body). Failure may draw its full attention to the holder.

## Custody — Monk to Sorcerer

**Canon for this campaign:** After the nursemaid fight and the master bedroom revelations, **Jake** carried the shard because he found it and his trinket **resonated** with it. Holding it through the night left him with a **taste of copper** and **half-slept memories** that were not his — Elisabeth at the keys, a baby crying downstairs.

Before ascending to Floor 3, Jake pressed the shard into **Scott's** hands.

**Why (play these beats or paraphrase):**

- *"You work magic. This thing isn't ki — it's someone else's will, bottled. I can open a door with it, but **commanding** whatever's down there? That's **voice** and **nerve**. That's you."*
- Scott had already **asked Rose** about the amber; he had **ownership of the story** as much as the monk had ownership of the clue hunt.
- Jake wants **both hands** and a clear head for what's above; passing the burden is **trust**, not rejection.

**OOC:** The Mound interaction keys off **Charisma** — sorcerer is a natural fit. Scott may feel the shard **feeding** faintly on wild magic surges or spell slots at your table (flavor only unless you want mechanical creep).
