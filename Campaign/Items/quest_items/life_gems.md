# The Three Life-Gems (Weaver's Gifts)
**Category:** Quest Item (Enchanted Artifacts)  
**Location:** Wizard of Wines winery (two); Yester Hill (one — must be recovered, Arc J)  
**Guardians:** The Martikov family (Keepers of the Feather)  
**Required For:** Reconsecrating the Three Fanes (Month 4, Week 3)

---

## Description
Smooth, egg-sized stones — not quite any natural gemstone color. One is deep green, one is amber-gold, one is pale blue-silver. Each one is warm to the touch regardless of ambient temperature. They pulse very faintly in rhythm, like a slow heartbeat, when held. When all three are together, the rhythm synchronizes.

The Martikovs have kept them in the winery's earth for generations. Planted there, they accelerate and enhance the vineyard's growth in ways that no mundane soil amendment can replicate.

---

## Their True Purpose
The gems were created by the Weaver (one of the Ladies Three, goddess of spring, birth, and family) as anchors of her divine presence in the valley. They were placed at the winery because the Weaver's symbol is the spider — and wine, woven from grapes, vine, and time, is her craft.

To reconsecrate the Three Fanes, each gem must be planted at a specific site:

| Gem | Color | Fane | Location | Who Plants It |
|-----|-------|------|----------|--------------|
| Gem 1 | Deep green | Forest Fane | Yester Hill stone circle | Party (after recovering it from the druids, Arc J) |
| Gem 2 | Amber-gold | Swamp Fane | Berez (center of swamp) | Davian Martikov (his family's five-generation responsibility) |
| Gem 3 | Pale blue-silver | Mountain Fane | Foot of Mt. Ghakis | Lady Fiona Wachter (after Karl hostage crisis) |

---

## Gem 1 — Recovery (Arc J, Month 3, Week 1)
The druids stole Gem 1 and carried it to Yester Hill as the center of their Wintersplinter ritual. It is embedded in the wicker effigy's chest.

**Retrieving it:** DC 14 Athletics, 1d10 fire damage (Dex DC 12 for half), while the effigy is burning or collapsing.

After recovery: Davian Martikov plants it back in the vineyard to restore the winery's health in the short term. The party must retrieve it again for the Fane consecration in Act IV — or Davian travels with the party to Yester Hill himself.

---

## Planting the Gems (Month 4, Week 3)
Each gem's planting is a ritual moment, not a combat encounter. No saving throws, no skill checks — just the act of placing it in the earth at the right location.

**What happens when a gem is planted:**
> "The gem settles into the earth and does not stop — it sinks until it disappears, and from the point of entry, light spreads along old root-lines and stone-patterns. The Fane's sigil, wherever it is carved, illuminates. Something ancient exhales."

Each planting:
1. Weakens Strahd (removes one set of lair actions; see `NPCs/monsters/strahd_von_zarovich.md`)
2. Allows the Fane to function as sacred ground again (undead passing through it take 2d6 radiant damage per round)
3. Triggers Strahd's awareness — after the third gem, the Heart of Sorrow activates

---

## Winery Without the Gems
The Wizard of Wines vineyard will fail without its gems — slowly, over decades, not overnight. The current degradation from Gem 1's theft is visible: grey-green discoloration of the vines, reduced fruit set, an overall air of wrongness.

**After Gem 1's recovery:** Partial restoration — the winery is healthy but fragile.  
**After all three Fanes are consecrated:** The gems are consumed by the Fanes but their energy returns to the land as a whole. The vineyard recovers fully — a signal that the land itself is healing.

---

## Notes for DM
- Track which party member is carrying which gem between Acts
- The gems are not combat items — do not let the party accidentally use them as thrown weapons or spell components
- Their warmth and heartbeat quality should be noticeable — describe them occasionally in rest scenes so they feel present
- Davian Martikov's insistence on being the one to plant Gem 2 is non-negotiable. Five generations. It's his.
