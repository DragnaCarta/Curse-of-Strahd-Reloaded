# Argynvost's Skull
**Category:** Quest Item (Dragon Remains)  
**Location:** Castle Ravenloft — Hall of Bones (mounted as a trophy)  
**Required For:** Lighting the Beacon at Argynvostholt (Arc M/Q, Month 3)  
**Session:** Recovered Month 3, Week 4 (Ravenloft heist); returned Month 3, Week 4 (same session)

---

## Description
The skull of an adult silver dragon — the size of a carriage, bone-white, with eye sockets large enough to climb inside. It has been mounted on an iron stand in Castle Ravenloft's Hall of Bones for 300 years, displayed as a trophy. Even bare of flesh and flesh-magic, it radiates something. A quality. A presence. The echo of something noble that hasn't quite left.

Looking at it in the Hall of Bones is uncomfortable in a specific way — like seeing a cathedral used as a stable.

---

## Strahd's Hall of Bones
The Hall of Bones is a long gallery of trophies: humanoid skulls on shelves, weapons of the Order of the Silver Dragon displayed on one wall, and at the end — Argynvost's skull on its iron stand. Strahd has not entered this room in decades. He is not proud of it, exactly. He just hasn't taken it down.

**Guards:** 2 vampire spawn on patrol (standard patrol route — 12 rounds per circuit). Stealth DC 14 to bypass.

---

## The Logistical Problem
The skull weighs approximately **200 pounds**. Getting it out of the castle requires creative problem-solving.

**Options (encourage player creativity):**
| Method | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| *Reduce* spell | Reduces a target to 1/8 size (approx 25 lbs) — perfect if anyone has it prepared |
| *Leomund's Tiny Hut* / extradimensional storage | Bag of Holding won't work (a skull is a living creature's remains — it counts as organic matter) |
| Ezmerelda's wagon (parked outside) | Pre-position the wagon; lower the skull from a window with rope |
| Muriel Vinshaw (wereraven) | She can carry small objects in raven form; she can coordinate a relay of ravens for larger items as a pulley system |
| Improvise a stretcher/sled | Two characters, Strength DC 15 every 100 feet while carrying 200 lbs between two people |
| *Levitate* | Floats the skull at the caster's movement rate — quiet, elegant, works perfectly |

**DM note:** This puzzle is a fun logistics moment in an otherwise tense heist. Let the party solve it. Don't have a "right answer."

---

## Returning the Skull to Argynvostholt
The skull is placed in Argynvost's mausoleum — a chamber behind the manor's chapel. The mausoleum is simple stone, with a raised platform that the skull obviously belongs on. The socket marks where the iron stand pierced it for 300 years are visible. They fit the platform perfectly.

**The moment:**
> "In silence, the skull settles into its resting place. The revenants of the Order stand around the mausoleum in a circle. No one speaks. Sir Godfrey kneels. The others follow."

**Then:** the beacon (Act Q) can be lit.

---

## What Happens When the Skull Is Placed
Argynvost's ghost, which has been present but faint since his death, becomes stronger. He cannot speak — he never could without the gargoyle focus — but the warmth in the chapel increases noticeably. When the beacon lights, it is fueled by his will finally finding rest.

**The skull is not removed after this.** It stays in the mausoleum. It belongs there.

---

## Notes for DM
- The Hall of Bones scene works best as a quiet, tense infiltration — no combat if possible
- Describe the skull carefully when the party first sees it: give it the weight of something sacred desecrated
- The 200 lb logistics problem should be solved before entering the castle, not improvised at the last minute — reward parties who planned ahead with Ezmerelda's wagon or a *Reduce* spell slot
