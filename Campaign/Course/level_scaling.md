# Level Scaling Guide — Curse of Strahd: Reloaded
**Party starts at Level 1. Milestone leveling: 1–2 levels per session. 16 sessions total.**

> Use this file to know what level the party should be walking into each session, and how to adjust encounters accordingly. Encounters marked with ⚠️ need significant adjustment from the published stat blocks.

### Level-Up Triggers (When to Award 2 Levels)
Grant 2 levels when the party completes a major chapter milestone:
- Session 1: Killing the Flesh Mound and escaping Death House (Story + hard fight)
- Session 6: Van Richten's reveal AND Izek fight in the same session
- Session 9: Clearing the winery AND winning the Yester Hill druid confrontation
- Session 12: Successfully completing the full Ravenloft heist
- Session 16: Completing the final battle (award level 17 + bonus milestone XP)

## Encounter Scaling by Act

### ACT I — Sessions 1–4 (Levels 1–5)

#### ⚠️ SESSION 1: Death House (Level 1)
The original Death House is designed for Level 2 parties. For Level 1, reduce:

**Nursemaid's Specter (Death House, 3F):**
- Original: CR 1 (66 HP, 3d6 necrotic Life Drain)  
- **Adjusted:** 30 HP, Life Drain: 1d6 necrotic + Constitution DC 10 (instead of 10 Constitution DC 10 + 1d6)  
- Remove the "Max HP reduced" effect on first encounter; add it back once the party reaches the cult chambers

**Rose and Thorn (possession):**
- Possession save DC: reduce to 11 (from 13)
- Ghosts: 28 HP each (from 45 HP) — they are children, they shouldn't feel like CR 4 threats

**⚠️ Flesh Mound (Final Boss, Basement):**
- Original: Modified Shambling Mound (CR 5, 136 HP, Multiattack for 2d8+4 each)
- **Adjusted for Level 1 party of 4–5:** 75 HP; Slam: 1d8+3 (instead of 2d8+4); remove the Engulf action for the first round (let it telegraph); Absorbed creature takes 1d8 per round (from 2d8)
- Keep the "Faces of the Lost" and radiant vulnerability — this is the key mechanic, let them discover it
- Adjust upward if your group has 5+ players

**House Collapse:**
- Original: 3 rounds to escape, 4d6 bludgeoning per round
- **Adjusted:** 4 rounds to escape; 2d6 bludgeoning per round; one failed round doesn't immediately kill a Level 1 character

---

#### SESSION 2: Barovia Village (Level 2–3)
- Zombie mob at the church: Use 6 zombies (not the listed 9). Easy encounter is fine — this is narrative.
- Doru (vampire spawn): This is NOT a fight at this stage. If it becomes one, Doru flees at 40% HP. Do not kill him unless the party corners him.
- Wolf ambush on the road: 2 wolves + 1 dire wolf (reduced from 3 wolves + 2 dire wolves)

---

#### SESSION 3: Tser Pool / Madam Eva (Level 3–4)
- Vistani guards: These are NPCs, not fights. If combat is forced, they have 18 HP and a +3 attack.
- Strix (raven-like bat creatures): Use their standard stats (CR 1/8 × 8 = CR 1 swarm equivalent). Fine for Level 3.
- Strahd's appearance: No combat. He is observing. If the party attacks, he smiles and leaves.

---

#### ⚠️ SESSION 4: Tser Falls / Road to Vallaki (Level 4–5)
**Vampire spawn (bridge ambush):** 2 vampire spawn (CR 5 each) vs Level 4 party = Hard encounter.
- **Adjusted:** 1 vampire spawn + 2 wolves (the spawn commands them). Remove multiattack from the spawn for round 1 (they are ambushing, not fully engaged). This allows the party to feel the threat without a TPK risk.
- If Ireena is present: the spawn explicitly ignores her (Strahd's orders). This removes one targeting priority and creates dramatic tension.

---

### ACT II — Sessions 5–8 (Levels 5–9)

#### SESSION 5: St. Andral's / Arabelle (Level 5–6)
- Vampire spawn in the coffin-maker's shop: Use standard CR 5. Level 5 party handles 2 spawn well.
- ⚠️ Volenta Popofsky: Original stat block is CR 6 (two-phase). **For Level 5:** Phase 1 (55 HP, standard attacks). If she reaches Phase 2 (0 HP in Phase 1): she retreats. Save Phase 2 combat for her return in Act III.
- Franz and the possessed children: This is social/rescue, not combat. Franz has 12 HP and surrenders immediately.

---

#### ⚠️ SESSION 6: Van Richten / Izek / Festival (Level 6–7)
**Izek Strazni:** Original CR 6 (90 HP, devil's arm fireball attack).
- **At Level 6:** Use as written. This is a Hard encounter. It's meant to be the session's climax.
- Tactical adjustment: Izek prioritizes the character most threatening to him. He will NOT kill Ireena (he will try to grab her). This changes his action economy each round.

**Festival confrontation (Strahd's arrival):**
- No combat. Strahd arrives at the festival and regards the chaos with cold disappointment.
- One party member may attempt to engage him. He will speak, observe Ireena, and leave.

---

#### SESSION 7: Lady Wachter / Lost Soul (Level 7–8)
- Lady Wachter's cultists (CR 1/2 each × 6): Fine for Level 7. Use as written.
- Stella's rescue in the Ethereal: No combat. This is the ritual.
- Will-o'-wisps (Ethereal Plane): 2 will-o'-wisps (CR 2 each) as obstacles during the ritual. They target whoever disrupts their feeding.

---

#### ⚠️ SESSION 8: Night Hags / Krezk (Level 8–9)
**Morgantha's Coven (3 night hags):**
- Combined CR 11 (coven power). Level 8 party is capable but this is a VERY HARD encounter.
- **Recommended approach:** Split the coven (fight during midday when 1 hag is selling pastries = 2 hags vs party, CR 9). This is Hard but winnable.
- ⚠️ If all 3 fight together: they have access to *Nightmare Haunting* (targets sleeping creatures) and *Polymorph*. Have an escape route ready for the party (the windmill's upper floors are precarious; the hags won't pursue onto unstable footing). At 50% HP, Morgantha will offer a deal.

---

### ACT III — Sessions 9–12 (Levels 9–13)

#### SESSION 9: Winery / Yester Hill (Level 9–10)
- Druid shamans (CR 2 each × 6): Fine for Level 9. Use as written.
- ⚠️ Wintersplinter (Tree Blight, CR 5): At Level 9, this is easy. Upgrade to 120 HP (from 87); add a *Entangling Root* bonus action (restrained on DC 14 Strength save). Still not a major threat, but feels satisfying.
- The druid leader at Yester Hill: Use as a CR 4 druid (spellcaster). Give him *Wall of Thorns* as a prepared spell (3rd level).

---

#### ⚠️ SESSION 10: Abbey / Werewolf Den (Level 10–11)
**Kiril Stoyanovich (Werewolf Alpha):**
- Original CR 7 (custom). Level 10 party handles him without scaling.
- Add 3 veteran werewolves to his pack (CR 3 each with pack tactics) to make the fight more dynamic.
- **Wolf Den terrain:** The ravine is narrow — this is tactically significant. Ranged casters have clear shots; melee fighters must be deliberate about positioning (flanking applies).

**The Abbot (before redemption):**
- CR 10 Deva. Level 10 party can fight him — barely. **Do not make this a fight if possible.** The Icon of Ravenloft is the solution, not combat. If combat erupts: the Abbot pulls his punches the first round (he is genuinely confused about why the party is hostile). This gives the party one round to attempt the Icon solution.

---

#### SESSION 11: Argynvostholt / Dinner (Level 11–12)
- Revenants (CR 5 each): Use as written. 4 revenants in the manor are manageable at Level 11.
- ⚠️ Vladimir Horngaard (CR 15): He is NOT meant to be defeated in combat at this stage. If the party attacks him: he wins. Heavily. Use Vladimir as a scene of terrible, patient authority — he can be reasoned with over the course of the session. Combat with him is a waste of resources and a story dead-end.
- Strahd's dinner: No combat. This is pure roleplay. Strahd's vampire bride attendants have standard CR 13 stats but zero interest in fighting at the dinner table unless ordered.

---

#### ⚠️ SESSION 12: Ravenloft Heist (Level 12–13)
This session is a stealth/heist, not a fight. Encounters are meant to be bypassed, not won:

**Rahadin (CR 15):**
- At Level 12: The party cannot win this fight. Rahadin has 135 HP, his *Screams of the Dead* psychic aura (3d6 psychic per creature within 30 ft at round start), and Multiattack (3 attacks for 1d6+7 + 3d6 psychic each).
- If the party engages Rahadin: he is a "run away now" encounter. 2 rounds of fighting costs them 3-4 party member health bars. Use him to drive urgency, not to kill.
- If they stealth past him: reward excellent play.

**Vampire spawn guards (standard rotation):**
- 8 total in the castle during the heist. Standard CR 5.
- Patrol pattern: 12-round circuits, 2 per zone.
- A Level 12 party can fight 2 at once confidently. 4+ is very hard.

**The castle itself:**
- Treat the castle as a character. Strahd is aware the party is there after round 1 of any alarm being triggered. He does not immediately appear — he gives them rope to hang themselves. He arrives only when they have found something significant.

---

### ACT IV — Sessions 13–16 (Levels 13–17)

#### SESSION 13: Mountain Pass (Level 13–14)
- Mountain wolves (CR 1/2 each, 12 total as swarm): Treat as 2 dire wolves + 4 wolves. Easy.
- ⚠️ Roc of Mt. Ghakis: CR 11. Level 13 party can fight it — it's not a lethal encounter but it's memorable. It is uninterested in eating humanoids (too small) and attacks only if its nest is threatened. Circumventing it (climbing around the nest route) is viable with DC 14 Athletics.
- Amber Temple approach (ambush by surviving druids from Yester Hill): 4 druids + 1 shambling mound. CR 9 equivalent. Medium encounter.

---

#### ⚠️ SESSION 14: Amber Temple (Level 14–15)
**Amber Golems (CR 18 construct):**  
- Original stat blocks are CR 18 — deadly for Level 14.
- **Adjusted:** 1 golem (not 2) guarding the entrance hall. 210 HP (from 300). Reduce the slam damage to 3d10+8 (from 3d10+11). Remove Immutable Form for one fight only (Kasimir's knowledge of the temple's fail-safe allows a DC 14 Arcana check to shut the golem's power intake for one round).

**Exethanter (hostile if memory lost):** He has ghost stats (CR 4). Level 14 party destroys him in 2 rounds. Do not make him fight. Use the Tome of Strahd restoration mechanic first.

**Vampyr (spirit form, cannot physically fight):** The Vampyr's whisper in the Amber Chamber is NOT a combat encounter. It is a monologue. Let it breathe.

---

#### ⚠️ SESSION 15: Three Fanes / Baba Lysaga (Level 15–16)

**Baba Lysaga (CR 11 archmage witch):**
- Original CR 11. Level 15 party outclasses her significantly.
- **Adjusted for Level 15:** 200 HP (from 114); add two extra spell slots (she can cast *Power Word Stun* and *Time Stop* once each); her flying skull has 90 HP (from 60).
- Keep her at medium difficulty — the Fane consecrations are the session's climax, not Lysaga.

**Swamp Fane (Berez):** No combat — this is ritual. The swamp itself is hostile (poisonous mist, 1d4 poison per round outside the path), but the Fane site is clear. Davian Martikov plants the second gem.

**Mountain Fane (Mt. Ghakis):** No combat — ritual site. Lady Wachter plants the third gem, completing her arc. "If this frees one child from this valley, it is worth every sacrifice I refused to make before now."

---

#### ⚠️ SESSION 16: Final Battle (Level 16)
This is the campaign climax. Strahd needs to feel genuinely dangerous at Level 16.

**⚠️ SCALED STRAHD — Level 16 Version:**

| Stat | Original | Level 16 Adjusted |
|------|----------|------------------|
| HP | 144 | **220** |
| AC | 16 | 17 (vampiric speed) |
| Attack Bonus | +9 | +11 |
| Multiattack | 3 attacks | 3 attacks (Unarmed + Unarmed + Bite) |
| Bite damage | 3d6+4 + HP reduce | 4d6+6 + HP reduce (max HP reduction) |
| Unarmed damage | 2d8+4 | 2d8+7 |
| Save DCs | 17 | 18 |
| Legendary Resistances | 3/day | 3/day |
| Legendary Actions | 3/round | 3/round (add **Summon Spawn** as option: 1 action, 2 spawn appear) |
| Lair Actions | Standard | **Add:** *Mist Wall* (blocks LoS, 1d6 cold per round in 10-ft radius); *Static Shock* (all creatures in metal armor make DC 15 Dex or take 3d6 lightning) |

**Phase 2 (below 110 HP):** Strahd's Regeneration kicks in faster (at start of each turn, not just own turn). He uses *Misty Escape* immediately at 110 HP (first time).

**Phase 3 (below 50 HP):** Strahd drops Gentleman/Tyrant pretense entirely. He is desperate. Unarmed attacks gain the Knockdown property (DC 16 Strength or prone). He will attempt to reach his coffin.

**The Heart of Sorrow:** The red heart beating in the top of the castle's spire. 80 HP, AC 10, vulnerability to radiant. While it beats, Strahd regains 10 HP at the start of each round (separate from Regeneration). **Destroy it before engaging Strahd** if possible — or split the party to deal with it mid-fight.

## General Scaling Principles

**When encounters feel too easy:**
- Add environmental hazards (fog that limits darkvision, unstable floors, fire spreading)
- Add a secondary objective (protect an NPC, don't let the enemy reach the artifact)
- Add reinforcements on round 3 (rather than starting with a larger fight)

**When encounters feel too hard:**
- Give NPCs a reason to negotiate or flee at 50% HP
- Have a "distraction" arrive (Muriel brings news, a raven triggers a different threat)
- Allow clever tactics to bypass the fight entirely

**Never fudge dice openly.** But: you can always decide an enemy makes a suboptimal choice (the vampire spawn moves toward the door instead of attacking) without explaining it. The players don't need to know. The story is better for it.
