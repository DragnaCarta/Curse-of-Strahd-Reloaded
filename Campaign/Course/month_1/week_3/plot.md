# Month 1 · Week 3 — Arc C (Part 1): Into the Valley
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 2, morning → afternoon  
**Party Level:** 3 → 4 (award Level 4 after Madam Eva's full reading)  
**Location:** Old Svalich Road → River Ivlis Crossroads → Tser Pool  
**Session Goal:** Travel the road to Tser Pool, encounter Strahd at the crossroads, and receive Madam Eva's Tarokka reading.

---

## Opening — Departure from Barovia
> "The morning is grey and cold. No sun — just a diffuse white light filtering through clouds so thick they look like a ceiling. Ismark loads a small cart with Ireena's belongings. She's already dressed and waiting, her sword at her hip. She gives the village one last look — not sentimental, just memorizing it. 'Ready,' she says."

The road west out of the Village of Barovia. The fog hangs at 30-foot visibility. Ravens follow at a distance — always two or three of them, perching and then fluttering ahead.

**Optional Encounter — The Plague Graves:**
> "At the side of the road, a row of mounds. Simple wooden markers, names barely legible. A child's pinwheel spins in no wind."

A DC 14 Religion check identifies these as plague victims buried hastily — Barovian superstition says plague dead must be burned, not buried. These graves are wrong, and something is stirring in them. If players investigate: 4 **skeletons** claw their way out. After battle, Ismark says quietly: "That's why we burn the dead."

---

## Scene 1 — The River Ivlis Crossroads
> "The road splits. A signpost reads: north to Tser Pool, west to Vallaki. The fog seems thicker here. And then you hear it — hoofbeats. Four black horses pulling a black carriage emerge from the mist. The driver is skeletal, its eyes empty lanterns. The carriage stops. A door opens."

**Strahd von Zarovich — The Gentleman**

Take your time here. This is the first impression of the campaign's villain.

> "He steps out wearing black and deep burgundy — elegant, unhurried. He is pale as ivory, with dark hair and dark eyes that catch light like a hawk's. He looks at each of you in turn with what might be academic curiosity, and then he smiles. It doesn't reach his eyes. Nothing about him reaches his eyes."

**Standing beside him:** **Escher** — a young man of perhaps twenty, vampire-pale, beautiful, dressed in silk. He watches the party with anxious calculation.

**Strahd's Opening Line:**
> "Travelers. How unusual. I am Strahd von Zarovich. This is my land. You are my guests — and I do not receive many guests. I confess I am… curious. What brings outsiders to Barovia? And — " his gaze finds Ireena " — what a remarkable coincidence."

**Roleplay Guidance — The Gentleman Phase:**
- He is never threatening. He is polite to the point of menace.
- He asks questions. He *listens* to the answers. He finds the party interesting.
- He does not acknowledge Ireena as Tatyana's reincarnation openly — he speaks to her as if meeting an old acquaintance he's glad to see again.
- If a player threatens him: he tilts his head, pauses, and says "How refreshing. Spirit." Then ignores the threat entirely.
- He will not fight here. He will not harm Ireena here. His goal is to intrigue the party, not frighten them yet.

**Key Lines:**
> "I have ruled this land for four hundred years. The fog, the wolves, the cold — these are not my cruelty. They are simply the nature of this place. I keep order here. Without me, it would be worse. Much worse."

> *(to Ireena)* "My lady. I hope your journey has not been too frightening. You have, after all, seen the worst this valley has to offer, and yet here you stand."

> "I would invite you to dine with me at my castle — but I sense the timing is not quite right. Perhaps later. We shall meet again."

He withdraws to his carriage. The horses vanish into the fog without sound.

**Aftermath:** Ismark grips Ireena's arm. She is pale — shaken in a way she won't admit. She stares at the place where the carriage was. "He knows my name," she says. "I never told him my name."

## Scene 2 — Tser Pool
> "The north road leads to a clearing around a broad, still lake — Tser Pool. A camp of brightly painted wagons rings the water. Firelight. Music. The smell of cooking. And at the edge of the camp, an old woman sits alone in a large tent, looking directly at you as if she knew you were coming. Because she did."

### The Vistani Camp
Lively, colorful, suspicious of outsiders at first. The Vistani are one of the few groups free to travel in and out of Barovia's Mists.

**Arturi Radanavich** — a middle-aged Vistani man approaches the party. He's missing two fingers. He knows of Van Richten. If asked about the hunter: "He destroyed my clan. Not on purpose — but what's the difference to the dead?" (This is a story for later, relevant to Arc E.)

**Muriel Vinshaw** — the silver-winged raven that's been following the party lands near the camp and transforms into a young woman with dark eyes and quick hands. She's been watching the party since the Svalich Road. She introduces herself simply: "I'm Muriel. I'm… a friend of friends. The ravens watch this valley. You've done things worth watching."

## Scene 3 — Madam Eva's Tarokka Reading ⭐ PIVOTAL SCENE
> "The tent is simple but layered with meaning — dried herbs hanging from the ceiling, a brazier of coals, walls hung with woven tapestries of stars and wolves. Madam Eva is ancient. Her face is a map of decades, her eyes dark and calm as deep water. She gestures to the seats across from her. 'Sit. I have been expecting you. I have been expecting you since before you were born.'"

**Madam Eva — Roleplay Notes:**
- She is the avatar of the Seeker goddess. She does not guess. She **knows**.
- Speak slowly. Long pauses between lines. She looks through the players, not at them.
- She is not ominous — she is sorrowful. She has seen this play out before. She hopes this time is different.

**The Reading:**
> "The cards do not lie. They never lie. They simply show the truth before you are ready to hear it."

She draws five cards from her Tarokka deck. Each represents a location. Use the Tarokka draw results you recorded in Week 1's plan.md:

**Card 1 — The Tome of Strahd:**
> "This card tells of history — knowledge of the ancient. The master's secrets are hidden in *(location from your pre-determined draw)*."

**Card 2 — The Holy Symbol of Ravenkind:**
> "This card tells of a powerful force for good — its voice is heard in the chirp of birds. Seek it where *(location)*."

**Card 3 — The Sunsword:**
> "This is a card of power and strength. It tells of a weapon of sunlight — a blade that was broken, and waits to be whole again. It slumbers in *(location)*."

**Card 4 — The Ally:**
> "This card sheds light in the darkness. An ally of flesh and blood whose fate is entwined with yours. Look for this one *(location)*."

**Card 5 — Strahd's Enemy (The Sunsword Reading's Addendum):**
> She turns the final card and holds it for a long moment. "This… is the card that matters most. The one who will stand in the darkness's path. That is you. All of you. If you are willing."

**After the Reading:**
> "You will face the master of this land. You may not survive. Many have tried. But the cards show something different this time — a thread of hope so faint I almost missed it." She folds her hands. "The morning will not wait. Travel safely. Barovia has its eye on you now."

**Players May Ask Questions — Madam Eva's Answers:**
- *"Who is Strahd?"* → "A man who loved once, and lost, and made the worst bargain imaginable to have that love again. He has been paying ever since."
- *"Can he be killed?"* → "He can. But first you must understand him."
- *"What is the Grand Conjunction?"* → She goes very still. "You are not ready for that answer. Come back when you've read the book."
- *"Who are you?"* → "A very tired old woman. And something older than that."

## Closing Beat — Departing Tser Pool
The party camps at Tser Pool (or presses on toward Vallaki — encourage the camp; they need the rest). Ravens circle overhead and nest in the wagon wheels. As the party sleeps:

> "In your dreams, each of you sees the same image: a castle on a cliff, silhouetted against a violet sky. In one high window, a figure watches you. And even in the dream — even across that impossible distance — you feel his gaze."

**Dream foreshadowing of Castle Ravenloft. No mechanical effect. Pure atmosphere.**
