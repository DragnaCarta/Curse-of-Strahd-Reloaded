# Month 1 · Week 1 — Arc A: Escape From Death House
**In-Game Date:** Highharvesttide Eve  
**Party Level:** 1 → 2 → 3 (Level 2 at Floor 2, Level 3 upon escaping Death House)  
**Location:** Daggerford → Svalich Woods border → Death House  
**Session Goal:** Lure players into Death House, survive floors 1–5, defeat the Flesh Mound in the basement, escape before collapse.

---

## Scene 1 — The Hook (Daggerford)
> *Read aloud or paraphrase:*
>
> "The city of Daggerford hums with the sounds of Highharvesttide (Forgotten Realms wiki harvest festival) — laughter, music, the smell of roasting meats. But as the evening wears on, a strange fog begins to roll in from the east, thicker than any ocean mist you've seen. Lanterns flicker. Dogs refuse to bark. And then — a child's voice cuts through the revelry."

Each player character independently receives a different enchanted trinket (a locket, a carved bone charm, a tarnished coin) that begins to tug them eastward. The items cannot be discarded — they vanish and reappear in a pocket. Run this as individual hooks if desired, or narrate a shared tavern scene at **The Nightmare's Bridle**.

**Read Aloud — The Children:**
> "Through the fog, two small figures take shape — a girl, maybe eight, and a younger boy pressed close behind her. Their clothes are fine but soaked through. The girl's eyes find yours immediately — not the wide, darting eyes of a frightened child, but steady. Too steady. Like she has been waiting a very long time and has learned not to show how long."
>
> *She speaks quietly, each word deliberate:*
>
> "We live in that house. We've been waiting for someone to come down this road for — a long time. There is something in our basement. It has been there for as long as I can remember. Our mother and father went down to deal with it. They didn't come back up."
>
> *A pause. She doesn't look away.*
>
> "We can hear it at night. Moving. We can't leave. We've tried. The fog just — brings us back. You're the first people who have ever come."
>
> *The boy says nothing. He is looking at his feet. Then, without looking up:*
>
> "We're very tired."

**If the party hesitates or asks questions — Rose's follow-up lines (use one at a time):**

- *"I know how it sounds. I know. But please look at us. We've been standing in this rain. Where else would we go?"*
- *"I'm not asking you to fight anything. Just — come inside. It's warm. See for yourself. If you don't believe me after that, you can leave."* (She knows they can't. She doesn't say so.)
- *"My brother hasn't slept properly in a very long time. Neither have I."* (Said quietly, to herself almost, not as manipulation — which makes it worse.)
- *(If someone crouches down to her level)* She meets their eyes and says simply: *"Please. You're the first people who have ever come."* Then she takes their hand. Gently. Waiting.

**If the party still won't move — Thorn speaks (use only once, maximum impact):**
> *He looks up from the ground for the first time. His eyes are very calm.*
> *"The house won't let us leave. But it will let you in."*
> *He looks back down. He says it the same way you'd say the sky is grey — just information.*

---

- **Rose Durst** (girl): Composed, precise, disturbingly patient. Speaks like someone who has rehearsed this conversation in their head for a very long time. Does not beg — she *reasons*. That is more unsettling than begging.
- **Thorn Durst** (boy): Says almost nothing. When he does speak, it is one sentence, completely flat, and completely true. He is the one that will stay with players after the session ends.

> *DM Note: Rose and Thorn are already dead — they are ghosts bound to this location. They do not know they are dead. Do not reveal this yet. If players cast Detect Magic, the children appear as faint illusions of conjuration magic.*

The fog thickens. The road east is the only way forward.

---

## Scene 2 — Arrival at Death House (16 Durst Lane)
> "The fog closes behind you as if a curtain has been drawn. A house looms ahead — three stories of Gothic stonework, iron-latticed windows, and a door carved with two serpents devouring each other. A plaque reads: 16 DURST. The windows on the upper floor glow faintly. The street around it is silent."

**Key House Points:**
- **Floor 1:** Foyer, dining room, kitchen, den with suspicious taxidermied wolves (they animate if disturbed). Hidden room behind the bookcase contains the **Dungeon Master's note** — a letter from Gustav Durst mentioning "the nursemaid's suite."
- **Floor 2:** Master bedroom (ghostly visions of the Dursts arguing), the nursemaid's room (a **dark mimic** of the nursemaid attacks if her crib is disturbed — she clutches a baby that is actually a doll).

**The Nursemaid (Dark Mimic — NOT the real Klara):**
This creature looks like a ghoul but is not a reanimated corpse. It is a **dark construct** — the house's magic shaped from Klara's suffering, forced to guard the nursery for eternity. Elisabeth's final punishment: *you cared for children in life, now you'll guard an empty crib forever.*

When killed, the mimic does **not** leave a body. It dissolves — skin turns to black ichor, bones crumble to powder, the whole form collapses into a dark stain on the floorboards that seeps away in seconds. Only the locket remains, lying in a wet smear on the wood. **This is a clue:** what they killed was never a real person. The real Klara is somewhere else in this house.

> **MILESTONE — Level 2:** Award Level 2 after the party completes Floor 2 (nursemaid defeated or master bedroom vision witnessed). Pause briefly: *"You feel something shift — the house is testing you, and you are learning its language. Take your Level 2 now."* Let players update HP before they go deeper. They'll need it.
### Floor 3 — Read Aloud When They Start Climbing

> *"The stairs narrow as you climb. The wallpaper is gone — bare plaster, cracked, stained with something dark that could be water or could be blood. The air smells different up here. Copper. Dust. Something faintly chemical, like old medicine.*
>
> *You reach the third-floor landing. It opens into a wide balcony that overlooks the stairwell — you can see straight down to the ground floor, three stories below. The railing is wrought iron, rusted, with deep scratch marks along the top bar.*
>
> *Against the far wall stands a suit of black plate armor on a wooden stand, draped in cobwebs. Its visor is down. Its gauntlets rest at its sides. Beside it, a closed door. To your left, an open archway leads to what looks like a library. To your right, another door — and from behind it, you can hear something. Faint. A harpsichord, playing by itself. One note. Over and over. The same note."*

**Let them take it in.** They see: the armor, the stairwell drop, the library, and the door with the harpsichord. Multiple things pulling their attention. The armor is in plain sight — just one detail among several. That's the trap. It looks like set dressing until it isn't.

**Animated Armor Ambush:**
- The armor does NOT activate for the first person up the stairs. Or the second. **It waits.** It lets the heavy armor pass. It lets the confident ones through. When someone in light armor or robes steps onto the balcony — leather, cloth, no metal — it strikes. The house is choosing its victim.
- The armor makes a **shove attack:** automatic hit, no roll. The target is hurled over the railing.
- **The fall is three stories down the open stairwell to stone and rusted iron spikes at the bottom** (old cult defenses). **3d6 bludgeoning + 2d10 piercing.** Average: 21.5 damage. Max: 38. **If this damage reduces the character to 0 HP, they are dead. No death saves. Instant kill.**
- **Clue (if anyone checks first):** DC 14 Perception on the armor reveals the gauntlets are positioned like they're about to push something. Scratch marks run along the balcony railing. A dark stain on the stone floor far below. If nobody checks — that's on them.
- If the target somehow survives: they are prone, impaled, and alone at the bottom of the stairwell. The armor leaps down after them (no fall damage — it's metal) and attacks. Multiattack: two slams, +4 to hit, 1d6+2 each. Attacks against prone targets have advantage.

**Animated Armor Stats:** AC 18, HP 33, Speed 25 ft. Immune to poison, psychic. Blind but has blindsight 60 ft.

> *DM Note: This trap is designed to guarantee a kill. It waits for a squishy target, the shove auto-hits, and 3d6+2d10 averages 21.5 against characters with 12-18 HP. Someone is dying on this floor. When it happens: describe the fall in slow motion. The weightlessness. The spikes rushing up. The sound of impact. Then silence. The party is standing three stories above, looking down. Let them see the body.*

### When a Character Dies — Handling It At the Table

**Step 1:** Describe the death. Don't rush it. Let the table sit in silence for a beat.

**Step 2:** Tell the player privately: *"You're dead. But you have choices."*

**Step 3:** Offer three options:

**A) Spirit.** Keep playing the same character as a ghost. Can talk, scout through walls, distract enemies (DC 12 WIS or disadvantage), possess willing allies once per short rest. Cannot attack, cannot hold weapons, cannot cast most spells. Levels up with the party. Can be returned to life later in the campaign (Month 3-4). Full rules: `Campaign/Course/templates/spirit_character.md`

**B) Dark Gift.** The Dark Powers whisper in the void: *"We can give you back. Right now. But it will cost you something."* If accepted: the character wakes in their body with 1 HP. They're alive. But something is wrong — a hidden curse that reveals itself over time:
- Eyes turn amber. Animals flee from them.
- They heal when creatures die near them. They start craving it.
- A stat drops by 1 every two sessions as something eats their humanity.
- Sunlight burns faintly. Darkness feels like home.

The player doesn't choose the curse. The DM does. It reveals itself slowly. This is the fast resurrection — but the cost escalates through the campaign.

**C) New Character.** Fresh character, enters within 15 minutes at full party level. The dead character stays dead.

**Bonus option — Rose and Thorn's Sacrifice (Death House only):**
If the party buries Rose and Thorn's bones before the house collapses, and a spirit is present, Rose can offer:
> *"I can feel you. You're like us now. But you shouldn't be. We've been holding on for three hundred years. We're tired. We can give you what's left of us."*
Rose and Thorn sacrifice their remaining spiritual energy to pull the soul back into the body. The spirit wakes with 1 HP. No curse. But Rose and Thorn are gone — forever. Peacefully. Save this for a player who is genuinely not enjoying being a ghost.

### Floor 3 — Library (TRAPPED)

Klara's resignation letter on the desk (revealing her pregnancy). Secret door behind the bookshelf — requires the **amber shard** placed in a niche to open.

**The Bookshelf Trap:** When someone pulls the switch to move the bookshelf, a **poison dart volley** fires from hidden holes in the wall behind it. Every creature within 10 ft. of the bookshelf: **DC 13 DEX save or take 2d6 piercing + 1d6 poison damage.** On a failed save, the target is also **poisoned for 1 hour** (disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks). A character reduced to 0 HP by this trap is dead — the darts are coated in concentrated serpent venom.

Behind the bookshelf: the **Secret Room** with a skeleton in a chest and **Strahd's letter to Elisabeth** — the party's first encounter with the name Strahd von Zarovich.

### Floor 3 — Conservatory (KILL ZONE)

A harpsichord with sheet music titled *Waltz for Klara*. If played, ghostly dancers appear — Gustav and Klara dancing together while Elisabeth watches. She grabs the pendant, her eyes flash amber, and the dancers vanish.

**Then Elisabeth turns on the player at the harpsichord.**

> *"The apparition doesn't vanish with the others. She turns. She looks at you — at YOUR hands on her harpsichord. Her mouth opens wider than it should. The temperature drops to freezing. Frost spreads across the keys under your fingers."*

**Elisabeth's Wrath (one-time event):** The player at the harpsichord must make a **DC 14 Wisdom save.**
- **Fail:** 3d8 psychic damage and **frightened for 1 minute** (can't willingly move toward the conservatory). If this drops them to 0 HP: their heart stops. Dead.
- **Pass:** Half damage, no frightened condition.

The bookshelf in the Library scrapes open (the secret door is now accessible).

### Floor 4 (Attic) — Read Aloud When They Arrive

> *"The attic stairs creak with every step. The wood up here is different — warped, swollen with moisture, dark with mold. The ceiling sags. Something skitters inside the walls — not one thing, many things. Small. Fast. The floorboards bow underfoot. This part of the house was never meant for guests."*

**Unstable Floors:** The attic floorboards are rotted. Any character in **heavy armor** or who **runs** (moves more than half speed) must make a **DC 12 DEX save** or crash through the floor, falling 10 ft. to Floor 3 below. **1d6 bludgeoning damage**, and they have to climb back up (or the party has to help them). If the Animated Armor is still active on Floor 3, it attacks them while they're prone.

### Floor 4 — Children's Room

Rose & Thorn's true resting place — their skeletons are in the toy chest.

**The Toy Chest — Centipede Swarm:**
When the chest is opened, hundreds of **black centipedes** pour out from under the stuffed animals, swarming across the opener's hands and arms before the skeletons are even visible.

> *"You lift the lid. For a second you see stuffed animals, wooden blocks — and then the centipedes. Hundreds of them. They pour over your hands, up your arms, across your face. They're in your hair. They're under your armor. They bite."*

**Swarm of Centipedes:** +3 to hit, **4d4 piercing damage**, and DC 11 CON save or **poisoned for 1 minute** (disadvantage on attacks and ability checks). The swarm has AC 12, HP 22, resistance to bludgeoning/piercing/slashing. Occupies the same space as the opener. Takes half damage from single-target attacks; vulnerable to fire and area effects.

Once the swarm is dealt with — **then** they see the skeletons underneath.

> *"Beneath the dead centipedes and scattered toys — two small skeletons. A girl and a younger boy. Curled together. The girl's arm is around the boy. They're wearing the same clothes as Rose and Thorn."*

Rose realizes they are dead. She flickers. She says: *"That's us. We're in the chest. We're — we're dead."* Thorn looks at his feet. He already knew.

**Rose Possession (after the reveal):**
> *"The little girl's form flickers. 'You found us,' she whispers. 'We've been waiting so long.' Her hand reaches toward [character name]'s chest — not threatening, but yearning. 'Can I stay? Just for a little while? I'm so cold.'"*

If a player allows possession: Rose uses the body to explore the house, is frightened but helpful — she knows the basement route. She leaves willingly when asked, or when the Flesh Mound fight begins: *"I don't want to see it again."* If refused: she weeps but respects it.

### Floor 4 — Spare Bedroom

A music box that plays by itself, then goes off-key and screeches to a halt. Contains a rusted skinning knife and a crude **basement map** (three rectangles: QUARTERS, SHRINE, ALTAR).

**More skittering in the walls.** If anyone puts their ear to the wall: the centipedes are everywhere up here. The house is infested. This floor is *wrong*.

### Floor 4 — Storage Room (HOSTILE)

The trunk is against the back wall, half-hidden under a moth-eaten curtain.

> *"You open the trunk. Inside — a woman's body. Real. Not a ghost. Skin tight over bone. Wrists scarred. Fingers removed. Eyes sewn shut. Lips and teeth torn out. She starved to death in this trunk.*
>
> *This is Miss Klara. The real Miss Klara.*
>
> *The thing you killed on Floor 2 — it dissolved when it died. It wasn't a body. It was never her. That was a dark echo, a punishment the house created from her pain. This is what Elisabeth actually did to her. This is where she actually died."*

Let that land. The party thought they'd already dealt with Klara. They hadn't. They'd killed a shadow puppet. The real woman was locked in a trunk three floors above the whole time — tortured, mutilated, left to starve. Three hundred years, alone in the dark.

**The moment anyone touches the corpse:** Klara's spirit erupts from the body as a **specter**. Her soul has been bound to this trunk — to the place of her actual death — since Elisabeth killed her.
- **AC** 12, **HP** 22, Speed 0 ft. / 50 ft. fly (hover)
- **Life Drain:** +4 to hit, 3d6 necrotic damage. Target's max HP is reduced by the damage taken until a long rest. **If max HP is reduced to 0, the target dies instantly and their body withers.**
- She attacks whoever touched her corpse, then the nearest living creature. She is not Klara anymore — she is three hundred years of agony given form.
- **Klara does not stay in the storage room.** She pursues. She follows the party through the attic. She phases through walls and floors. She only stops if destroyed or if the party reaches the basement stairs — the cult's magic repels her at the threshold.

> *DM Note: Floor 4 is hostile territory. Rotting floors, centipede swarm, Klara's corpse reveal, and a specter that chases them. By the time they reach the basement, they should be bleeding, terrified, and down at least one member. That's the point. This is Death House.*

### How They Get to the Basement — The Path Down

There is no obvious staircase to the basement. The house hides it. The party has to earn the path through exploration:

1. **The Library (Floor 3)** has a bookshelf with a niche behind it. The **amber shard** (which the Monk picked up on Floor 2) fits the niche like a key. When held near, it pulls toward the slot like a magnet. Place it in — the bookshelf grinds open, revealing a secret room with Strahd's letter and a skeleton.
2. **The Spare Bedroom (Floor 4)** has a music box containing a crude **basement map** (three rectangles: QUARTERS, SHRINE, ALTAR). This tells them the basement exists but not how to reach it.
3. **The Storage Room (Floor 4)** — behind the trunk where Klara's corpse is hidden — has a **hidden door in the back wall**. DC 14 Investigation to find it, or Rose will point it out if she's possessing someone: *"That's the way down. That's where they did the bad things."*

The hidden door opens to a narrow spiral staircase that descends three full floors into absolute darkness.

> *"The stairs spiral down into darkness. One floor. Two. Three. The walls close in. The air turns cold and wet. Cobwebs cling to your face. After what feels like an eternity, you reach packed earth. A narrow tunnel stretches ahead. And from somewhere deep in the dark — chanting. Low. Rhythmic. It doesn't stop."*

> *DM Note: If the party can't find the stairs, they'll wander. That's fine. Let them feel trapped. If they're truly stuck for more than 10 minutes of real-world time, have the house help — a cold draft from behind a wall, a child's handprint appearing on the plaster near the hidden door, or Rose whispering "down" from somewhere they can't see. The house WANTS them in the basement. It's been starving for three hundred years.*

## Scene 3 — The Basement & the Cult Shrine
> "A stone staircase descends below the house. The air turns cold and wet. Torchlight reveals a worked stone corridor. On the walls, crude paintings depict hooded figures surrounding a dark mass. Chanting seems to echo — but when you stop, so does it."

**Key Basement Areas:**
- **The Well:** A 40-foot shaft. A swarm of rats nests inside.
- **The Ghoul Pits:** Chained ghouls — former cult members. They still wear rotted Durst family livery.
- **The Cult Shrine:** A blood-stained altar before a 20-foot statue of a dark, featureless figure. The altar reads: *"He who serves may rest. He who feeds shall rise."*
- **The Ritual Chamber:** The outer ring. Thirteen cult-member shadows rise from the floor — they do not attack unless the party attempts to leave before completing the ritual. They chant: *"One must die. One must feed the master."*

## Scene 4 — The Flesh Mound (Boss Encounter)
> "At the center of the lowest chamber floats a mass of flesh the size of a cart horse — limbs, faces, and torsos fused into a single horrific whole. Eyes open across its surface and fix upon you. A sound rises: not a roar, but a chorus of moaning, as if every person ever consumed by this creature is still trapped inside, still screaming."

### The Cult's Demand

The party enters the ritual chamber. Thirteen shadow-cultists rise from the stone floor in a circle around the altar. They do not attack. They chant.

> *"One must die. One must die. One must die."*

The Flesh Mound writhes behind the altar. It hasn't attacked yet. It's waiting.

> *"The shadows aren't looking at the creature. They're looking at the altar. It's a stone slab, chest-height, stained black with centuries of dried blood. The chanting grows louder. Louder. The shadows' hands are pointing — at the altar. At you."*

**Give the party a moment.** Let them talk. Let them argue. Let the spirits of their dead friends watch. The Mound pulses. The shadows chant. Nobody is attacking — yet.

### Resolution Path A — The Sacrifice (Expected)

A character willingly lays on the altar. Read aloud:

> *"[Character name] steps forward. The chanting stops — instantly, like a breath held. The shadows part. [Character] climbs onto the altar. The stone is ice cold. The Flesh Mound surges forward — not violently but hungrily, reverently — and wraps itself around them. There is no scream. Just silence. And then the Mound settles. The shadows bow their heads. One by one, they sink into the floor and are gone. The torches flicker. The chanting is over. It's just you now. You and the silence. And from somewhere above — a crack. The ceiling shifts. Dust rains down. The house is dying."*

The sacrificed character is **dead**. No save. No extraction. The Mound heals to full and goes dormant. The cult is satisfied. The house begins to collapse (Scene 5).

> *DM Note: This is the intended resolution. By this point the party should be 2-3 living characters and 2-3 spirits. They are beaten, terrified, and outnumbered by shadows. The sacrifice should feel like a mercy — not a punishment. The player who volunteers is the bravest person at the table. Honor that. Describe it slowly. Let the table grieve. Then: "The house is collapsing. Run."*

### Resolution Path B — They Fight (3 vs. the Mound)

If nobody volunteers and they attack the Mound, the shadows join the fight.

**Adjusted Flesh Mound (Depleted — Scaled for 2-3 Characters):**
The Mound has been starving for decades. It is weaker than it would be at full strength.
- **HP: 68** (half normal — it hasn't been fed in years)
- **AC:** 15 | **Speed:** 20 ft.
- **Slam:** +7 to hit, 2d8+4 bludgeoning. Two per round.
- **Engulf:** If both slams hit the same target — DC 14 STR/DEX or grappled + restrained. Takes 2d8+4 bludgeoning + 2d8 acid at start of Mound's turn.
- **Absorb (Reaction):** Creature drops to 0 HP within 5 ft. → pulled inside. Heals 15 HP. Death saves continue. DC 17 STR to extract.
- **Wail of the Consumed (Recharge 5–6):** 20 ft. AoE, DC 13 CON, 3d6 necrotic (half on save), reduces max HP on fail.
- **Vulnerability:** Radiant (+5 extra per hit) — the Paladin's Divine Smite is the key.
- **Resistance:** Nonmagical bludgeoning, piercing, slashing (Monk's fists are halved)

**The Shadow Cultists (if the party fights):** 4 shadows join on Round 2. AC 12, HP 16 each, +4 to hit, 2d6+2 necrotic. STR drain on hit. They flank and target whoever is lowest HP.

**What the Spirits Can Do:**
Spirits cannot attack, but they CAN help:
- **Distract (Action):** Choose one enemy. That enemy has **disadvantage on its next attack.** DC 12 WIS or the distraction works.
- **Possess a willing ally (1/short rest):** The spirit inhabits a living character, granting **+2 to one ability check or saving throw** and **advantage on death saves** for 1 minute. The spirit is ejected after.
- **Scout:** Spirits can see through walls. They can warn the party about flanking shadows or tell them where the Mound is weakest.

> *DM Note: At 68 HP with radiant vulnerability, the Paladin can burn both smites for ~36 radiant damage in 2 rounds. The Monk's fists are halved but Flurry adds volume. Third character contributes what they can. Math: roughly 15 effective DPR = ~5 rounds to kill it. But the Mound does 26 DPR and the shadows add another 20. Someone else is dying in this fight. Probably two of them. This could end with 1 survivor and 4 ghosts stumbling out of a collapsing house. That is a perfectly valid outcome for Death House.*

### Resolution Path C — They Flee

If they try to run without fighting or sacrificing:
- The shadows pursue for 2 rooms, then stop
- The Mound does not follow — it cannot leave the chamber
- The house collapses at **10 minutes per floor** (slower than the combat collapse)
- They still have to get through the sealed front door (DC 15 STR or the key from the master bedroom)
- The cult is NOT satisfied — this has consequences later in the campaign. The house's hunger follows them into Barovia.

## Scene 5 — Escape from Death House
> "The house shudders. A crack splits the ceiling. Dust and plaster rain down. Rose's voice echoes from everywhere: 'Run. Please. RUN.'"

**The Collapse (Chase Encounter):**
- Each round, the party must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw or take 3 (1d6) bludgeoning damage from falling debris.
- Every 2 failed saves, a section of floor collapses, dropping the party 10 feet (1d6 fall damage) to the floor below.
- At the front door: it is magically sealed. A character must use their action and succeed on DC 15 Strength check to force it — or find the **key** in the master bedroom (it has been there all along, hanging on a hook by the fireplace).

**Escape Read Aloud:**
> "You burst through the front door and tumble onto wet cobblestones. Behind you, 16 Durst Lane collapses with a sound like a great exhalation — as if the house itself has finally died. When the dust settles, the fog still surrounds you. The children are nowhere to be seen. Ahead, through the grey murk, a road leads onward — and faintly, you hear the sound of wolves."

## Closing Beat — The Mists of Barovia
> "You are no longer in Daggerford. The road is different — the cobblestones are older, cracked, overgrown with black moss. The trees on either side are twisted, leafless. Fog hangs at the treeline like a curtain. A crow lands on a nearby branch and tilts its head at you. Then — in the distance — a wolf howls. Answered by another. And another."

> **MILESTONE — Level 3:** Award Level 3 the moment the party escapes the house. They earned it in blood. *"The house is gone. You are not who you were when you walked in. Take your Level 3."* Any characters who died and returned as spirits or new characters also receive Level 3.
