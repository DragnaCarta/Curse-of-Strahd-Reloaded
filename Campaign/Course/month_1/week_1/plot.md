# Month 1 · Week 1 — Arc A: Escape From Death House
**In-Game Date:** Highharvesttide Eve  
**Party Level:** 1 → 2 (award Level 2 after escaping Death House)  
**Location:** Daggerford → Svalich Woods border → Death House  
**Session Goal:** Lure players into Death House, survive floors 1–5, defeat the Flesh Mound in the basement, escape before collapse.

---

## Scene 1 — The Hook (Daggerford)
> *Read aloud or paraphrase:*
>
> "The city of Daggerford hums with the sounds of Highharvesttide — laughter, music, the smell of roasting meats. But as the evening wears on, a strange fog begins to roll in from the east, thicker than any ocean mist you've seen. Lanterns flicker. Dogs refuse to bark. And then — a child's voice cuts through the revelry."

Each player character independently receives a different enchanted trinket (a locket, a carved bone charm, a tarnished coin) that begins to tug them eastward. The items cannot be discarded — they vanish and reappear in a pocket. Run this as individual hooks if desired, or narrate a shared tavern scene at **The Nightmare's Bridle**.

**Read Aloud — The Children:**
> "Through the fog, two small figures appear: a boy, perhaps ten, and a girl a few years younger. The boy clutches the girl's hand tightly. 'Please,' he says, his voice barely audible. 'There's a monster in our house. Our parents are asleep. We can't wake them.' His eyes are wide — not with the fear of a child, but with something far older."

- **Rose Durst** (girl): Sweet, precise, holds her brother's hand. Speaks in a small voice about "the monster in the basement."
- **Thorn Durst** (boy): Quieter. Sometimes looks at his feet instead of the party.

> *DM Note: Rose and Thorn are already dead — they are ghosts bound to this location. They do not know they are dead. Do not reveal this yet. If players cast Detect Magic, the children appear as faint illusions of conjuration magic.*

The fog thickens. The road east is the only way forward.

---

## Scene 2 — Arrival at Death House (16 Durst Lane)
> "The fog closes behind you as if a curtain has been drawn. A house looms ahead — three stories of Gothic stonework, iron-latticed windows, and a door carved with two serpents devouring each other. A plaque reads: 16 DURST. The windows on the upper floor glow faintly. The street around it is silent."

**Key House Points:**
- **Floor 1:** Foyer, dining room, kitchen, den with suspicious taxidermied wolves (they animate if disturbed). Hidden room behind the bookcase contains the **Dungeon Master's note** — a letter from Gustav Durst mentioning "the nursemaid's suite."
- **Floor 2:** Master bedroom (ghostly visions of the Dursts arguing), the nursemaid's room (a ghoulified nursemaid attacks if her crib is disturbed — she clutches a baby that is actually a doll).
- **Floor 3:** The children's room (Rose & Thorn's true resting place — their skeletons are in the toy chest). Library. Walter's secret room.
- **Floor 4 (Attic):** Servants' quarters, storage. The **Rose and Thorn encounter**: if players find the skeletons, the children wail. Rose attempts to possess a player. Thorn flees.

**Rose Possession Roleplay:**
> "The little girl's form flickers. 'You found us,' she whispers. 'We've been waiting so long.' Her hand reaches toward [character name]'s chest — not threatening, but yearning. 'Can I stay? Just for a little while? I'm so cold.'"

If a player allows possession: Rose uses the body to explore the house, is frightened but helpful — she knows the basement route. She leaves willingly when asked. If refused: she weeps but respects it.

---

## Scene 3 — The Basement & the Cult Shrine
> "A stone staircase descends below the house. The air turns cold and wet. Torchlight reveals a worked stone corridor. On the walls, crude paintings depict hooded figures surrounding a dark mass. Chanting seems to echo — but when you stop, so does it."

**Key Basement Areas:**
- **The Well:** A 40-foot shaft. A swarm of rats nests inside.
- **The Ghoul Pits:** Chained ghouls — former cult members. They still wear rotted Durst family livery.
- **The Cult Shrine:** A blood-stained altar before a 20-foot statue of a dark, featureless figure. The altar reads: *"He who serves may rest. He who feeds shall rise."*
- **The Ritual Chamber:** The outer ring. Thirteen cult-member shadows rise from the floor — they do not attack unless the party attempts to leave before completing the ritual. They chant: *"One must die. One must feed the master."*

---

## Scene 4 — The Flesh Mound (Boss Encounter)
> "At the center of the lowest chamber floats a mass of flesh the size of a cart horse — limbs, faces, and torsos fused into a single horrific whole. Eyes open across its surface and fix upon you. A sound rises: not a roar, but a chorus of moaning, as if every person ever consumed by this creature is still trapped inside, still screaming."

**Flesh Mound Stats:** Use the **Shambling Mound** statblock with the following modifications:
- **Absorb (Reaction):** When a creature within 5 ft is reduced to 0 hp, the Mound absorbs the body — healing 10 hp.
- **Faces of the Lost:** Once per turn as a bonus action, one face of the Mound speaks — screaming the name of a missing Barovian villager. Players who have met that NPC must make DC 12 Wisdom save or be frightened until end of next turn.
- **Vulnerability:** Radiant damage (5 extra damage per hit).

**Resolution Options:**
1. **Defeat the Flesh Mound** → The house begins to collapse. Skip to Scene 5.
2. **Sacrifice a party member** → The Mound is satisfied; the cult shadows release the party. But the house still begins to groan and settle — escape within 1 hour.
3. **Flee without killing it** → The shadows pursue up to 2 rooms. The house begins collapsing at a slower pace (10 minutes per floor).

> *DM Note: No player should die here permanently. If a character falls to 0 hp and fails death saves, the Mound absorbs them — their consciousness is trapped but extractable if someone reaches inside (DC 15 Strength, 1 action) before the Mound seals the body in.*

---

## Scene 5 — Escape from Death House
> "The house shudders. A crack splits the ceiling. Dust and plaster rain down. Rose's voice echoes from everywhere: 'Run. Please. RUN.'"

**The Collapse (Chase Encounter):**
- Each round, the party must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw or take 3 (1d6) bludgeoning damage from falling debris.
- Every 2 failed saves, a section of floor collapses, dropping the party 10 feet (1d6 fall damage) to the floor below.
- At the front door: it is magically sealed. A character must use their action and succeed on DC 15 Strength check to force it — or find the **key** in the master bedroom (it has been there all along, hanging on a hook by the fireplace).

**Escape Read Aloud:**
> "You burst through the front door and tumble onto wet cobblestones. Behind you, 16 Durst Lane collapses with a sound like a great exhalation — as if the house itself has finally died. When the dust settles, the fog still surrounds you. The children are nowhere to be seen. Ahead, through the grey murk, a road leads onward — and faintly, you hear the sound of wolves."

---

## Closing Beat — The Mists of Barovia
> "You are no longer in Daggerford. The road is different — the cobblestones are older, cracked, overgrown with black moss. The trees on either side are twisted, leafless. Fog hangs at the treeline like a curtain. A crow lands on a nearby branch and tilts its head at you. Then — in the distance — a wolf howls. Answered by another. And another."

**Award:** 300 XP each (or milestone: Level 3 is earned at the end of Session 2/Week 2).
