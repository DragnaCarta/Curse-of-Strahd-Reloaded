# Month 1 · Week 2 — Arc B: Welcome to Barovia
**In-Game Date:** Day 1 of Barovia (Neyavr 1, evening)  
**Party Level:** 2 → 3 (award Level 3 after Kolyan's burial / Doru resolution)  
**Location:** Svalich Woods → Village of Barovia  
**Session Goal:** Arrive in Barovia, fight off the final night of the undead siege, bury Kolyan Indirovich, meet Ismark and Ireena, deal with Doru and Father Donavich.

---

## Opening — The Road to Barovia
> "The Svalich Road stretches before you through a dead forest. No birds. No insects. The trees are black-barked and leafless even in what should be early autumn. Ahead, at the edge of your torchlight, a skeletal figure in a hooded cloak stands motionless in the road. As you approach, it turns — and gallops away at terrifying speed on a skeletal horse, vanishing around a bend. At its feet, you see a crumpled body."

**The Body:** A Barovian man named **Dalvan Olensky**, dead from the cold. In his hand, a note: *"I tried to escape. I walked east for three days. The road turned me around. I am going home now."* His boots are worn through. He is a ghost now, condemned to walk the road forever — players may or may not see this. He represents Barovia's inescapable nature.

**The Svalich Woods Encounters (roll or choose):**
- A **dire wolf** pack (3 wolves + 1 dire wolf) shadows the party, never attacking unless provoked — they are Strahd's scouts.
- A **raven** with silver-tipped wings perches ahead of the party and hops along the road — showing the way to the village. This is **Muriel Vinshaw** in bird form.
- The road occasionally doubles back — impossible geography. The party eventually arrives regardless.

---

## Scene 1 — The Village Under Siege (Dusk)
> "Through the treeline, you see torchlight and hear shouting. A cluster of buildings surrounded by a crude barricade of wagon wheels and sharpened stakes — the Village of Barovia. At the barricades, men and women with pitchforks and torches are pushing back a wave of shambling undead. 'They come every night!' a man shouts as he sees you. 'Help us! Please!'"

**The Last Night of the Siege (Combat):**
- 8 **zombies** press against the barricades. 4 are already inside the perimeter.
- The villagers (commoner stats) are exhausted — they have been at this for 6 nights.
- If the party helps: the siege is broken within 3 rounds. The final zombie falls and the others simply… stop. They turn and walk back into the fog.
- **Mood:** The villagers are not grateful — they are numb. One old woman says: "Every night it's the same. They stop at dawn. They come again at dusk. Until the master decides they don't."

**Award:** After the siege, award **Level 3** here. The villagers see the party as outsiders but grudgingly accept their help.

---

## Scene 2 — The Burgomaster's Mansion
> "A two-story manor at the center of town. The front door hangs open. Inside, a young man with dark circles under his eyes sits at a table, staring at a cold cup of tea. He looks up. 'I am Ismark Kolyanovich. My father died last night. Strahd came himself — the first time in years he's left his castle. He… fed from my sister. I need help. My father needs to be buried at the church. And Ireena needs to leave.'"

### Ismark Kolyanovich
**Appearance:** Late 20s, broad-shouldered, dark hair, haunted eyes. The kind of man who holds himself like he's carrying weight he didn't ask for.  
**Voice:** Quiet, direct, self-deprecating. He mentions "Ismark the Great" — a legendary ancestor — with bitterness. He doesn't think he measures up.  
**What he wants:** Bury his father. Get Ireena to Vallaki safely.  
**What he offers:** Directions, local knowledge, and his sword (he will accompany the party to Vallaki if asked, though he won't go into Castle Ravenloft).

> *Ismark's lines:*  
> "They call me Ismark the Lesser. Not without reason."  
> "Ireena didn't choose to look like… whoever she looks like. That's not her fault."  
> "My father died trying to keep the undead out with a sword. He was fifty-eight years old. He deserved better than this land."

### Ireena Kolyana
**Appearance:** Early 20s, auburn hair, two bite marks on her neck (now scarring). Sharp eyes. She's been awake for six nights defending the house.  
**Personality:** Stubborn, brave, quick to argue. She does NOT want to be escorted anywhere like cargo. She wants to fight.  
**Key Moment:** She will accept leaving Barovia for Vallaki only if Ismark explains it's for the people they'd leave behind — not for her own safety. She cares more about others than herself.

> *Ireena's lines:*  
> "I'm not a package to be delivered."  
> "He looked at me like… like he recognized me. Like I was something he'd lost and found again. It was the most terrifying thing I've ever felt."  
> "Where exactly is safe in Barovia? Tell me. I'll pack a bag."

---

## Scene 3 — The Church of the Morninglord
> "The village church is small and badly maintained. The windows are boarded. From inside, you can hear soft, broken weeping — and underneath it, something else. A scratching. A hissing. A thin voice that doesn't sound fully human anymore: 'Father… Father please… I'm so hungry…'"

### Father Donavich
**Appearance:** Grey-haired, gaunt, eyes red from weeping. He hasn't slept in days. He answers the door clutching a holy symbol that he doesn't seem to fully believe in anymore.  
**The truth:** His son Doru led a mob to Castle Ravenloft after Van Richten's inspiration. Strahd turned Doru into a vampire spawn and sent him back as a punishment — still sane enough to hate what he's become, not sane enough to control it.  
**Donavich will not let Doru be killed** — at first. He believes his prayers will cure him. He is wrong.

### Doru
**Location:** Locked in the church basement.  
**Appearance:** Young man, 19, dark hair, pale as wax, veins visible under skin. Eyes flash red when hungry. Wears remnants of the clothing he left home in.  
**Personality (when calm):** Terrified of himself. Lucid in brief windows. Whispers apologies. His last words before transformation were probably about his betrothed, **Gertruda**.  
**Vampire Spawn Stats** — but only attack if a player gets within 5 feet OR if he smells blood.

**Three Paths with Doru:**
1. **Convince Father Donavich to let Doru be destroyed** (DC 14 Persuasion or show compassion + evidence of what Strahd is): Donavich weeps, unlocks the door, holds his son one last time. Doru, during a lucid moment, thanks the party and asks someone to tell Gertruda he's sorry. When destroyed, his face returns to peaceful.  
2. **Attempt to cure Doru** (requires a Remove Curse spell + successful DC 16 Religion to channel the Morninglord's grace): This *barely* works — Doru is freed from vampire spawn status but weakened and haunted. He becomes a neutral NPC who can later join the resistance.  
3. **Leave it unresolved:** Donavich continues praying. Doru continues suffering. This becomes a dangling thread.

---

## Scene 4 — Burying Kolyan Indirovich
> "The churchyard is overgrown. Old headstones lean at angles. At dawn, the four of you lower the coffin of Kolyan Indirovich into the earth while Father Donavich speaks words he no longer believes. Ireena holds her brother's arm. She doesn't cry. 'He tried so hard,' she says quietly. 'He wanted us to have a normal life. In Barovia. As if that were possible.'"

This is a quiet, solemn scene. Let the players breathe. It sets the tone for what Barovia costs people.

**A Crow Watches:** From the cemetery wall, a crow observes the burial, then flies west. Players who succeed on DC 13 Perception notice it blinks sideways — wrong, somehow.

---

## Closing Beat — Departure Prepared
With the burial done, Ismark asks the party to help him and Ireena leave for Vallaki first thing the next morning. Ireena packs one bag. She looks back once at the house she grew up in.

> "The fog is thicker at night. From somewhere in the trees, you hear music — a harpsichord, distant and melancholy, playing something you almost recognize."

**Award:** Milestone — Party reaches **Level 3** (if not already granted after the siege).
