# Month 1 · Week 4 — Arc C (Part 2): The Road to Vallaki
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 2, morning → Neyavr 2, late evening  
**Party Level:** 4 → 5 (award Level 5 on arriving safely in Vallaki)  
**Location:** Tser Pool → Old Svalich Road → Vallaki gates  
**Session Goal:** Travel the remaining road to Vallaki, encounter Morgantha and the road's dangers, and arrive in Vallaki for the first time.

---

## Opening — Departure from Tser Pool
> "Dawn comes to Barovia the way it always does — grudgingly. The sky lightens from black to grey without ever quite becoming day. The Vistani camp stirs around you: fires being fed, horses watered, low morning voices. Madam Eva's tent is dark and still. She does not emerge to see you off. She doesn't need to."

Muriel Vinshaw offers to travel with the party for a while. She's heading toward Vallaki anyway. She is cheerful in a strange way — Ravens are by nature trickster-adjacent. She deflects personal questions but seems to find the party amusing.

---

## Scene 1 — Sir Godfrey Gwilym (The Road Encounter)
> "A figure in tarnished silver armor stands at a crossroads marker, motionless as a statue. As you approach, it turns — a knight whose face is gaunt and hollow-eyed, skin stretched too tight, clearly dead and yet clearly aware. 'Travelers,' it says in a voice like a creaking door. 'I am Sir Godfrey Gwilym of the Order of the Silver Dragon. I have walked this road for three hundred years. I protect travelers from the dark — when I can. Tell me: do you come to serve the devil, or to oppose him?'"

**Sir Godfrey — Revenant:**
- Lawful Good. He has been waiting for someone worthy since Argynvost fell.
- If the party says they oppose Strahd: he nods slowly. "Then you may yet have allies. Seek Argynvostholt. The Order's ghost lingers there. And our Lord Argynvost's skull rests in the castle of the vampire. Until it is restored, there is no hope for us."
- He gives the party directions to Argynvostholt (southwest of Vallaki). "When you are ready. Not yet."
- He resumes his post and does not follow. He has been waiting. He can wait longer.

---

## Scene 2 — Morgantha's Dream Pastries
> "Coming the other direction: a hunched figure with a wide-brimmed hat, pushing a wooden cart. The cart smells extraordinary — sweet, warm, spiced. Like every comfort you've ever known. The figure calls out in a cracked voice: 'Dream pastries! Fresh-baked dream pastries! Sweet sleep in every bite! Two copper pieces, dearies!'"

**Morgantha** (disguised as an old human woman — she is actually a **night hag**):
- She knows who the party is. She knows a great deal. She pretends not to.
- She sells "dream pastries" — Barovian narcotic treats that give the eater 8 hours of blissful, pleasant dream-sleep. Addictive. The Barovians who eat them regularly are becoming catatonic.
- She's on her way from Old Bonegrinder (visible in the distance on a hill — an old windmill) to Vallaki to sell to her regular customers.

**If players are suspicious (DC 14 Insight):** She slips up — mentions a detail about the party she couldn't know from just passing them on the road. Then smiles with too many teeth.

**If players attack her:** She is not here for a fight. She cackles, says "Not today, not today," and vanishes in a cloud of smoke. She'll remember them.

**If players buy a pastry and eat it:** They sleep deeply. Dreams of home — whatever home means to that character. When they wake, they feel hollow, as if the dream took something instead of giving it.

**Note the windmill (Old Bonegrinder):** Two smaller figures can be glimpsed moving in the upper floors. This is where Morgantha's sisters and the **heartstone** used in Arc H will be found. Plant the seed.

---

## Scene 3 — The Werewolf Ambush
> "The horses are restless. Ismark reaches for his sword. And then — from both sides of the road — shapes pour from the treeline. Not wolves. Not men. Something between."

**The Ambush:** 4 **werewolves** (use stat block) + 2 regular **wolves** flanking. This is a patrol loyal to **Kiril Stoyanovich**, the pack alpha. They are testing new arrivals.

**Encounter Notes:**
- Werewolves will attempt to bite at least one party member (introducing the lycanthropy risk mechanic: player must succeed on DC 12 Constitution save or become cursed with lycanthropy, curable before next full moon with *Remove Curse* or wolfsbane).
- They retreat after 2 are downed — this is reconnaissance, not war.
- If the party captures one: under duress, the werewolf growls: "The master counts all who enter the valley. You will be counted." It will not say more.
- **Muriel transforms** to fight if the party is overwhelmed — she shifts into a large raven form with silver-tipped talons. This reveals her wereraven nature to the party.

**After the fight:** Muriel is matter-of-fact about the reveal. "I'm a wereraven. We're different from werewolves. We chose our gift." She doesn't elaborate unless pressed.

---

## Scene 4 — Vallaki's Gates at Dusk
> "As the light fails, the walls of Vallaki appear through the trees. High timber palisade walls, guard towers, iron gates. Two guards in padded armor block the road. 'State your business. And be quick — gates close at sundown.'"

**The Guards:** Suspicious, tired, following orders.
- Party must state a reason for entering (travelers, merchants, nothing suspicious).
- If Ireena is with them: the guard squints at her. "She looks like one of them Barovia locals. We've had… trouble from those types lately. Izek's orders. Verify with the innkeeper."
- DC 12 Persuasion to enter smoothly. On a fail: they're escorted to the Baron's mansion for "questioning" (which becomes an early Arc G preview).

**Inside Vallaki:**
> "Colorful banners hang from every building, far more than any practical purpose could explain. They say 'ALL IS WELL' in bright paint. Painted suns are nailed to doors. Citizens walk with their eyes down, smiling slightly — the fixed smile of people who have been reminded what happens to those who don't smile. A man is being dragged toward the stocks. 'But the festival failed!' he shouts. 'It rained the whole time!' 'ALL IS WELL,' a guard barks, and shoves him forward."

**The Blue Water Inn:** Where the party will stay. Run by **Urwin Martikov** and his wife Danika. Two burly sons help manage the place. It smells like real food and has actual beds. The party's first safe rest since Death House.

**Urwin, aside, to the party:** "You look like trouble. We like trouble here. Carefully, quietly." He slides them each an extra drink. "The ravens tell me you came from Tser Pool. Madam Eva's people. That means something to us."

---

## Closing Beat — Vallaki Orientation
The party has arrived. This is their base of operations for the next month (in-game). Before they sleep, give them a quick orientation:

- **St. Andral's Church** — visible through the inn window; warm light, Father Lucian inside.
- **The Baron's Mansion** — imposing structure, guards at the door.
- **Blinsky Toys** — odd little shop on the square, lights on even at this hour.
- **Coffin-maker's shop** — dark, no lights. Something feels wrong about it.

> "Vallaki. It is not safe. Nothing in Barovia is safe. But it is, at least, a roof over your heads — and for tonight, that might be enough."

**Award:** The session ends with a long rest. Next session begins with Act II.
