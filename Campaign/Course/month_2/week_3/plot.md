# Month 2 · Week 3 — Arc G: The Strazni Siblings + Arc H: The Lost Soul (Part 1)
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 5–6  
**Party Level:** 7 → 8 (award Level 8 after Wachter's coup succeeds + Stella rescued)  
**Location:** Vallaki — The Baron's Festival, Streets, Ethereal Plane approach  
**Session Goal:** Confront or eliminate Izek Strazni; begin the Lost Soul arc (recover the heartstone from Old Bonegrinder).

---

## Scene 1 — Izek Strazni in the Streets
> "You hear him before you see him — a heavy tread, the creak of leather armor, and the sound of shouting. Around a corner: Izek Strazni, the Baron's enforcer. He is enormous — over six feet, muscled like a draft horse, with one arm of ordinary flesh and one arm that is something else entirely. A diabolic limb, red-black and scaled, terminating in fingers that curl like a hawk's talons. From it, a flame flickers. He is dragging a man toward the stocks who is apparently guilty of 'conducting himself without sufficient optimism.'"

**Izek Strazni — Reading the Character:**
- He is brutal, simple, and utterly loyal to the Baron — but he is not stupid.
- He is obsessed with a woman who matches Ireena's description. He doesn't know why. He can't explain it to himself. It is not love — it is something that predates love, a hole in his memory where a sister used to be.
- He does not recognize Ireena on sight (he was separated from her as a child, before either could form clear memories). But when he sees her — he *stares*. And goes still.

**If Ireena is with the party when they encounter Izek:**
> "Izek drops the man he's dragging. He stares at Ireena the way a drowning man stares at a rope. His diabolic arm trembles. 'You,' he says. Just that. Ireena takes a step back. 'I— have we met?' she asks. Something crosses his face — not cruelty. Something much more confused. He shakes his head once, like a dog clearing water from its ears. Then his eyes harden. 'Festival. Neyavr 7. You will attend.' He walks away."

**The Doll Evidence:** If the party has already visited Blinsky Toys, they know about the Ireena dolls. If a player confronts Izek: he grabs the doll from whoever's holding it, looks at it, and throws it away. "Blinsky makes what I ask." He doesn't explain.

## Scene 2 — The Festival of the Blazing Sun (Neyavr 7)
> "The town square is decorated in yellow and orange. A large wicker effigy of a sun stands at the center. Citizens are present — some by choice, some by guard escort. The Baron himself stands on a platform in golden robes, beaming. Lady Vallakovich watches from a window, looking exhausted. Izek prowls the perimeter."

**The Festival's Descent:**
- At midday, it begins to rain.
- The Baron declares it is still going well.
- An old woman in the crowd says, audibly: "This is foolish."
- Izek moves toward her.

**If the party doesn't intervene:** Izek drags the old woman toward the wicker sun effigy and lights it with his diabolic arm — with her inside it. This is the moment the Baron's regime tips into unambiguous atrocity. Players should feel the weight of not acting.

**If the party intervenes:** Combat with Izek. This is a significant fight.

**Izek Strazni — Boss Combat:**
- AC 15 (splint armor + natural resilience)
- HP: 112
- **Diabolic Arm (Action):** Melee attack +8, 2d8+5 fire damage
- **Fan of Flames (Action, Recharge 5–6):** 30-ft cone, DC 14 Dex, 5d6 fire
- **Hellfire Hurl (Bonus Action):** 60-ft range, +7 to hit, 3d6 fire
- **Reckless (1/turn):** Advantage on one attack; attacks against him have advantage until his next turn
- **If Izek is reduced to 0 HP:** He falls but does not dissolve into mist — he is not undead. He is simply a man. A dying man.

**The Sibling Reveal (Optional — triggers if Izek is at 10 HP or less and Ireena is present):**
> "Ireena kneels next to Izek. Something about the angle of his jaw, the shade of his hair — she reaches out and touches the scar on his collarbone. 'This scar,' she says softly. 'I have one exactly like this. We had a dog when we were little. My brother and I —' She goes pale. 'My brother's name was Izek.'"
> Izek opens his eyes. The fire in his arm gutters. "...Sister?" he says. Then dies, or lives depending on what the party chooses.

> *DM Note: The revelation that Ireena and Izek are biological siblings is one of the campaign's most powerful moments. Let it breathe. No one should speak for a full 10 seconds. Ireena will weep quietly — not for what he was, but for what he might have been.*

## Scene 3 — Aftermath: Lady Wachter Takes Power
If Izek is dead: by evening, Lady Wachter's people have removed Baron Vallakovich from the mansion (not killed — he is confined to his room and declared "unwell"). Lady Wachter addresses the town square:

> "'Vallaki has suffered under fear long enough. The festivals end today. The stocks are removed tomorrow. We will maintain order — through law, not terror. And we will pay our tributes to Castle Ravenloft, as is prudent, and nothing more.' She doesn't smile. She doesn't need to."

**The transition is not perfect:** Some guards remain loyal to the Baron. Some citizens distrust Lady Wachter. Vallaki is better, but not fixed. Father Lucian remains the town's moral anchor.

## Scene 4 — Victor Vallakovich and the Lost Soul (Arc H Hook)
That night, a ghost floats through the inn wall. Young boy, translucent, holding a ball he drops and reaches for and never catches.

> "'You can see me!' he says, startled. 'Most people can't see me. My name is Erasmus. Erasmus van Richten. I've been looking for someone who can help. My father — he doesn't know I'm here. And there's a girl. A living girl whose soul is lost in the Ethereal Plane. She's been calling for help for so long.'"

**Erasmus van Richten** — ghost of Van Richten's dead son. This is heart-wrenching if played well. He is cheerful in the way of ghosts who haven't fully accepted their state yet. He misses his father.

He leads the party to **Victor Vallakovich's** hidden workshop in the mansion's upper tower — Victor has been evading the chaos downstairs by working on his *spirit mirror*.

**Victor Vallakovich:**
> "A teenager surrounded by books, components, and a mirror that reflects a room slightly different from the one you're in. He looks at the party with the narrow-eyed suspicion of someone who expects to be mocked. 'You can see Erasmus? Right. Fine. Then you know about the mirror. Stella's soul is inside it — or was. It got pushed further into the Ethereal Plane three weeks ago and I can't get it back from here. I need a heartstone. From a night hag. I know where to get one. I just need backup because I'm not brave and I am aware of this.'"

**The Plan:** Victor can open a window to the Ethereal Plane on the night of the full moon (3 nights away — Neyavr 9). But first they need a **heartstone**, which only night hags carry, and the closest hag coven is at **Old Bonegrinder** — the windmill they passed on the road.

## Closing Beat — Old Bonegrinder
The session ends with the party heading toward the windmill on the hill. As they approach:

> "The windmill's sails turn even though there's no wind. The upper floor window is lit orange. Something about the light is wrong — too red, too warm for the cold air. And on the road below the windmill, three children sit in the mud, eating dream pastries and smiling at nothing."
