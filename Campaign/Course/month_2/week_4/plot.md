# Month 2 · Week 4 — Arc H (Conclusion) + Arc I: The Walls of Krezk
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 6–8  
**Party Level:** 8 → 9 (award Level 9 after night hag resolution + arriving Krezk)  
**Location:** Old Bonegrinder → Vallaki → Berez → Krezk  
**Session Goal:** Defeat the night hag coven, perform the Ethereal Plane ritual to rescue Stella, then travel to Krezk via Berez.

---

## Scene 1 — Old Bonegrinder (Night Hag Coven)
> "The windmill is built of weathered stone and black wood, three stories high. The sails turn slowly. At the base: three children huddled in blankets, eating pastries with dull eyes. They are alive — just barely present."

**The Hag Coven: Morgantha + Sisters**
- **Morgantha** (matriarch): The woman the party met on the road.
- **Bella Sunbane** and **Offalia Wormwiggle** (sisters): Younger, wilder, less controlled than Morgantha.

**Three hags together = coven = shared Coven Spells (PHB rules apply).**

**Approaches:**
1. **Frontal assault:** The hags are brutal in combat. They fight in their true forms — hideous, clawed, iron-tough. If any two hags are reduced to half HP, one flees to draw enemies away while the others attack from different directions.  
2. **Separate them:** One hag is always on the road selling pastries. If the party acts in the day, the coven is split (no coven powers). One-on-one a hag is manageable at Level 4.  
3. **Trade:** Morgantha will trade the heartstone for something she wants more. She wants Ireena. (Obviously the party should refuse. She knows they'll refuse. She asks anyway because it amuses her.)  
4. **Stealth + theft:** DC 18 Sleight of Hand to take the heartstone while Morgantha is asleep (she sleeps in the middle of the day — but her Truesight functions even then, so this is very hard).

**The Dream Pastry Operation:**
In the windmill: dozens of pastries in the oven, a trapped soul visible to anyone with Detect Magic in each one. The children the party saw outside are from Barovia village — their parents sent them here for the pastries because the addictive dreams are the only happiness available. This is deeply sad.

**If the children are freed from the addiction:** Victor can (in theory) use his magic to break the narcotic hold — DC 14 Arcana, consuming a spell slot per child. This earns genuine gratitude from the parents, who come to Vallaki days later to thank the party in person.

**The Heartstone:** An egg-sized purple-black gemstone, cold to the touch, that vibrates softly. It is the focal point for the Ethereal Plane ritual. Victor needs it for 1 hour.

---

## Scene 2 — The Ethereal Plane Ritual (Full Moon, Neyavr 9)
> "Victor chalks a circle on his workshop floor while Erasmus hovers in the corner, looking nervous. 'I've practiced this three hundred times,' Victor says, which is not entirely reassuring. The heartstone glows as the midnight bell rings. The circle flares silver. 'Hold on to someone,' Victor warns, and the room goes translucent."

**The Ethereal Plane:**
- Everything in the physical world is visible but ghostly, as if seen through water.
- Stella Wachter's soul appears as a glowing, transparent girl — she's been wandering the Ethereal Plane alone for weeks, calling for help. When she sees the party: relief so profound it reads as physical.
- But between the party and Stella: **Leo Dilisnya**, a bloated, ancient ghost in decayed noble clothing. He was a villain in life — a betrayer of the Order of the Silver Dragon, responsible for the death of many innocent servants. His soul has been trapped here for 300 years, feeding on other spirits.

**Leo Dilisnya — Boss Fight (Ghost Stat Block + Modifications):**
- **Possessing Howl (Action):** DC 14 Wis or one party member is possessed (they attack their allies for 1 turn, then expel the spirit on a DC 12 Wis saving throw each turn)
- **Soul Drain (Action):** One creature, DC 14 Wis, 4d6 psychic, and target's max HP is reduced by the same amount until a long rest
- **Ethereal Resistance:** Leo is immune to all damage except Force, Radiant, or attacks made with the heartstone (bonus: +2d6 radiant if heartstone is used as a focus)
- **Weakness:** If the Morninglord's name is invoked (Religion DC 12), Leo has disadvantage on all attacks for 1 round

**Stella's Rescue:** When Leo is destroyed, Stella's soul rushes back toward her body. The ritual completes. Back in the physical world, Stella Wachter takes a gasping breath and opens her eyes for the first time in weeks.

> "Lady Wachter, summoned by the sound of Stella's scream-gasp of consciousness, enters the room. She crosses it in four steps and holds her daughter's face in both hands. 'Stella. Stella.' Stella blinks at her. 'Mama? What happened?' Lady Wachter — this woman who measures every word like currency — says nothing. She just holds on."

**Award:** Milestone — **Level 5**.

---

## Scene 3 — Departure for Krezk (Arc I)
The party has unfinished business to the west: the village of Krezk is a fortified community, but its gates are closed to outsiders. **Baron Dmitri Krezkov** will only open the gates if the party does him a favor: travel to the ruins of Berez and have the swamp-witch **Baba Lysaga** use her scrying powers to find his missing son Ilya.

**The Berez Road:**
> "The path to Berez is barely a path — more like a suggestion of one, cutting south through increasingly wet, black-earthed terrain. The trees lean over the road. Crows dot every branch. Ahead, rising from the mist: the ruins of a drowned village, half-submerged, with a hut built from the bones of the place — a hut that is also, somehow, a skull."

---

## Scene 4 — Baba Lysaga
> "The hut is constructed from the timbers and stones of the submerged village, with a massive carved skull at its peak — the skull seems to breathe. From within, the sound of singing: an old lullaby in an older language. The door opens before you knock."

**Baba Lysaga — First Meeting:**
- She is ancient — centuries old — and in the grips of a delusion: she believes she is Strahd's mother (she was his wet-nurse in life, and the obsession calcified into madness over 400 years).
- She is not here to fight. Not yet. She is curious.
- She will scry for Ilya Krezkov in exchange for something: a personal item belonging to the missing boy. Baron Krezkov gave the party a childhood toy — a wooden horse Ilya carved himself.

**The Scrying:**
> Baba Lysaga stirs a pot of black water. An image forms: a young man with a wolf's instincts in the mountains north of Krezk, near Lake Baratok. He is alive. He is not entirely human. "Your lost boy is where wolves run," she says. "He went there willingly. He belongs there now, in his bones, if not in his heart." She adds: "Tell Krezkov his son will return when he is ready. And not a day before."

**Important Observation:** The players see the scale of what Baba Lysaga is. She commands animated **tree blights**. Her hut has more skulls inside. She is not a challenge they can take on yet — this is reconnaissance, not confrontation. She is a boss encounter for Act IV.

---

## Closing Beat — Arrival at Krezk
With Baba Lysaga's answer in hand, the party returns to Krezk. Baron Krezkov receives the news of his son with grief but acceptance — and opens the gates. Inside: a walled village, quieter than Vallaki, with a shared sadness. And on the hill above: **the Abbey of Saint Markovia**, where a light burns in the upper windows.

> "Something about the light in those abbey windows is wrong. It's too bright for night. Too warm for stone. And as you watch, a winged figure — too large to be a bird — circles once above the abbey and descends."

**End of Act II. Act III begins next month.**
