# Month 2 · Week 2 — Arc E: The Missing Vistana + Arc F: Lady Wachter's Wish
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 4–5  
**Party Level:** 6 → 7 (award Level 7 after Van Richten reveal + Izek fight)  
**Location:** Vallaki — Vistani Camp, Blinsky Toys, Blue Water Inn → Wachterhaus  
**Session Goal:** Find and rescue Arabelle; accept Lady Wachter's dinner invitation; decide whether to help her.

---

## Scene 1 — Investigating Arabelle's Disappearance
The party can follow several leads around Vallaki:

**Blinsky Toys:**
> "The toy shop window is full of marionettes, windup contraptions, and — you stop — dolls. Dozens of dolls with auburn hair and green eyes, wearing traveling cloaks. They all look like Ireena. The door opens with a bell jingle. A small, sad man in a jester's costume looks up from a workbench. 'Welcome, welcome. Blinsky says: if it's not fun, it's not Blinsky's.'"

**Blinsky (Gadof Blinsky):** Perpetually melancholy toy-maker. He made the Ireena-lookalike dolls on commission — from Izek Strazni. "He comes in with charcoal sketches. He wants them to look exactly right. I ask no questions. Izek has no sense of humor about questions." This is a major Izek foreshadowing moment.

**On Arabelle:** Blinsky says a young Vistani girl came in asking about a "silver carriage" two days ago, then left.

**Vistani Camp (outside Vallaki's east gate):**
Luvash is disheveled and desperate. Arrigal is watching the party with careful eyes.

> **Arrigal (aside, while Luvash is distracted):** "My brother will offer you the stars to find his daughter. Bring her back alive and unhurt. Whatever Van Richten has told you about Vistani — about *us* — remember that a father's love for his child is the same in any language." He looks directly at you. Too directly.

**Investigation Leads:**
- A Vistani woman saw a man with a gray horse and a wagon with painted stars (Rictavio's wagon) near where Arabelle vanished.
- Footprints lead to **Rictavio's carnival wagon** parked outside the Blue Water Inn — large, locked.
- DC 13 Perception: the wagon smells of animals and herbs. Something is alive inside.

---

## Scene 2 — Rictavio / Dr. Van Richten
> "The wagon door is locked with three separate locks. While you're examining it, a voice behind you: 'I wouldn't do that if I were you. The tiger gets cranky when strangers knock.'"

A man in a wide-brimmed hat, false nose, and theatrical makeup leans against the inn wall. He calls himself **Rictavio**, a traveling carnival master.

**Van Richten's Cover:** He is Van Richten, but he won't admit it — not yet. If the party figures it out (DC 15 Insight or mentions Van Richten's name), he shows the faintest flicker of reaction before controlling it.

**The truth about Arabelle:** She was being watched by a Vistani informant of Strahd's. Van Richten recognized her divine bloodline (she is descended from the Seeker goddess — Strahd wants her for the Grand Conjunction). He "rescued" her by hiding her in his wagon. He genuinely believes she's safe. He's wrong that this approach was right, but he meant well.

**Arabelle:** A girl of perhaps twelve, sitting cross-legged in the wagon, calmly reading a book. She looks up at the party with dark, ancient eyes.

> "Hello. I know who you are. You're the ones from Madam Eva's vision. She told me you might come." She closes the book. "I had a dream last night. I dreamed the sun came back to Barovia. I think it has something to do with you."

She willingly returns to her father. Before she goes:
> "Beware of the man with the silver arm. He is not the monster he appears, but he is dangerous to my father's brother. And beware the pastry woman on the hill — her kindness is hungry."

**Returning Arabelle to Luvash:** Tearful reunion. Luvash nearly crushes her. The reward is given. Arrigal watches Arabelle return with something complicated in his eyes.

**Kasimir Velikov Encounter:** Near the Vistani camp, a grey-robed figure sits apart — a dusk elf with a signet ring and the look of someone carrying a very old grief.

> "You are going to Ravenloft eventually," he says without looking up. "I know this because you are the kind of people who end up in Ravenloft. When you do — there is a place below, in the crypts. St. Markovia's crypt. There is something there I need. We may be able to help each other."

He does not explain further today. He plants the seed.

## Scene 3 — Lady Wachter's Dinner Invitation
That evening, a well-dressed servant delivers a sealed note to the party at the Blue Water Inn:

> *"You are invited to dine at Wachterhaus this evening at the ninth bell. I have a matter of mutual interest to discuss. Come alone — that is, without the Baron's guards knowing. I will make it worth your while. — F. Wachter"*

**Wachterhaus:**
> "The Wachter house is old money — stone walls, oil portraits of ancestors who all look like they disapproved of something, furniture that costs more than most Barovians earn in a year. The servant shows you to a dining room. At the head of the table sits a woman in black silk who makes it look like armor."

**Lady Fiona Wachter:**
- **Voice:** Measured, precise, never raises it. Every word is placed like a chess piece.
- **Personality:** She is not cruel. She is *practical*, which is sometimes worse.
- **What she wants:** She wants Baron Vallakovich dead — or at minimum, politically destroyed. She has a longer plan: a Vallaki that cooperates with Strahd lives longer than one that defies him pointlessly. She sees herself as the valley's best hope for survival.

> "Baron Vallakovich is insane. That alone might be forgivable. He is also *dangerous* — his festivals will eventually provoke Strahd into an act of mass violence that will make what happened to the Village of Barovia look mild. I want him removed. I want Izek Strazni removed. I want to lead this city, and I want to lead it *well*."

**Her Offer:**
She wants the party to kill **Izek Strazni** specifically. She provides:
- A vial of poison (odorless, for his wine at the Festival)
- A burlap sack "for his head — proof"
- Payment: 200 gp each + a promise of safe passage through Vallaki for the duration of their stay

**Her Leverage:**
She knows who Van Richten really is. She knows the party arrived from Barovia. She knows about the bones at St. Andral's (Ernst Larnak, her spy, is everywhere). She doesn't threaten — she informs, which is more unsettling.

**Ernst Larnak:** Lady Wachter's spy, present at dinner. Middle-aged, forgettable face, the kind of person you don't remember after he leaves a room. He is watching the party carefully.

**Stella Wachter:** Lady Wachter's daughter sits in a chair in the corner, staring at nothing. She is catatonic — her soul is shattered. Lady Wachter briefly, painfully, looks at her daughter during dinner. "My daughter had an accident involving a young man and forbidden magic. I would appreciate any assistance in that matter as well — but that is a separate conversation."

## Closing Beat — A Difficult Choice
The party leaves Wachterhaus with Lady Wachter's offer in hand. This is a morally complex decision:

**Killing Izek arguments:**
- He terrorizes Vallaki's citizens daily
- He will kidnap Ireena during the Festival (Arc G)
- Lady Wachter's leadership, while pragmatic, is better than Vallakovich's insanity

**Not killing Izek:**
- He is, potentially, Ireena's lost biological brother
- Killing him for political hire feels morally dirty
- Lady Wachter's loyalism to Strahd is its own problem

Let the players argue it out. Don't resolve it yet — Arc G happens next session and forces the issue.
