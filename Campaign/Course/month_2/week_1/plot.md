# Month 2 · Week 1 — Arc D: St. Andral's Feast + Arc E (Opening)
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 3–4  
**Party Level:** 5 → 6 (award Level 6 after returning the Bones and securing the ward)  
**Location:** Vallaki — St. Andral's Church, Coffin-Maker's Shop  
**Session Goal:** Discover the stolen bones of St. Andral, track them to the coffin-maker, confront Volenta and her vampire spawn before the Feast on Neyavr 7.

---

## Opening — Morning in Vallaki (Neyavr 3)
> "You wake to the sound of guards outside calling: 'ALL IS WELL. ALL IS WELL.' It is, demonstrably, not well. Through the inn window you see a man being escorted toward the stocks for the crime of — you catch a fragment — 'expressing undue pessimism.'"

**Urwin Martikov** delivers breakfast and whispers: "The Festival of the Blazing Sun is in four days — Neyavr 7. The Baron insists it will prove everything is fine. People are afraid. The town church is the only place most of them actually feel safe — Father Lucian keeps the darkness out."

**Transition to St. Andral's Church:**
Ireena wants to attend morning prayers. The church is warm and smells of incense and pine. **Father Lucian Petrovich** greets them with genuine warmth — not the strained warmth of someone performing, but the real thing.

---

## Scene 1 — Father Lucian's Worry
> "Father Lucian sets down his broom and gives you his full attention — the way some people give you their full attention, like you're the only person in the world. He's perhaps fifty, with grey at his temples and laugh lines around his eyes. He doesn't look like a man who laughs much lately. 'I'm glad you're here,' he says. 'I need help. I need it quietly.'"

**The Problem:** The bones of St. Andral — relics consecrated into the floor of the church that create a protective ward — have been stolen. Without them, on the night of St. Andral's Feast (Neyavr 7), the church's protection vanishes at midnight. If Volenta strikes that night: dozens die.

**Father Lucian's Lines:**
> "I noticed them gone three mornings ago. I've told no one — if the Baron finds out, he'll blame it on Barovian immigrants and do something terrible. I just need someone to find them and bring them back. Quietly."

> "The only person who knew where they were hidden — under the altar — was our groundskeeper, Milivoj. He's been strange lately. Distant. And he keeps looking toward the coffin-maker's shop."

**Milivoj (Groundskeeper):** Young man, 17, guilt written all over him. With a DC 10 Persuasion (or just being kind), he breaks. He sold the bones to **Henrik van der Voort**, the coffin-maker, for a handful of coins. He needed the money — his parents are dead and he has seven younger siblings to feed. "I didn't know what they were for," he says. "I thought they were just old bones."

> *DM Note: Don't punish Milivoj too harshly in play. He's a desperate teenager. Father Lucian forgives him on the spot — "You were afraid and hungry. That is not a sin I can condemn." This moment sets Father Lucian as a moral anchor for the campaign.*

---

## Scene 2 — The Coffin-Maker's Shop
> "Henrik van der Voort's shop sits at the edge of the square, always slightly apart from its neighbors. The windows are shuttered. No lights. The sign — a carved coffin with a lily — creaks in the wind. On the doorstep: a fresh set of footprints in the mud. Something with bare feet, clawed."

**Henrik van der Voort:** A nervous middle-aged man with dark circles. He answers the door, takes one look at the party, and tries to close it. He is terrified — not of the party, of what's in his shop.

**The truth:** Volenta Popofsky moved in three nights ago. She's been using his shop as her lair, building a small nest of three **vampire spawn** she's converting from kidnapped Vallakians. She has Henrik utterly cowed — "She'll kill my children if I speak to anyone."

**Players must get into the shop:**
- **Persuasion DC 16** to get Henrik to talk (he's terrified, but he wants out).
- **Intimidation DC 12** — more effective, ironically, because at least the party is threatening something he can see.
- **Breaking in at night** works — but Volenta will know they're coming.

**The Best Approach:** Be kind to Henrik. If the party explains the church and what's at stake, he breaks — "I didn't know they were for a massacre," he says. "I thought Volenta just wanted the bones as… a joke. She makes horrible jokes."

---

## Scene 3 — Volenta Popofsky (Boss Fight)
> "The upper floor of the shop is dark. Then a giggle — light, bright, almost childlike — breaks the silence. 'Visitors! How fun. Henrik never has visitors.' A figure drops from the rafters like a spider. She's young-looking, dark-haired, with paint-stained fingers and wide eyes that haven't blinked in a while. 'Oh, you're here for the bones! I was going to do such interesting things with them.' She tilts her head ninety degrees. 'Pity.'"

**Volenta Popofsky — Phase 1 Stat Block (modified Vampire Spawn):**
- **Alchemical Vials (Action):** She throws a vial as a ranged attack (30 ft). Effect by color:
  - **Red (Alchemist's Fire):** 2d6 fire, ignites on hit (1d6 ongoing until DC 12 Dex to extinguish)
  - **Green (Acid):** 2d6 acid, -2 to AC until end of next turn
  - **Black (Necrotic):** 2d6 necrotic, DC 14 Con or -1 to max HP until long rest
- **Screech (Bonus Action, 1/day):** Volenta shrieks — DC 13 Con or deafened for 1 minute
- **Bat Swarm (Reaction, triggered at half HP):** 1d4+1 **swarms of bats** burst from the walls and join the fight
- **At 0 HP:** Volenta doesn't die — she dissolves into mist and retreats through a crack in the wall. "This isn't over! I'll tell Strahd about you — he'll love you!" She escapes. She will be encountered again at Castle Ravenloft (Arc O).

**The Three Vampire Spawn:** Standard stat blocks. They are freshly turned Vallakians and can be identified by clothing — one wears a cobbler's apron, one a baker's hat. They fight mindlessly.

**The Bones:** In a locked chest behind the altar replica Volenta built as a mockery. The chest key is on a chain around Henrik's neck — he'll give it the moment Volenta is driven off.

---

## Scene 4 — Returning the Bones
> "Father Lucian takes the relics with both hands, pressing them to his chest as if they were a returned child. 'Thank you,' he says simply. Then, more quietly: 'I don't know how much longer I can hold this place together. The town is so afraid. But while these walls stand — so do I.'"

**Restoring the Ward:** Father Lucian resets the bones beneath the altar with a brief ceremony. The church's protective field hums back to life — visible to anyone with Detect Magic as a faint golden dome over the building.

**Award:** Milestone — **Level 4** here. The party has saved dozens of lives.

---

## Scene 5 — Arc E Hook: The Missing Girl
**Transition to Arc E:** That evening at the Blue Water Inn, a commotion outside. Shouting in Vistani. A man with a broken arm and wild eyes is demanding the party's help.

> "'My daughter!' he shouts, slamming his fist on the bar. 'Arabelle! She's gone! Gone from the camp! Three days — three days she's been missing and these cowards —'" he gestures at the Vallaki guards flanking him "'— they say she's just a Vistani girl. Just a Vistani girl! I am Luvash, and I am calling in every debt this city owes my people!'"

**Luvash:** Desperate, furious, loving father. His brother **Arrigal** stands behind him, calm and watchful — too calm. Arrigal is spying for Strahd. He doesn't look like it.

**The reward:** Luvash offers a trunk of gold, a cart horse, and "a favor that will be worth more than any of those" in exchange for finding Arabelle.

End the session here — the investigation of Arc E begins next week.
