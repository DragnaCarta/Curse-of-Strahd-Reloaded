# Month 4 · Week 1 — Arc R: Trials of the Mountain
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 16–18  
**Party Level:** 13 → 14 (award Level 14 after reaching the Amber Temple approach)  
**Location:** Vallaki → Tsolenka Pass → Soldav  
**Session Goal:** Journey with Kasimir to Soldav; learn the secret of the Three Fanes from Elder Burebis; survive the mountain pass under Strahd's Tyrant-phase harassment.

---

## Opening — Strahd the Tyrant Begins
> "The first night after the beacon lit, he came. Not in his carriage, not with courtly grace — he simply appeared at the edge of the camp, sitting on a rock, watching the fire. When someone noticed him: 'I am re-evaluating you,' he said. 'I thought you were interesting. Now I see you are *dangerous*. I have not faced dangerous in some time.' He stood. 'I will enjoy destroying you.' And then he was gone."

**Tyrant-Phase Strahd Behavior:**
- Appears near the party each night (but never fights — he is testing their nerves)
- Sends targeted threats to their allies: Lady Wachter receives a raven with a message, "Cooperate or the town suffers"
- Dispatches Rahadin to observe the party covertly (DC 18 Perception to spot him)
- Increases undead patrols on the road

---

## Scene 1 — Departure with Kasimir Velikov
> "The dusk elf is ready at dawn, a single pack on his back, his platinum signet ring glowing faintly in the morning light. He looks at the party with the expression of a man who has waited too long for this moment and is afraid of what he'll find at the end of it. 'The Amber Temple is at the peak of Mount Ghakis,' he says. 'The road goes through Tsolenka Pass. It is not a safe road. Nothing in Barovia is safe. But you already know that.'"

**Kasimir's Story (revealed on the road):**
He can be drawn out over the journey. If players ask:
- His people, the dusk elves, once lived freely in the valley. Strahd forced them out. Those who remain are refugees under Vistani protection.
- His sister Patrina was a brilliant archmage who made a catastrophic mistake: she offered to help Strahd achieve immortality. Her own people stoned her to death for it. Strahd turned her corpse into a wight and put her down himself — "an act of mercy," he said.
- Kasimir wants to reach the Amber Temple and find Patrina's soul. He wants to bring her back.
- He is not sure this is a good idea. He is going to do it anyway.

> *DM Note: Kasimir is not a villain. He is a man carrying a grief too large to set down. His plan to resurrect Patrina is complicated — she hid the Sunsword inside a vestige's sarcophagus as her own phylactery. The party will need to deal with her. Kasimir will not like this.*

---

## Scene 2 — Tsolenka Pass
> "The pass is a narrow ledge cut into the mountain face — on one side, sheer rock rising to the clouds; on the other, a drop into white nothing. Wind cuts through every layer of clothing. The road is covered with frozen ash that crumbles like old bones underfoot."

**The Pass Encounters:**
- **Strahd's Nightly Visit (Night 1 on the mountain):** He appears at the camp's edge again. He sits across the fire from the party. He says: "The Temple will corrupt you. It does. It corrupts everyone." He pauses. "I speak from experience." Then he leaves.

- **Sangzor, the Mountain Goat (optional encounter):** A giant goat the size of a draft horse, jet black with yellow eyes, blocks the pass. It attacks anything that comes too close — it is a sacred animal to the mountain folk of Soldav. **Combat or non-combat:** a DC 16 Animal Handling check convinces it to step aside; alternatively, a DC 14 Persuasion check invoking the Ladies' names works because Sangzor recognizes the reverence.

- **The Fiends:** Corrupted creatures from the Amber Temple's miasma attack twice: **2 vrocks** at a chokepoint in the pass. At Level 7-8, this is a challenging but winnable fight.

- **Strahd's Second Visit (Night 2):** He does not speak. He only watches. His silence is worse than his words.

---

## Scene 3 — Soldav: The Hidden Village
> "A village you couldn't find if you weren't led here — nestled in a hidden valley off the main pass, invisible from below. The buildings are ancient, built of stone fitted without mortar. The people have the look of those who have been hidden for so long that secrecy has become a reflex."

**The Mountain Folk (Tabaxi-adjacent stat blocks, uses Tribal Warrior for combat):**
- **Kavan:** Weapons-master, suspicious of outsiders, will warm to the party if they show respect for the old ways
- **Helwa:** Keeper of the village's oral history, knows the lore of the Ladies
- **Diegia, Oroles, Como:** Village leaders, cautious but not hostile

They accept Kasimir — barely. The dusk elves and mountain folk have old tensions. But they accept the party if the party approaches without weapons drawn.

---

## Scene 4 — Elder Burebis ⭐ PIVOTAL SCENE
> "In the village's largest building — which is still small — an ancient figure sits wrapped in blankets. He is enormous even seated: a storm giant, shrunken by incredible age. His skin is the color of thunderclouds. His eyes are the color of lightning. He is dying. He knows it. He seems at peace with it."

**Elder Burebis** is ancient beyond reckoning — one of the last storm giants who watched over the valley before Strahd arrived.

> "I have been waiting for someone worth telling," he says. His voice is thunder turned gentle. "The Ladies Three. You know their names. You know their Fanes. You do not yet know what the Fanes are — truly. Sit."

**The Revelation:**
> "The Fanes are not simply sacred sites. They are *anchors* — the Ladies wove their power into the valley through them. Three points: the Swamp Fane at Berez, for the Weaver. The Forest Fane near Yester Hill, for the Huntress. The Mountain Fane at the base of this very peak, for the Seeker. When the man who calls himself master of this valley desecrated those sites and bound himself to them — he became, in a literal sense, part of the land. He cannot die while that bond holds."

> "To sever the bond: reconsecrate each Fane. The Weaver's gem, carried by the winery's Martikovs, must be planted at Berez. The Huntress's spear — which I sense you carry — must be planted at Yester Hill. The Seeker's gem, similarly, at the Mountain Fane. Each reconsecration weakens him. All three completed: he loses his lair actions in the final battle and cannot use the land's power to regenerate."

> He closes his eyes. "I have told you what I have kept for a century. My work is done." He opens them one more time. "The man in the dark castle — he was not always what he is. Remember that. In the last moment, when you stand before him — remember that. It may matter."

He dies quietly, before morning. The mountain folk mourn in silence.

**Award:** Milestone — **Level 8** at this scene's conclusion. The party now has the full strategic picture.

## Closing Beat — The Amber Temple Ahead
> "Above Soldav, the mountain continues to rise into clouds so thick they look solid. Somewhere up there, in the perpetual cold, the Amber Temple waits. Kasimir stares up at the peak with an expression that is impossible to read — hope and dread wearing each other's clothes."
