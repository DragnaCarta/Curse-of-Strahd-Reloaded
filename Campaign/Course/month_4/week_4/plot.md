# Month 4 · Week 4 — Arc U: Dreams of Dawn + The Final Battle
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 23  
**Party Level:** 16 → 17 (award Level 17 on defeating Strahd and freeing Barovia)  
**Location:** Vallaki War Council → The Dream → Castle Ravenloft (Final Assault)  
**Session Goal:** Hold the war council; refuse the Dark Powers' bargain; assault Castle Ravenloft; destroy the Heart of Sorrow; defeat Strahd. The dawn rises.

---

## Opening — Eternal Night in Vallaki
> "There is no dawn. The sky is black — not night-black, but *nothing*-black, as if the concept of day has simply been removed. Torches and lanterns burn but cast light only a few feet. People stay indoors. Children are quiet in a way that is wrong. Lady Wachter stands on the town hall steps, looking east. 'How long do we have?' she asks. You don't have an answer. The sound of the undead horde is just audible on the wind."

---

**Award:** Milestone — **Level 10** here. The party stands at the height of their power, surrounded by every alliance they've built.

**Strategic Decisions:**
The party must assign allies to defensive roles. The Whispering Wall will hit Vallaki's western wall in approximately 4 hours. The undead horde hits the eastern wall in 3. The war council gives the party a window to strike Ravenloft — but only if the defenses hold.

> Lady Wachter: "You cannot be here when the walls break. You must be at Ravenloft destroying whatever is making this dark. Else none of this matters."

## Scene 2 — The Dark Powers' Dream
The party sleeps briefly before departing (or is pulled into the dream involuntarily):

> "You are in a white place — not light, but whiteness. No walls, no floor, no ceiling. From the white, a voice that has no body: smooth, old, patient."
>
> *"You have done well. Better than most. We are impressed, which is not a thing we say lightly. And so we offer you a gift. Give us Ireena Kolyana. Her soul — not her body, her soul — and we will end this. Strahd will sleep. Barovia will be freed. The fog will lift. All of it, tonight, without blood and without war, if you give us one soul."*

**What the Dark Powers are:** Ancient, amoral entities that created Barovia as a prison-domain. They imprisoned Strahd here. They are not allies. They are not enemies. They are collectors. They want Ireena because Tatyana's soul, reborn as Ireena, is the only thing in Barovia that is genuinely uncorrupted — genuinely good, genuinely her own. That rarity is the prize.

**Ireena's Response (she is present in the dream):**
> "No," she says immediately. She doesn't hesitate. She looks at the white. "You don't get to trade me for Barovia. I am not a price. I am a person." She reaches for the party's hands. "We do this ourselves."

**The Refusal:** The white goes cold. The voice says: *"Then the cost is yours."* The dream ends. **Strahd activates his full power.** The dark doubles. The undead horde accelerates. The Whispering Wall moves faster.

This is the point of no return.

---

## Scene 3 — The March to Ravenloft
> "The road to Ravenloft is empty of living things. Even the wolves are gone. The fog on either side seems to breathe. The castle is illuminated from within by a dull red light — the Heart of Sorrow — visible through every window."

**The Allies Hold the Line:** As the party approaches, ravens circle overhead — the Keepers of the Feather, watching, as they always have. One lands on Ezmerelda's shoulder. She looks at it and nods.

**The Bridge:** Still intact. Strahd wants them to come. He's ready.

**Rahadin at the Gate:** He stands at the threshold and simply steps aside. He looks at the party with something that might be respect. "The master is at the Heart," he says. "He is waiting for you. So am I." He does not move from the doorway. He will let them pass — and he will follow. He is Strahd's last line.

**Optional Rahadin Fight (if party wants to clear him):** CR 15. Hard but possible at Level 10. If they spend resources here: note it — the final fight requires resources.

---

## Scene 4 — The Heart of Sorrow
> "Deep in Castle Ravenloft, below the crypts, in a cavern that wasn't on any map: a sphere the size of a barn, pulsing deep crimson. It hangs in the center of a vast circular chamber, suspended by chains of wrought iron. Around it: faces. Hundreds of faces, pressed against the crystal's interior — Barovian faces, all with eyes closed, all breathing in a slow, terrible synchrony. Their souls are inside it. The Heart is *eating* them."

**What must be done:** Strike the Heart with the Sunsword — three times (legendary action economy — each strike is a separate round's action) — while defending against Strahd's attacks and the Heart's own defenses.

**Heart of Sorrow Defenses:**
- **Round 1:** 4 **vampire spawn** emerge from the crystal and attack
- **Round 2:** The Heart pulses outward — 30-ft radius, DC 16 Con, 4d10 psychic damage and stunned until end of next turn
- **Round 3:** Strahd arrives.

**The Sunsword's Effect:** Each strike deals 8d10 radiant damage to the Heart. On the third strike: the Heart cracks. The crimson light shatters. The faces inside exhale — and the sky outside begins to lighten.

---

## Scene 5 — Strahd von Zarovich: The Monster ⭐ FINAL BATTLE
> "He walks into the chamber as the Heart breaks. He looks at the shards on the floor. He looks at you. He is not angry. He is not dramatic. He is *done*."
>
> "'Four months,' he says. 'You have undone four hundred years of work in four months. That is — ' a pause that might be respect, 'remarkable.' He draws his blade. 'I will not insult you with mercy. End this.'"

**Strahd Final Stat Block Adjustments (Level 10 party):**
- Use official Strahd stat block from Curse of Strahd module (CR 15)
- **Heart of Sorrow destroyed:** Strahd cannot use Heart of Sorrow lair action
- **Three Fanes reconsecrated:** Strahd cannot regenerate from land energy; loses all lair actions
- **Sunsword wielded:** His regeneration is suppressed in the blade's light; he takes +2d8 radiant per hit
- He retains: legendary actions, legendary resistances (3), Misty Escape

**Strahd's Combat Personality — The Monster:**
- No speeches. No monologues. He fights with total focus.
- He prioritizes the Sunsword wielder — he *knows* what that blade means.
- He uses Charm Person on the PC he calculated is most likely to succeed.
- His last legendary resistance: he uses it to stay standing when the killing blow should drop him. He looks at the blade. He looks at the party. Something very old moves behind his eyes.

**The Final Blow:**
> "Strahd falls. Not dramatically — he falls the way very old trees fall, slowly, all at once. The sword of sunlight is still burning. His face, in the last moment, is not the Monster's face, not the Tyrant's, not even the Gentleman's. It is a face from three centuries ago — a face that loved someone once and made a catastrophic choice and has been paying for it ever since."
>
> "He looks at your party. His last words are barely a whisper. 'Tell Tatyana…' He does not finish the sentence. He dissolves."

---

## Scene 6 — The Dawn
> "Light."
>
> "Not the flat, grey light of a Barovian morning. *Light.* Warm, golden, coming from the east, pouring over the mountains. Through the castle windows — every window — sunlight. Real sunlight. For the first time in four hundred years."
>
> "In Vallaki, the fighting stops. The undead crumble as the sun touches them. The Whispering Wall stills and then fragments, and from the fragments: light, not darkness. Lady Wachter shields her eyes and stares east. Father Lucian Petrovich collapses to his knees in the town square, arms wide, face turned up to the sun."
>
> "In the Village of Barovia, Father Donavich — standing in the churchyard where Kolyan Indirovich is buried — watches the dawn come. 'He would have liked this,' he says to no one."
>
> "Castle Ravenloft rises. Slowly, impossibly, the entire castle lifts from its clifftop and drifts upward into the sky and simply… fades. As if the land itself is exhaling."
