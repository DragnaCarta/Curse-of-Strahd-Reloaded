# Month 4 · Week 3 — Arc T: The Three Fanes
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 21–22  
**Party Level:** 15 → 16 (award Level 16 after consecrating all three Fanes)  
**Location:** Yester Hill → Berez → Mountain Fane  
**Session Goal:** Reconsecrate all three Fanes; defeat Baba Lysaga; trigger Strahd's Heart of Sorrow response.

---

## Opening — Strahd's Monster Shift
> "After the Sunsword blazed to life in the Amber Temple, something changed. His visits stopped. His emissaries stopped. The silence that replaced them is worse. And then — a raven arrives from Vallaki, carrying a note in Lady Wachter's handwriting: 'Come back. Something is happening to the sky.'"

**Strahd has shifted to the Monster.** The warm politeness and the cold cruelty both stop. What's left is efficiency — the indifferent focus of a predator who has made his decision.

---

## Fane 1 — Forest Fane (Yester Hill)
The party returns to Yester Hill — scene of the druid battle in Month 3. Now the site is quiet. At the hill's peak, a stone circle with the Huntress's sigil carved into it.

> "The circle is old. Ancient. Older than Strahd, older than the druids who corrupted it. Standing here, you feel the forest itself paying attention."

**Planting the Spear:**
Drive the Spear of the Huntress into the earth at the center of the stone circle. No combat required — this is a ritual moment.

> "The spear drives into the earth and does not stop at bedrock — it sinks further, and from it, roots spread. Silver-green light traces along the carved sigil. The hill shudders. Every tree around the circle straightens slightly. The druids — those who survive — feel it: an old authority reclaiming what was taken. Two of them kneel."

**What changes:** Strahd loses his Forest lair actions (plant commands, vine grasps, beast telepathy within 1 mile of Yester Hill).

---

## Fane 2 — Swamp Fane (Berez) — Major Boss: Baba Lysaga
> "Berez has changed. The swamp feels *angry* — the trees lean inward, the water is black and still, and the skull-hut at its center has been joined by a ring of animated tree stumps that pulse with dark energy. Baba Lysaga knows you're coming. She has known for days."

**The approach:** Animated **tree blights** (10) form a perimeter. The skull-hut rises on roots. This is a full encounter.

**Baba Lysaga — Boss Fight:**
She rides the skull (her flying skull is a separate creature — attack it to force her to the ground):

**Skull Stats:**
- AC 12, HP 80
- **Bite (Action):** +8 to hit, 4d10+4 piercing
- If the skull is destroyed: Baba Lysaga falls 20 feet (2d6 bludgeoning) and lands prone

**Baba Lysaga Stats (modified from official):**
- AC 15 (natural), HP 114
- **Treeblight Summon (1/day):** Calls 1d4+1 vine blights
- **Madness (Action):** 30-ft radius, DC 15 Wis, target acts randomly on failed save (roll 1d4: 1=attack ally, 2=stand still, 3=flee, 4=nothing)
- **Strix Barrage (Bonus Action, Recharge 5-6):** Releases 3 strix (scarecrow-ravens) that attack one target each (treat as Scarecrow stat block)
- **Regeneration:** Heals 10 HP per turn while in contact with the swamp earth. If she's kept aloft or the ground is consecrated, this stops.
- **At 0 HP:** She dissolves into the swamp water, screaming: "MY SON WILL PUNISH YOU! *MOTHER* WILL NOT—" She is destroyed.

**After Baba Lysaga falls:**
The swamp immediately begins to settle — the water clears slightly, the trees stop pressing inward. The Fane marker is at the center of the swamp, under 4 feet of black water.

> "The Weaver's gem glows as the Martikovs — Davian and Urwin, who came despite everything — plant it at the marker's base. A feeling spreads from the Fane outward: warmth, almost. Like something that was cold for four centuries is remembering that it used to be alive."

**What changes:** Strahd loses his Swamp lair actions and his ability to scry from the swamp.

---

## The Martikov Moment:
Davian Martikov stands at the Fane's edge as the ritual completes. He looks at the gem sinking into the earth. He says: "My great-great-grandmother planted the original gem. She died defending this place from Strahd's first assault." He is silent for a moment. "It took us five generations to undo the damage. Three hundred years." He looks at the party. "Thank you." He walks away before his composure breaks.

---

## Fane 3 — Mountain Fane (Mt. Ghakis foot)
The Mountain Fane is accessible from Soldav — the mountain folk lead the party to it. It is at the base of the mountain's first peak, marked by a cairn with the Seeker's raven sigil.

Lady Fiona Wachter arrives, having ridden through the night. She brings her son Franz and daughter Stella — but Strahd's emissary has taken her eldest son Karl hostage in Vallaki.

**The Crisis:** Strahd's chamberlain holds Karl. Wachter must choose between her child and reconsecrating the Fane. The party must resolve this.

**Resolving Karl's Hostage situation:**
- Muriel Vinshaw: "My people can extract him. Give us an hour." (Keepers of the Feather operation — success on 2d6 + 4 vs. DC 12)
- Victor Vallakovich can cast *Misty Step* twice and reach the building Victor-fast (he loves any plan where he gets to do something impressive)
- Direct combat approach: Rahadin has 3 guards there; the party can overwhelm them before Strahd is contacted

**When Karl is safe:** Lady Wachter plants the Seeker's gem without a word. The Fane marker comes alive with raven imagery — carved ravens seem to turn their heads.

**What changes:** Strahd loses his Mountain lair actions and his intelligence network (the ravens that spy for him go silent).

---

## The Heart of Sorrow Response
Three Fanes reconsecrated. Strahd's land-bond severed.

> "You feel it from here — a tremor through the earth, not an earthquake, but something more fundamental. As if a great machine has had three of its gears removed. And then — from Castle Ravenloft, visible on the cliff from Soldav — a pulse of deep red light erupts. It spreads across the valley in a wave. Where the wave passes, the sky darkens. Not to night — to nothing. No stars. No moon. A total blackout."

**The Heart of Sorrow activates:** Strahd's backup plan — the Heart is a massive crystalline sphere deep in Castle Ravenloft, powered by harvested Barovian despair, that creates eternal darkness as a last resort.

**Vallaki is under attack:** An undead horde marches from the east. The Whispering Wall (the Devourer's corpse at Yester Hill) moves — it has uprooted itself and is drifting toward Vallaki's western wall. A raven reaches the party: *"It is beginning. Come back."*

**End of Act IV (Arcs R–T). Arc U begins next session.**
