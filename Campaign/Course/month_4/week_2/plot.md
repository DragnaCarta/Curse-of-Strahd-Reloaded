# Month 4 · Week 2 — Arc S: A Sword of Sunlight (The Amber Temple)
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 19–20  
**Party Level:** 14 → 15 (award Level 15 after empowering the Sunsword with Sergei)  
**Location:** Amber Temple (peak of Mount Ghakis)  
**Session Goal:** Navigate the Amber Temple, defeat Patrina Velikovna's spirit, retrieve the Sunsword hilt, and conjure Sergei von Zarovich to empower it. Decode the Tome of Strahd.

---

## Opening — Approach to the Amber Temple
> "The Temple appears through blowing snow — a fortress carved from amber-colored stone, its walls translucent in places, things visible within the amber that shouldn't still be moving. Runes line every surface. The air here tastes of electricity and old, old power. And underneath everything: a sound just below hearing, like a thousand voices all whispering different words."

**Kasimir goes pale.** He has been building to this moment for decades. His hands are shaking. "She's in there," he says. "She's been in there for three hundred years. I just need to find her."

**Warning:** The Temple's magical miasma causes a **Corruption Check** — every hour spent in the Temple requires a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or the character gains one level of *Corruption*: mild paranoia, dark impulses, craving for power. Track levels per player. Corruption resets with a long rest outside the Temple.

---

## Scene 1 — Amber Sarcophagi (The Vestiges)
> "Amber sarcophagi line the great hall, each one 10 feet tall, each one containing a dark shape — not a body, but a presence. You can feel them, the way you feel someone watching you from across a crowded room. As you walk past, each one whispers."

**The Vestiges' Offers (one per sarcophagus, choose 4–5):**
- *"Power beyond your mortal frame. I offer strength. Take it. What has your body cost you so far? I can take that pain away."*
- *"Knowledge. Every secret. Every plan. I know what Strahd intends. I can tell you. All I ask is... a taste."*
- *"Immortality. You have come so far. Would you like to guarantee the ending? I ask so little."*
- *"Vengeance. Against those who wronged you. I see their faces in your memory. I can show you the path."*

**Mechanic:** Accepting a vestige's offer grants a powerful dark gift — but also advances Corruption by 2 and changes the character's alignment one step toward Evil.

**The Vampyr's Sarcophagus:** One sarcophagus is different — sealed from the inside, radiating cold so intense frost forms on nearby amber. This is where Patrina hid the Sunsword hilt. The whisper from inside is familiar: *"She is gone. She left something behind. Come in. I won't hurt you. I've been alone for so long."*

This is the Vampyr — the entity that offered Strahd his vampirism. It WILL hurt them. But the Sunsword is inside.

---

## Scene 2 — Exethanter (Ghost Archmage)
> "A translucent figure in archaic robes drifts down a corridor toward you. He stops when he sees you. He looks confused. 'You are new,' he says. 'Have I met you? I cannot — I cannot quite —' He presses a hand to his head. 'My name was Exethanter. I was the master here. Before the betrayal. Before…' He fades in and out. 'What year is it?'"

**Exethanter** is the ghost of the temple's former archmage lich — betrayed by his apprentice Vilnius, who stole his phylactery and shattered it, reducing him to a ghost without memory.

**Restoring his memory:** If the party finds Vilnius's old journal (in the temple's library, DC 15 Investigation) and reads it aloud to Exethanter: his eyes clear. The memories return. He weeps — for his students, for what the temple became, for centuries of empty haunting.

**What Exethanter knows:**
- The location of every vestige in the temple
- How to safely retrieve the Sunsword hilt from the Vampyr's sarcophagus (it requires a circle of protection vs. evil — he can draw it)
- How to decode the **Tome of Strahd** (if the party has it): "I know this cipher. I taught it to him. To Strahd, when he was young and clever and came here to study. Before he made his bargain." He can decode the Tome if given an hour.

**The Tome's Secret (Decoded):**
The Tome reveals Strahd's full plan — the Grand Conjunction. For the first time, the party learns: Strahd needs **Arabelle** (the child of divine Seeker blood) as a control mechanism. He needs to harvest the souls of all Barovians. He needs Rahadin to die as a sacrifice. And he needs the Fanes to power the gate.

*This is the party's "now we understand the stakes" moment.* They were always heroes. Now they understand what exactly they're preventing.

---

## Scene 3 — Neferon's Interference
**Neferon**, an **arcanaloth** disguised as a helpful wizard named "Mordenkainen's old apprentice," has been in the temple for months, working to obtain vestige power.

> "He finds the party just as they're approaching the Vampyr's sarcophagus. 'Oh, don't touch that one,' he says pleasantly. 'I've claimed that one. Work arrangement. You understand.' His illusion slips for just a moment — yellow eyes, parchment skin. Then it's back. 'We could work together, you know.'"

**Neferon Fight:** He turns hostile if denied. He uses *Teleport* to escape at below 30 HP but drops his disguise ring — revealing his true nature. This confirms he's been manipulating temple visitors for some time.

---

## Scene 4 — Patrina Velikovna (Boss Fight)
With Exethanter's protection circle drawn around the Vampyr's sarcophagus, Patrina's spirit is forced out of it:

> "She is beautiful and terrible. Three centuries of rage, regret, and isolation have not softened her — they have refined her, like iron into steel. She looks at Kasimir and something crosses her face: love and fury in equal measure. 'Brother,' she says. 'You should not have come.'"

**Kasimir breaks:** He steps forward. "Patrina. I came to bring you home." She stares at him. "You came to watch me die *again*." She raises her hands.

**Patrina — Archmage Ghost Stat Block (modified):**
- She fights to kill (she is furious at the violation of her sanctuary)
- Kasimir will throw himself in the way of attacks against Patrina — the party must pull him back
- At 30 HP: Patrina pauses. She looks at Kasimir. "It hurts," she says. "It still hurts." A moment of clarity. "Take it," she says, and dissolves — not destroyed, but withdrawing. The Sunsword hilt drops from the sarcophagus.

**Note:** Patrina is not fully destroyed — she withdraws deeper into the Amber Temple. Kasimir can still attempt to resurrect her (this is a long-term arc that the DM can continue after the main campaign).

---

## Scene 5 — Conjuring Sergei von Zarovich
> "The Sunsword hilt is cold in your hand. Exethanter says: 'The blade requires its soul — Sergei von Zarovich, the murdered prince. He wanders the Mists between worlds. A circle of summoning will call him. But he must come willingly. And it will not be painless for him.'"

**Sergei's Appearance:**
A young man, early 20s, translucent and faintly golden. He is Strahd's younger brother — the one Strahd murdered to make his bargain with the Dark Powers. He looks at the party with eyes like morning light.

> "'I have been waiting,' he says, 'for someone brave enough to ask. Not for the sword — for me. To acknowledge what was taken from me.' He looks at the hilt. 'Will you use this to free Barovia? Will you use it for vengeance?' He waits. The answer matters."

**The Right Answer:** Not "to kill Strahd" (which is vengeance). The answer that calls the blade is: *to free Barovia* / *to end the suffering* / *to restore the light.* Any sincere version of this answer works.

When the answer is given: the hilt blazes. A blade of pure sunlight extends from it. **The Sunsword is complete.** It sheds bright sunlight in a 15-foot radius (negating vampire regeneration and darkness spells). Against undead: +2d8 radiant bonus damage.

> Sergei smiles. "Tell my brother I forgive him. Whatever good that does either of us." He fades.

**Award:** Level 9 awarded at the end of this scene.
