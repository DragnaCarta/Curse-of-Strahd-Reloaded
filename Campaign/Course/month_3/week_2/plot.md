# Month 3 · Week 2 — Arc K: The Fallen Abbey + Arc L: The Den of Wolves
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 11–12  
**Party Level:** 10 → 11 (award Level 11 after recovering the Holy Symbol from the werewolf den)  
**Location:** Krezk → Abbey of Saint Markovia → Werewolf Den (Lake Baratok)  
**Session Goal:** Investigate the Abbey fully; meet Ezmerelda; clear the werewolf den and rescue Emil Toranescu.

---

## Scene 1 — The Abbey of Saint Markovia (Full Exploration)
> "The abbey is quiet in the way that empty places are quiet — not peaceful, but absent. The courtyard holds the mongrelfolk, the Abbot's failed experiments. Some have animal features, some have too many limbs, some have eyes where there shouldn't be eyes. They watch the party with expressions ranging from frightened to fascinated to blank."

**The Mongrelfolk:** These are humans corrupted by the Abbot's well-intentioned but catastrophically misguided healing experiments. He was trying to repair their injuries using spare parts. They do not hate him — most of them adore him in a way that is heartbreaking.

**Key Mongrelfolk:**
- **Cyrus Belview** — The Belview clan's eldest, who has too many features and a surprisingly intact mind. He has opinions. "The Abbot means well. The road to the Abyss is paved with good meaning, is it not?"
- **Otto and Zygfrek** — Brothers, sharing a single intelligible sentence between them but communicating volumes through gesture.

---

## Scene 2 — Vasilka
> "In a room on the upper floor, a figure stands at a window looking out over Krezk. She is beautiful — heartbreakingly, wrongly beautiful, like a sculpture given motion. She turns when you enter. Her eyes are kind and empty simultaneously. 'Hello,' she says. 'I am Vasilka. The Abbot made me. I am going to be given to the master, and then everything will be better. This is what I am told.' She pauses. 'I am not sure I understand what being given means. But I trust the Abbot.'"

**Vasilka as a Character:**
- She has no memories, no past. She is aware she was assembled from parts.
- She is curious about everything and has been told only what the Abbot wanted her to know.
- If the party speaks to her honestly — explains what Strahd actually is — she absorbs it without reaction. "I see. Then perhaps being given to him is not the kindness I was told." She is not distressed. She simply updates her understanding.
- She is NOT the villain here. She is a victim of the Abbot's delusion.

---

## Scene 3 — Confronting the Abbot (Arc K Core)
> "The Abbot listens to the party's concerns about Vasilka with the patience of someone who has been waiting for this conversation. 'I have lived here for two hundred years,' he says. 'I have watched Strahd destroy everything this valley has tried to build. A bride — a perfect, uncomplaining bride — is the only thing that has ever satisfied a man like him. I have tried prayer. I have tried patience. I tried hope. Hope died with the forty-seventh generation of Barovians I watched be born and die without ever seeing the sun.' He looks at Vasilka with something that is almost parental love. 'This is the kindest thing I can do.'"

**The Abbot's Tragedy:** He is a celestial being who has looked at a problem for 200 years and concluded that collaboration with evil is the only path to good. He is wrong. But his wrongness comes from genuine exhaustion and genuine loss of faith, not malice.

**How to Redeem Him:**
The Abbot can be redeemed, but only by presenting him with the **Icon of Ravenloft** (from Castle Ravenloft's crypts — the heist in Week 3). If the party does this, the Icon's radiance cuts through his corruption and reconnects him to his celestial nature. He weeps. He destroys Vasilka himself (gently). He becomes an ally.

**Without the Icon:** The Abbot becomes hostile when the players push back hard enough. As a fallen deva, he is a formidable enemy — hold this fight for Arc Q (Week 4) when the party is Level 7.

**Ezmerelda's Arrival:**
As the conversation with the Abbot reaches its most tense point, a loud crash from the abbey's courtyard. A woman with a prosthetic leg has just broken in through a second-story window, dispatched two of the Abbot's guard mongrelfolk, and is now looking at the party with a crossbow raised and the expression of someone who has walked in on an unusual situation.

> "I was going to ask who you are," she says, not lowering the crossbow, "but you're clearly not Strahd's people and you're clearly not dead yet, which puts you ahead of most visitors to this abbey. I'm Ezmerelda d'Avenir. I'm a monster hunter. I'm looking for Dr. Van Richten. And you look like the people Madam Eva told me to find."

**Ezmerelda d'Avenir:**
- Vistana. Former apprentice of Van Richten. Prosthetic leg that she maintains herself (it has a silver hollow-point for silver weapons).
- Sharp, warm, resourceful. She's been hunting on her own since Van Richten's curse made it dangerous to travel with him.
- She IS the foretold ally from the Tarokka reading (if that was her card). She joins the party.
- She has her own wagon north of Krezk with supplies and a wooden stake collection.

**Award:** Milestone — **Level 6** at the end of this scene. Ezmerelda's arrival is a narrative landmark.

## Scene 4 — The Den of Wolves (Arc L)
With Ezmerelda's help, the party locates the werewolf den near Lake Baratok — a series of caverns under a rocky promontory.

**Zuleika Toranescu** — the priestess of Mother Night — meets them at the den's entrance:
> "I knew you would come. Mother Night showed me. My mate Emil is in Ravenloft's dungeons. Strahd put him there to punish our pack for resisting. I cannot free him alone. If you bring him home — if you bring him home — the Holy Symbol of Ravenkind is yours. Our people will pledge to stay neutral in whatever war is coming."

**The Den's Layout:**
- Outer caverns: 4 **werewolf** guards (Kiril's loyalists)
- Inner hall: 3 more guards + their pack's communal space (women, children, non-combatants — the party should note and avoid harming them)
- Kiril Stoyanovich is NOT here — he's out on patrol (sets up Arc Q)

**Combat:** Clear the guard werewolves. The fight is manageable at Level 6 — 4 + 3 = 7 werewolves, but they are defending, not coordinating, and Ezmerelda is a significant addition to the party's firepower.

**The Deal Made:** Zuleika formally agrees to the exchange. She gives the party a silver token — "Show this to the guards at Ravenloft's dungeons. There is one guard who will recognize it. He is not Strahd's man."
