# Month 3 · Week 4 — Arc P: Ravenloft Heist + Arc Q: A Shining Beacon
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 14–15  
**Party Level:** 12 → 13 (award Level 13 after completing the full Ravenloft heist + lighting the Beacon)  
**Location:** Castle Ravenloft → Abbey → Argynvostholt  
**Session Goal:** Complete the Ravenloft heist (5 objectives); cash in rewards; light Argynvost's beacon. Strahd shifts to the Tyrant.

---

## Opening — The New Moon Night
> "Award Level 7 before the session begins."
> "The bridge is repaired at dawn. Strahd's coach departs an hour after sunset — you watch from a tower window as it vanishes into the fog below the cliff. Rahadin watches from the courtyard as the coach goes. Then he returns inside. He knows exactly what you are considering. His face says: try it."

The party has Sasha's key, knowledge of the layout from Varushka's dream, and one night with Strahd absent.

---

## Scene 1 — The Hall of Bones (Argynvost's Skull)
> "The Hall of Bones is a long gallery of trophies — the remains of everything Strahd has ever conquered. Humanoid skulls line shelves. Weapon displays of the Order of the Silver Dragon fill one wall. And at the hall's far end, mounted on an iron stand like a hunting trophy: a skull the size of a carriage. Dragon skull. Silver dragon skull. Even bare of flesh it radiates something — a quality, a presence. Power, or the memory of power."

**Guards:** 2 **vampire spawn** on patrol. Stealth DC 14 to bypass them. Fight if detected — they're manageable at Level 7.

**Recovering the skull:** It weighs 200 lbs. Solutions: Reduce spell, creative thinking (Leomund's Tiny Hut trick), wereraven courier from Muriel, or Ezmerelda's wagon parked outside. Let the players figure it out — reward creativity.

---

## Scene 2 — St. Markovia's Crypt (Icon of Ravenloft)
> "The crypt level is cold and silent. Stone coffins in alcoves, the name plates worn smooth. At the far end: a separate chamber, newer than the rest. The door bears the symbol of the Morninglord — and it has been magically sealed, as if to keep something in. Or keep something out."

**The Seal:** DC 16 Arcana to identify it as a ward against evil — meaning undead cannot enter. The Icon is inside: a golden sunburst on a chain, radiating faint warmth even after centuries.

**Gertruda:** In a nearby cell (not a crypt), a young woman sits on a pallet with a book open in her lap. She is pale, tired, but intact. She looks up when the door opens.

> "'You're not Strahd's,' she says immediately. Then: 'You're alive.' She closes the book. 'I've been here three weeks. I came willingly — I wanted to find Doru. I found him. Strahd found me. He hasn't hurt me. He's mostly… ignored me. I think he finds me tiresome.' She stands. 'I would like to go home now.'"

**Gertruda's Situation:** Strahd kept her alive partly as a message to the village, partly out of an odd boredom. Escorting her out is its own logistical challenge.

---

## Scene 3 — The Dungeons (Emil Toranescu + Spear of the Huntress)
> "The dungeon level smells of stone and old fear. Iron doors line the corridor. From one: the sound of shallow breathing."

**Emil Toranescu:** A large man with wolf-gold eyes, chained to the wall. He is thin from deprivation but not broken.
> "Zuleika sent you. I knew she would." He holds up his chained wrists. "These chains are silver. I can't transform to break them. You'll need the key — the guard has it."

**The Sympathetic Guard:** Show him the silver token from Zuleika. He looks at it, looks at the party, and looks away. He slides the key under the cell door. "I was never here," he says. "My family lives in Krezk. If you get out of here — tell them I did the right thing."

**King Dostron's Crypt (the Spear):** Two rooms further, an ancient crypt containing the preserved body of a pre-Barovian king. In his stone hands: **the Spear of the Huntress**, a silver-headed weapon crackling with faint green energy. It radiates ancient, wild magic.

Taking it: the king's body crumbles to dust. The room fills with a smell of pine and rain and the feeling of open wilderness. The Huntress approves.

---

## Scene 4 — The Escape
With objectives complete, the party must exit before Strahd returns at dawn.

**Complications (roll or choose):**
- Volenta is still in the castle. She finds the party in the hall and *screams* with delight. If not dealt with quickly, she wakes the entire castle.
- Anastrasya stops the party in a corridor. She is not hostile — she is evaluating. "You have done this before," she says. "Or you will. Time is strange here." She steps aside. She files this information away for later.
- Rahadin knows the party is raiding. He allows it. He is testing them. If they try to fight him: he is CR 15. This is not a fight they win. Run.

**The Exit:** Dawn is breaking. The last party member crosses the bridge as the castle's bells ring — Strahd has returned. From far below the cliff, they hear his coach. A long silence. Then his voice, very calm, from somewhere inside the castle: *"How interesting."*

## Scene 5 — Paying Off the Rewards
**Day 15 — Rapid Resolution:**

**The Abbot + Icon of Ravenloft:**
> "The Abbot holds the Icon for a long time. Then the golden light intensifies — pouring into him, through him, stripping away the grey calcification of two centuries. He falls to his knees. When he looks up, his eyes are clear. 'What have I done,' he says. Not a question. 'What have I *done*.'"
He destroys Vasilka gently and with grief. He becomes a true healer again and will aid the party going forward.

**Zuleika + Emil:**
Reunion at the werewolf den. Zuleika gives the party the **Holy Symbol of Ravenkind** — a golden sunburst amulet that radiates genuine radiant light. Emil takes his place back as pack alpha.

**Argynvost's Skull → Argynvostholt:**
Return the skull to the mausoleum. Sir Godfrey and the revenants gather. In silence, the dragon's skull settles into its resting place. A tremor goes through the manor.

---

## Scene 6 — Lighting the Beacon
> "The beacon at Argynvostholt's peak has been dark for 300 years. The mechanism is simple — ancient oil that has somehow never dried. Someone must climb the tower and light it."

When the beacon lights:
> "Silver fire rises from the peak of Argynvostholt. The light cuts through Barovia's permanent overcast — not sunlight, not warmth, but *hope*. It is visible from every settlement in the valley. In Vallaki, people stop and stare. In Krezk, the Krezkov children run to the walls. In the Village of Barovia, Father Donavich looks up from his prayers and weeps."

**Vladimir Horngaard's Death:** The ancient revenant commander's hatred finally finds its release. He screams — a three-century scream — and collapses to dust. The Order is at peace. The remaining revenants kneel, then dissolve into light.

**Strahd's Response:** That night, a black storm rolls over the valley from Ravenloft. Lightning. Thunder. The voice of Strahd, carried on the wind: *"Enough. You want a war. Very well."*

**Strahd shifts to the Tyrant.** The Gentleman is gone.

**Award:** Milestone — **Level 7** (already awarded at session start). Act III is complete.
