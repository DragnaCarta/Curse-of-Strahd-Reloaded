# Month 3 · Week 1 — Arc J: The Stolen Gem + Arc K: The Fallen Abbey (Opening)
**In-Game Date:** Neyavr 9–10  
**Party Level:** 9 → 10 (award Level 10 after reclaiming the winery + Yester Hill)  
**Location:** Krezk → Wizard of Wines → Yester Hill  
**Session Goal:** Liberate the Wizard of Wines winery from druids; stop the Wintersplinter ritual at Yester Hill; first visit to the Abbey of Saint Markovia.

---

## Opening — Krezk and the Abbey
> "Morning in Krezk. The village is quiet, its people going about lives that have been pared down to the essentials — food, shelter, survival. Above the town, the Abbey of Saint Markovia crouches on a clifftop. In daylight it looks less ominous — just old, and grey, and immense."

**Brief Abbey Introduction:**
The party can briefly visit the Abbey before heading to the Wizard of Wines. This introduces the Abbot and plants the hook.

**The Abbot** meets them at the gate:
> "A man — if that is the word — opens the abbey gate. He is too beautiful. Not attractively-human beautiful but geometrically, abstractly beautiful, like someone built a person according to mathematics and forgot to add imperfection. His eyes are the color of old amber. He smiles and it's almost right. 'Travelers. I am the Abbot. This is a house of healing. What do you need?'"

The Abbot is a **deva** (celestial) who has been living in isolation for 200 years and gone quietly mad from it. He is building a flesh-golem bride named **Vasilka** for Strahd — convinced that a perfect bride will satisfy Strahd and end Barovia's curse. He is wrong. He means well. That combination is very dangerous.

**Plant the hook:** "There is something I require for my work. An item from Castle Ravenloft's crypts — the Icon of Ravenloft, consecrated to Saint Markovia. It was taken during the siege. If you should visit the castle..." He lets the request hang. The party should note this for the Ravenloft heist.

---

## Scene 1 — The Wizard of Wines: Occupied
> "The Wizard of Wines winery sits in a broad valley — a place that should smell of fermentation and autumn, but instead smells of rot and green sap. The vineyard's rows are trampled. Figures move between the buildings — robed in brown and green, wearing bark-and-leaf masks. Druids. And among them: shapes that aren't quite trees. Aren't quite anything."

**The Situation:** Forest druids — First Folk who have been corrupted by the Whispering Wall's dark energy — have occupied the winery and stolen one of three magical life-gems (enchanted seeds used to maintain the vineyard's supernatural growth). A larger ritual is underway at **Yester Hill** to the south.

**Urwin Martikov** arrives breathlessly at the winery's fence line when he sees the party (he's been watching from the tree line). He explains the situation: "The winery is how we feed half of Barovia. Without the wine — wine is the only trade the valley has. My father Davian is inside with the workers. Please."

**Combat: Liberating the Winery**
- 6 **vine blight** + 4 **needle blight** + 3 **druid** stat blocks (Medium CR)
- The druids do not negotiate — they are in a ritual fugue state, chanting continuously
- The ritual they're maintaining is feeding power to **Yester Hill** — if the party kills enough druids to break the ritual, a pulse of dark energy erupts from the south (visible: a black lightning bolt striking Yester Hill)

**Davian Martikov** — grizzled, proud, difficult. He thanks the party tersely and then immediately argues with his son about some long-standing dispute. The Martikovs are a family. They fight like one.

**The winery's three gems:** One is already missing (stolen to Yester Hill). Two remain embedded in the vineyard's earth. Davian explains: "Without all three, the vineyard weakens over years. Not overnight — but it will fail."

---

## Scene 2 — Yester Hill
> "The hill is a dead thing — black grass, black stones, and at its peak a 30-foot effigy of a giant woven from deadwood. Around the base, druids circle in a slow chant. The effigy is not empty. Something is forming inside it — something made of wood and anger and old dark power. It hasn't finished forming. Yet."

**Yester Hill Assault:**
- The ritual must be stopped before the effigy completes formation — a **Wintersplinter** is being born
- 8 druids (all in ritual, disadvantage on Perception) plus 2 **berserker** guards
- Each round the ritual continues: Wintersplinter's HP total increases by 10 (it starts at 0; at 114 it wakes)
- Party has approximately 5 rounds to stop it before it activates

**Wintersplinter (if awakened):**
- **Treant statblock** with modifications: it focuses attacks on structures, not adventurers (it wants to destroy the winery)
- If awakened, it marches toward the Wizard of Wines — the party has 3 rounds to fell it before it reaches the vineyard

**The Stolen Life-Gem:** At the ritual's center, embedded in the effigy's chest. Retrieving it requires a DC 14 Athletics check while the effigy is burning/collapsing (1d10 fire damage to retrieve it, Dex DC 12 for half).

**Muriel's Full Reveal:** Muriel Vinshaw, who has been traveling with the party, transforms at a critical moment and *flies* above the hill, drawing druid attention. When the fight is over: Davian Martikov stares at her. "Muriel Vinshaw. I thought you were dead." A beat. "You should have come home." She says nothing. The Keepers of the Feather (wereraven spy network) are larger than the party realized.

---

## Scene 3 — The Keepers of the Feather
Back at the winery, Davian gathers his family. The Martikovs reveal their nature: all wereravens, all members of the Keepers of the Feather, a network of spies and protectors that has been quietly working against Strahd for generations.

> "We can't fight him openly. We've never been able to. But we watch. We carry messages. We keep people alive who would otherwise die." Davian speaks with the weight of someone who has buried too many people. "You are the first party in decades that has survived long enough for me to trust."

**What the Keepers offer:** Intelligence, safe passage, a communication network (leave a note with any raven, addressed to "DM" — Davian Martikov — and they will deliver it).

**The Three Gems and the Fanes:** Davian knows the old lore — his family has kept it for generations. He explains, for the first time in full, what the three Fanes are and why they matter. "The gems must be planted at the Fanes. We must reconsecrate them. But to reach the Swamp Fane at Berez — Baba Lysaga guards it. We can't move without dealing with her."

**This is the Act IV setup being planted now — give the players a long-term picture.**

---

## Closing Beat — Return to Krezk
The party returns to Krezk that evening. Urwin embraces his father briefly, which both of them pretend didn't happen. The third gem is restored to the earth and the vineyard's sickly grey-green starts to fade back toward health.

The abbey light burns above Krezk. Vasilka (the Abbot's flesh-golem project) can be seen through an abbey window — a human shape, too still, too perfect.

**Transition hook for next week:** News arrives via raven from Vallaki — Strahd's chamberlain, **Rahadin**, has personally delivered invitations to the Blue Water Inn. "The master of Castle Ravenloft requests the pleasure of your company for dinner." The date: three days hence.
