# Death & Resurrection Rules — Barovia
> The Dark Powers do not release what they hold. But Barovia keeps its own rules.

---

## Core Rule — No Standard Resurrection

The following spells **do not function** in Barovia:
- Revivify
- Raise Dead
- Resurrection
- True Resurrection
- Reincarnate

Any such spell cast fizzles. The spell slot is spent. The body stays cold. The Dark Powers intercept every soul that dies within the Mists — it does not reach any deity, any afterlife, or any beyond.

**Tell players this at Session Zero.** It is not a surprise twist. It is a setting rule they should know going in.

---

## Death Is Real From Session 1

There is no protection. Death House earns its name. If a character hits 0 HP and fails three death saves — or takes damage that drops them to 0 HP from an instant-kill source — they are dead. The Dark Powers take their soul immediately.

---

## When a Character Dies

### Immediate Steps (at the table)

1. **Describe the death cinematically** — make it mean something
2. **Ask the player privately:** "Do you want to stay with this character as a spirit, or make someone new?"
3. **If spirit:** Copy `Campaign/Course/templates/spirit_character.md`, rename it `spirit_[name].md`, fill in the Tether section with the player *right now* (5 minutes)
4. **If new character:** New character enters within 15 minutes of real time, at full party level

### The Body

Immediately note in `notes.md`:
- Where the body is
- Whether it is protected, burned, or exposed

**If exposed and unprotected:** Strahd's ravens observe within 1d4 hours. He will claim the body at a dramatically appropriate moment — not immediately. He is patient. He saves it.

## Spirit Rules Summary

Full rules in `Campaign/Course/templates/spirit_character.md`.

**Quick reference:**
- Spirit levels up with the party at every milestone
- Retains INT/WIS/CHA and all mental class features
- Can possess willing party members (once per short rest)
- Can distract enemies (DC 12 Wis save or disadvantage)
- Has Spirit HP (level × 4), recovers on rests
- Fades after ~5 sessions if tether unresolved — this is a meaningful story ending, not a punishment

## Mass Death Protocol
> What happens if 3 or more party members die in the same session?

### Step 1 — Stop the Session

Do not try to continue. Call a 10-minute break. Let everyone decompress.

### Step 2 — Assess What Happened

**Was it a fair fight?** (Bad dice, good tactics, fair encounter)
→ Honor it. Proceed to Step 3.

**Was it a TPK from an encounter that was too hard?** (Death House twins, random bad luck, Level 1 against something they shouldn't have fought)
→ Use the Dark Powers Intervention below.

**Was it a player choice?** (They split the party, ignored warnings, picked the fight)
→ Honor it. Consequences are the point. Proceed to Step 3.

### Step 3 — The Dark Powers Intervention (Soft TPK Reset)

If the wipe was not a deliberate story beat — if it was just bad luck at a bad time — the Dark Powers intervene. Not out of mercy. Out of appetite.

> *The grey void. All of you, together, standing in fog so thick you can't see past your own hands. A voice — not a sound, more like a pressure — says one thing:*
>
> *"Not yet."*
>
> *You wake on the road outside the house/dungeon/location. It is one hour earlier than when you died. Your wounds are real. Your memory of dying is real. Whatever killed you is still in there.*
>
> *The Mists gave you back. You don't know why. You should find out.*

**Mechanical result:**
- Everyone wakes at 1 HP with one level of Exhaustion
- They remember dying — fully, clearly
- The threat is still present and unchanged
- They have one hour of in-game time and the knowledge of exactly what killed them

This is a second chance that feels like a second chance — not a retcon, not a fudge. The Dark Powers wanted them alive a little longer. That should be more frightening than dying.

**Use this once per campaign, maximum.** It loses all impact if it becomes a pattern.

### Step 4 — Full TPK (The Whole Party Is Gone)

If the entire party dies and the Dark Powers Intervention has already been used — or if this was a deliberate story moment — the campaign ends here.

**But it doesn't have to end badly.**

Options:
1. **Timeskip:** A new party, 10 years later, finds evidence of the first party's time in Barovia — notes, items, a spirit or two still haunting locations. They are the inheritors of the first party's unfinished work. The campaign continues with new characters who carry the weight of what the first party tried to do.

2. **Strahd Wins (For Now):** The Grand Conjunction completes. Barovia changes. Run a one-shot epilogue showing what Strahd's victory looks like — then roll new characters who wake up in a Barovia that has fundamentally shifted. The first party's deaths matter. The world reflects them.

3. **The Honest Conversation:** Step out of the fiction and ask the table: do we want to continue? If yes — new party, same world, same stakes. If no — that's okay too. Some campaigns end in tragedy. That's a real ending.

## Preventing Mass Death — DM Tools

**Before it happens:**

| Tool | When to Use |
|------|------------|
| Telegraph danger loudly | Before a potentially lethal encounter, describe the threat's scale clearly — let players choose to engage |
| Give an exit | Every combat should have a visible retreat option |
| Use minions first | Lead with weaker enemies so players can assess the threat before the boss hits |
| The 50% rule | If a boss NPC reaches 50% HP, have them offer terms, flee, or change tactics — full fights to the death are the exception |

**During a bad fight:**

| Tool | When to Use |
|------|------------|
| Strahd intervenes | He stops the fight — not to save them, but because he finds the creature/situation tiresome. He is not rescuing them. He just happens to be present. |
| Reinforcements arrive | Muriel, Ezmerelda, Van Richten, or another ally shows up at a dramatically convenient moment |
| The enemy has a goal | Most of Strahd's agents want to capture, not kill — have them switch to subdual damage when the party is losing badly |
