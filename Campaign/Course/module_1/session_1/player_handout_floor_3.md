## What they fought on Floor 2 (the “nursemaid”) — DM clarity

**It was not a wight.** A wight is a different monster (life drain, hates sunlight, commands undead). Your table may have called it a ghoul because it **felt** like one — claws, hunger, wrongness.

**What it actually was (your campaign):** a **dark mimic** — the house’s magic wearing **Miss Klara’s shape**. A **guardian** built from her suffering, forced to watch the nursery. When it “died,” **the body dissolved** into black ichor; only the **locket** was real. That’s how they know something was **off** even if they don’t have the full truth yet.

**What characters know:** They killed a **monster that looked like the nursemaid**. **Rose** said her name was **Klara**. They have clues about **Walter**, **Elisabeth**, and the **amber** piece. They have **not** necessarily been told “dark mimic” — you can describe it as: *“You’ve seen undead before in stories — this left no corpse. The house was wearing her face.”*

**If a player says “we killed the ghoul”:** *“Fair — it clawed like one. But ghouls leave meat. This left a stain.”*

## Floor 3 — what they see (matches the map)

Share **`images/maps/death-house/floor-3-balcony.svg`** on a screen or tablet when they reach the landing.

**Layout in plain language:**

1. **Stairs** end at a **landing** that becomes a **wide balcony**.
2. The **middle of the floor is a hole**: the **stairwell** continues **straight down** to the ground floor — **three stories**, railing around the edge. **Do not step back without looking.**
3. **Library** — open **archway** to one side; shelves, desk, air of dust and ink.
4. **Conservatory** — **closed door** on the other side; a **harpsichord** drones **one note**, over and over.
5. **Four suits of plate** stand like **sentries**: two near the library arch, one by the conservatory door, one along the **outer** bend of the balcony. **Every visor** is turned slightly **toward the stairs**.
