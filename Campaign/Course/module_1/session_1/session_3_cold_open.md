> Last session ended in the **Floor 3 Library**, party at **Level 3**, injured. Rose & Thorn's skeletons were revealed in the toy chest upstairs but the beat got eaten by combat. Scott is at 2 HP. Long rest is BLOCKED.

## Beat 1 — "Previously on" (paraphrase aloud, ~75 seconds)

> Two weeks ago Daggerford. Highharvesttide. Fog took the road. Two children — Rose and Thorn — led you to **16 Durst Lane**. You found Gustav's letter, the wrongness on every floor, the thing wearing the nursemaid's face that wasn't her. You found **Elisabeth's amber shard**, an **iron key**, and a wooden disc carved with **Walter**.
>
> Last week — only three of you went up. **Michael, Ben, Scott.** Floor 3 was teeth: two animated suits of plate woke up and tried to throw you over the railing. You put them down. Then the conservatory: a harpsichord, a vision — Miss Klara dancing with Mr. Durst, Elisabeth watching from a corner with the amber pendant in her hand — and then **Elisabeth turned**. Scott took the worst of it. He's at **two hit points**. He has been at two hit points since the conservatory.
>
> You found a secret door in the library wall. You pushed. It opened *up* — into the attic. The toy chest was there. You opened the toy chest. **Centipedes**. Hundreds. They poured over you, drove you back down to the library — and then they came **through the ceiling**. Scott burned a lightning-wave through them and they were gone.
>
> Beneath the dead centipedes, in the chest you'd left wide open upstairs: **two small skeletons. A girl. A younger boy. Curled together.**
>
> That was last week. You took the paintings off the attic walls — Ben — and you carried them down. You leveled. You **didn't rest**. The house doesn't permit it.
>
> Tonight — the iron groans somewhere downstairs.

## Beat 2 — Cold Open (read aloud)

> The library is cold the way deep water is cold. Books slumped where the centipedes broke through the ceiling. Plaster dust on every shoulder. The harpsichord, two rooms away, has been quiet since yesterday — for the first time since you got here, the **house itself** is quiet.
>
> Michael — your gauntlet has been clenched closed for **a long time**. You don't remember when you closed it. You open it now, slowly. Inside, the **amber shard** catches no light it should. It's warm.
>
> Scott — your back is against a bookshelf. You haven't moved. The corridor behind the library wall has been making a sound for the last ten minutes — not a voice; closer to *swallowing*. You stand up. You hand Michael the shard. You say, *"It's not for me. It was never for me. Walter was a name and I'm just a mouth. Find Jake. Give it to him."*
>
> Then the bookshelf at your back **opens.** Not the library secret door — a different one. Behind Scott: a corridor that wasn't there a minute ago, lined with mirrors, every mirror reflecting a different room. Scott steps in.
>
> *(beat)*
>
> The bookshelf grinds closed. Scott is gone. The harpsichord, two rooms away, plays one note. Then nothing.
>
> *(beat)*
>
> And then — *from the staircase below* — footsteps. Two sets. Familiar.
>
> **Jake. Jason.** You climb the stairs. The iron seal that took you yesterday is just **gone** — like it had never been there. Rose stands in the hall, holding Thorn's hand. She watches you climb. She says, quietly, *"He went the wrong way. Like our father did."*

## Beat 4 — Healing Window (CRITICAL — do not skip)

The party is at **Level 3** but **injured** and **cannot long rest**. Before any push to the attic or basement, give them a deliberate healing window in the library. This is the antidote to last week's "no recovery, just take more hits" feeling.

**What's available:**

| Resource | Owner | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Lay on Hands** | Michael | Pool of **15 HP** at L3 (5 × level). Confirm how much was spent S2; remaining is whatever's left. Touch a creature, restore HP up to remaining pool. Can also cure disease/poison (5 HP from pool). |
| **Cure Wounds** *(if prepared)* | Jason (Druid) | 1d8 + WIS per L1 slot. Has **2 L1 slots + 2 L2 slots** at L3. |
| **Goodberry** *(if prepared)* | Jason (Druid) | L1 slot → 10 berries, 1 HP each, lasts 24 hr. Massive value here — confirm if prepared. |
| **Healing Word** *(if prepared)* | Jason (Druid) | Bonus action, ranged 60 ft, 1d4 + WIS. |
| **Short rest** | Anyone | 1 hr. Spend Hit Dice (3 each at L3). Allow it in the library; the house lets you. The cost is *time* — see Pacing note below. |
| **Healing potions** | If found anywhere | None recorded; Death House's caches are sparse. |

**DM ruling for tonight:** Allow a short rest in the library. Narrate it as the house *listening*: the silence isn't peace, it's the house **watching them recover**, like a predator that's curious. The party recovers HP via Hit Dice; resources like spell slots stay spent (no long rest). Mike's bonus divine smite stays spent.

> *Read aloud at end of healing window:* "The shard in Jake's hand is warm now. The library is dim and the dust has settled. Nobody is bleeding anymore. The harpsichord has not played for an hour. Above you, the attic. The toy chest is still open up there, just the way you left it. Two small skeletons inside, beneath the dead centipedes."

Then the choice: push up to the attic, or descend and look for the basement another way.

## Beat 5 — Re-trigger the Rose Skeleton Reveal (when they go up)

When the party returns to the attic and approaches the toy chest, **stop**. Last week this beat got eaten by combat — give it 60 seconds tonight.

> *"You stand at the open toy chest. The centipedes are gone. The smell isn't. Beneath the dolls and the wooden blocks, two small skeletons — a girl, a younger boy — curled together. Her arm around him. Same clothes as Rose and Thorn. The same."*
>
> *Rose's form is in the doorway behind you. She has not followed you into the room. She is not flickering this time. She is very still.*
>
> *She says, in a voice that does not sound like a child anymore: 'That's us. We're in the chest. We're —' and then her face does something a child's face shouldn't, and she finishes: 'we're dead.'*
>
> *Thorn does not look up from his shoes. He says, 'I knew.'*

Let the table sit with it. **Don't fill the silence.**

If a PC offers to bury them, comfort them, take a knee — that's the table moment. Honor it. (Optional: this is the moment the players can offer Rose possession — read aloud the "Can I stay? Just for a little while?" beat from `plot.md` line 203.)
