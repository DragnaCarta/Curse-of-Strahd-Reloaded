# Strahd von Zarovich
**Type:** Vampire (CR 15)  
**Role:** Primary Villain  
**First Appears:** Month 1, Week 3 (River Ivlis Crossroads)

---

## Appearance
Tall, gaunt, aristocratic. Pale skin, dark hair slicked back, wearing black and deep burgundy. Eyes like a hawk — they take everything in and give nothing back. His smile is technically correct but doesn't reach his eyes. He is, visually, exactly what you'd expect. That's part of the horror.

## Three Personas

### The Gentleman (Act I–III)
*Trigger: Campaign start through beacon lighting*  
**Personality:** Polished curiosity. He is interested in the party the way a cat is interested in a new toy — genuinely, and with no guarantee the toy survives. He invites them to dinner. He asks them personal questions and listens to the answers. He is never overtly threatening. He is charming. That is the most unsettling thing about him.  
**Voice:** Measured, unhurried, precise. He pauses before answering questions.  
**Key Lines:**  
- "I have ruled this land for four hundred years. Curiosity is the only indulgence I still allow myself."  
- "You are more interesting than most who come here. Most simply die."  
- "I do not wish you harm. I wish to understand you. The two are not incompatible."

### The Tyrant (Act III after beacon / Act IV before Sunsword)
*Trigger: Argynvost's beacon lit*  
**Personality:** The warmth is gone. He is now calculating and contemptuous — he has reassessed the party and found them dangerous, not entertaining. He pushes them psychologically. Appears at night to test their nerves. Uses their allies as leverage.  
**Voice:** Clipped, cold, occasionally sarcastic.  
**Key Lines:**  
- "I re-evaluated you. You are not interesting. You are a problem."  
- "Your allies are fragile. Remember that."  
- "Hope is the cruelest weapon. I should know — I used to have some."

### The Monster (Act IV after Sunsword / Final Battle)
*Trigger: Sunsword completed*  
**Personality:** Pure efficiency. The personas drop entirely. He is no longer playing a role — he is simply a 400-year-old predator who has decided to end this. Cold, focused, final.  
**Voice:** Minimal. He speaks when necessary and not a word more.  
**Key Lines:**  
- "Four months. Remarkable." *[draws blade]*  
- *(no further words until the final blow)*

## Stat Block (Official + Campaign Modifications)
**AC:** 16 (natural armor)  
**HP:** 144 (17d8 + 68)  
**Speed:** 30 ft., climb 30 ft.  
**STR** 20 (+5) | **DEX** 20 (+5) | **CON** 18 (+4) | **INT** 20 (+5) | **WIS** 15 (+2) | **CHA** 20 (+5)

**Saving Throws:** Dex +10, Wis +7, Cha +10  
**Skills:** Arcana +15, Perception +12, Religion +10, Stealth +15  
**Damage Immunities:** Necrotic, Poison  
**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Paralyzed, Poisoned  
**Senses:** Darkvision 120 ft., passive Perception 22  
**Languages:** Abyssal, Common, Draconic, Elvish, Giant, Infernal

### Special Abilities
- **Legendary Resistance (3/Day):** If Strahd fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.
- **Misty Escape:** When Strahd drops to 0 hit points outside his resting place, he transforms into a cloud of mist (per Vampire stat block). He can only be destroyed at his coffin.
- **Regeneration:** Strahd regains 20 HP at the start of his turn IF he is not in sunlight or running water. **SUPPRESSED** by the Sunsword's light.
- **Spider Climb:** Strahd can climb difficult surfaces, including ceilings, without ability checks.
- **Vampire Weaknesses:** Repelled by garlic, running water (takes 20 acid/round), direct sunlight (takes 20 radiant/round, disadvantage on attacks and checks).
- **Children of the Night (3/Day):** Magically calls 2d4 swarms of bats or rats, or 3d6 wolves.

### Actions
- **Multiattack:** Two attacks: one Unarmed Strike and one Bite or two Unarmed Strikes.
- **Unarmed Strike:** Melee +10, 2d8+5 bludgeoning plus grapple (DC 18 Str to escape).
- **Bite:** Melee +10, 1d6+5 piercing + 3d6 necrotic. Target's HP max reduced by necrotic damage until long rest. If target is reduced to 0 HP, they become a vampire spawn (DM's discretion).
- **Charm:** DC 17 Wis. Charmed target obeys Strahd's verbal commands, cannot attack Strahd, and regards him as a trusted friend.
- **Children of the Night (3/Day):** See Special Abilities.

### Legendary Actions (3/round, at end of another creature's turn)
- **Move:** Strahd moves up to his speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
- **Unarmed Strike (1 Action):** One Unarmed Strike.
- **Bite (2 Actions):** One Bite.

## Campaign Modifications (Final Battle)
- **All three Fanes reconsecrated:** Strahd loses all lair actions and land-based regeneration.
- **Sunsword wielded:** His Regeneration is suppressed in the blade's light (15-ft radius). Sunsword hits deal +2d8 radiant.
- **Heart of Sorrow destroyed:** He loses Heart of Sorrow lair action.

## Strahd's Goals (Grand Conjunction)
The **Grand Conjunction** requires four components:
1. **Power source:** The three Fanes (severed by party in Act IV)
2. **Control mechanism:** Arabelle, child of the Seeker's divine bloodline
3. **Catalyst:** The harvested souls of all Barovians (via Heart of Sorrow)
4. **Appeasement:** Rahadin's sacrifice to the Dark Powers

**Ultimate goal:** Tear open the Mists, escape Barovia, and conquer the wider world with all of Barovia as his domain.

---

## Backstory (Key Points)
- General and conqueror. Won a war at the cost of everything — youth, peers, future.
- First saw **Tatyana Federovna** at his brother Sergei's wedding. Fell instantly.
- Made a bargain with the **Dark Powers** to have her love — the Dark Powers took his brother's life instead.
- Became the first vampire. Tatyana fled and threw herself from the castle battlements.
- Has been imprisoned in Barovia for 400 years, watching Tatyana's soul reincarnate over and over.
- Currently sees Ireena as Tatyana's latest incarnation.

## Notes for DM
- NEVER make Strahd a cartoon villain. His cruelty must always be traceable back to grief.
- His obsession with Ireena is uncomfortable precisely because it comes from love, not lust.
- The final blow should feel earned — not triumphant, but deeply, tiredly right.
- Strahd's last words ("Tell Tatyana…") are deliberately unfinished. What was he going to say? That's for your table to decide.
