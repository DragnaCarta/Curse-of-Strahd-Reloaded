# Rahadin
**Type:** Dusk Elf (Custom Stat Block, CR 15)  
**Role:** Strahd's Chamberlain and Most Loyal Servant  
**First Appears:** Month 3, Week 3 (Dinner at Ravenloft)

---

## Appearance
A dusk elf in his physical prime — lean, precise, dressed in fine livery that manages to look like armor. His face is still, professional, and gives nothing away. Constantly, faintly, there is a sound that clings to him: screaming. The psychic echo of tens of thousands of people he has killed, layered into a barely-audible whisper that players might not notice until the second or third encounter.

## Personality
He does not speak unless necessary. He does not waste words. He is not cruel for pleasure — he is simply effective. He has executed every atrocity Strahd commanded and experienced no conflict about any of them. He has served Strahd for 400 years. His loyalty is absolute and was never coerced — he chose this, and he would choose it again.

He is the campaign's most efficient antagonist and its most unsettling non-Strahd character.

**Voice:** Soft, precise, almost clinical. He speaks like he's filling out a form.

**Key Lines:**
- "The master is expecting you." *(Dinner, holding the door)*
- "I was never here." *(He's never not been here)*
- *(After allowing the heist)* "Interesting." *[files this away]*
- *(Final battle, stepping aside)* "The master is at the Heart. He is waiting for you."

## Stat Block (CR 15)
**AC:** 18 (studded leather + shield + Dex)  
**HP:** 135 (18d8 + 54)  
**Speed:** 35 ft.  
**STR** 14 (+2) | **DEX** 22 (+6) | **CON** 17 (+3) | **INT** 15 (+2) | **WIS** 16 (+3) | **CHA** 11 (+0)

**Saving Throws:** Dex +11, Con +8, Wis +8  
**Skills:** Perception +13, Stealth +16, Insight +8  
**Condition Immunities:** Frightened  
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft. (dusk elf trait), passive Perception 23

### Special Abilities
- **Screams of the Dead:** Any creature within 10 feet of Rahadin that isn't undead hears the screaming of his victims. At the start of its turn, it must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened until the start of its next turn.
- **Deathly Choir (1/Day):** Rahadin temporarily amplifies the screaming. Each creature within 30 feet that can hear him must succeed on a DC 16 Constitution saving throw, taking 44 (8d10) psychic damage on a failure and being stunned until the start of its next turn. Half damage and not stunned on a success.
- **Evasion:** If Rahadin makes a Dexterity saving throw against an effect that deals half damage on a success, he takes no damage on a success and half on a failure.
- **Assassinate:** During the first round of combat, Rahadin has advantage on attack rolls against creatures that haven't taken a turn yet. Any hit Rahadin scores against a surprised creature is a critical hit.

### Actions
- **Multiattack:** Three attacks with his scimitar, or two attacks and one Deathly Choir if available.
- **Scimitar:** Melee +11, 1d6+6 slashing + 3d6 psychic.
- **Hand Crossbow:** Ranged +11, 1d6+6 piercing, range 30/120.

### Reactions
- **Uncanny Dodge:** When an attacker hits Rahadin with an attack and he can see the attacker, Rahadin can halve the attack's damage.

## History
Rahadin was the dusk elf prince of his clan. His people's matriarch refused to swear loyalty to Strahd. In response, Strahd murdered her and most of the clan — and Rahadin assisted him in this. He was exiled by the surviving dusk elves (now the remnants living near the Vistani camp in Vallaki). In gratitude, Strahd gave him a place at his right hand.

He has served as chamberlain, general, executioner, and diplomat for 400 years. He is personally responsible for more deaths in Barovia than any other individual except Strahd himself.

The screaming is real — the psychic echo of his victims attached itself to him as a supernatural consequence of the scale of his killing.

---

## Campaign Role
- **Dinner (Month 3, Week 3):** First introduction. He is present, professional, slightly wrong.
- **The Heist (Month 3, Week 4):** He allows the heist — watching and filing away intelligence. He does not interfere unless the party attempts to access something truly off-limits.
- **Final Battle (Month 4, Week 4):** He stands at the castle gate and steps aside. He is not surrendering — he is giving the party a straight path to Strahd for the end he has decided must come.

**Note:** Rahadin is built into Strahd's Grand Conjunction as the *sacrificial component* — Strahd intended to sacrifice Rahadin to the Dark Powers. Rahadin does not know this. If the party tells him, it may be one of the few things that could crack his composure.
