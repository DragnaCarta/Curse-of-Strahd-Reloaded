# Vladimir Horngaard — The Bitter Commander
**Type:** Death Knight (CR 17) — with Legendary Actions  
**Location:** Argynvostholt, Great Hall — on his throne, staring at nothing  
**Role:** Former commander of the Order of the Silver Dragon; walking monument to grief that became hatred  
**Session:** Month 3, Week 3  
**Note:** He is NOT the enemy. He is wrong in a way that makes him dangerous if provoked.

---

## Appearance
Massive. Ancient plate armor — white-silver, now rusted dark in the gaps — seems fused to him. He does not breathe. He does not blink. He sits in the great hall and stares at the space where Argynvost used to stand.

He has the body language of someone waiting for a meeting that will never happen, in a room that has been empty for three hundred years.

---

## Personality
He did not become a Death Knight from evil. He became one from grief so complete that it calcified into something else. The Order of the Silver Dragon died stopping Strahd. Vladimir believes they died for nothing — and he cannot forgive that, cannot release it, because to release it would mean accepting that they are gone.

He hates Strahd. He also hates anyone who gives him hope, because hope means accepting the loss.

He will not fight the party unless they defile the manor or declare themselves Strahd's servants. But he will tell them they will fail. He has been telling everyone they will fail for three hundred years.

**Voice:** A sound like grinding stone and old armor. Slow. Each word costs him something.

**Key Lines:**
- *"You will fail. Every hero who has come to this valley has failed. I watched them fail."*
- *"If by some miracle you do not — bring our lord home. Let that much be undone."*
- *(if asked why he stays)* *"Because I cannot leave until he is avenged. Because he is not avenged. Because I have not decided what counts as avenged. I have had three hundred years. I am still deciding."*
- *(if a player pushes him)* *"Do not. I am not a puzzle to solve. I am not a quest. I am the proof that this valley breaks everything it touches, including me."*

---

## The Beacon Resolution
When Argynvost's skull is returned and the beacon lights, Vladimir sees the silver fire from the great hall window. He stands. He walks to the window. He stands there a long time.

Then he kneels. His armor falls. There is no body inside — just three hundred years of grief finally allowed to end. He dissolves into silver light, his spirit following the rest of the Order.

He never speaks. He doesn't need to.

## Stats — Death Knight (CR 17)
*Use only if the party attacks him or desecrates Argynvostholt. This is an almost-certain TPK at Level 10-11. That is intentional — he is not meant to be a combat encounter.*

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 180 (19d8 + 95) |
| AC | 20 (plate + shield) |
| Speed | 30 ft. |
| STR | 20 (+5) |
| DEX | 11 (+0) |
| CON | 20 (+5) |
| INT | 12 (+1) |
| WIS | 16 (+3) |
| CHA | 18 (+4) |

**Saving Throws:** DEX +6, WIS +9, CHA +10  
**Damage Immunities:** Necrotic, poison; bludgeoning/piercing/slashing from nonmagical weapons  
**Condition Immunities:** Exhaustion, frightened, poisoned  
**Senses:** Darkvision 120 ft.  
**CR:** 17 (13,000 XP)

### Special Traits
**Magic Resistance:** Advantage on saving throws against spells and magical effects.  
**Marshal Undead:** Undead creatures within 60 ft. of Vladimir have advantage on saving throws — as long as he lives.  
**Spellcasting (CHA, DC 18):** At will: *detect magic*, *thunderclap*; 3/day: *animate dead*, *dispel magic*, *hold person*; 1/day: *antimagic field*, *circle of death*, *finger of death*

### Actions
**Multiattack:** Three attacks with his Longsword of Wounding.

**Longsword of Wounding (+11 to hit):** 15 (1d8+9+1d6 necrotic) slashing, or 16 (1d10+9+1d6 necrotic) two-handed.

**Hellfire Orb (Recharge 5–6):** Vladimir hurls a 20-ft. radius orb of hellfire (DC 18 DEX). 35 (10d6) fire + 35 (10d6) necrotic on a failed save; half on success.

### Reactions
**Parry:** +6 bonus to AC against one melee attack that would hit him (must see the attacker).

### Legendary Actions (3/round, at end of another creature's turn)
**Cantrip (1 action):** Cast a cantrip.  
**Terrifying Glare (2 actions):** Vladimir targets one creature within 30 ft. — DC 18 WIS or frightened until end of Vladimir's next turn.  
**Disrupt Life (3 actions):** Each non-undead creature within 30 ft. takes 21 (6d6) necrotic damage (DC 18 CON for half).

### Undying
The first time Vladimir drops to 0 HP, he rises again at the start of his next turn with 30 HP, unless he took radiant damage or a bless spell was cast on him before he fell.

---

## DM Guidance — Why He Should NOT Be Fought

Vladimir is not designed as a combat encounter for this campaign arc. He is a tragedy to be witnessed and ultimately resolved by lighting the beacon.

If the party is pushing toward fighting him:
- Have Sir Godfrey appear and quietly say: *"He is what grief becomes when it has no end. Do not give him a clean death. Give him a purpose."*
- The source of the quest (Argynvost's skull, Ravenloft heist) is the answer — not swords

If they fight anyway: let it be brutal and real. Vladimir is CR 17 at Level 10-11. This will likely be a TPK. But he will not pursue them out of the manor — he is not interested in hunting heroes, only in occupying his grief.

---

## Relationship to Godfrey
Sir Godfrey (Revenant, CR 5, Monster Manual) is Vladimir's opposite — he kept his honor. He and Vladimir rarely speak. When the beacon lights, Godfrey kneels first. Vladimir watches Godfrey dissolve, and that — watching the last person who knew him find peace — is what finally breaks him.
