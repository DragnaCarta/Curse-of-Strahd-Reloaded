# Elisabeth Durst — The Amber Widow
**Location:** Death House — appears in the master bedroom vision, triggers the house collapse  
**Role:** True antagonist of Death House; the reason everything is wrong  
**Session:** Month 1, Week 1  
**Classification:** Ghost / Banshee equivalent — HOSTILE (final encounter)

---

## Appearance
In life: a beautiful, severe woman in expensive dark clothing with carefully arranged hair. In the visions, she radiates cold fury — not screaming, not raging. Just watching. Still. The stillness is what's frightening.

As a spirit at the end: her features are grotesquely distorted. Hair wild. Skin pallid. Lips peeled back. The amber shard at her neck glows ominously, a shadow swirling inside it. She looks like someone who let something in and became it over a very long time.

---

## Personality
In life: controlling, jealous, meticulous, proud. She discovered her husband's affair with the nursemaid and did not scream or cry. She *organized*. She used the cult she had built around Strahd to enact a precise, thorough revenge that destroyed everyone in the house including herself.

She does not see herself as evil. She sees herself as someone who was wronged and responded correctly.

In death: the amber shard has been feeding on her grief and rage for three hundred years. What is left is not really Elisabeth anymore. It is her worst moment, calcified.

---

## Her Role in Death House

**The Conservatory Vision:** The party sees her watching Gustav dance with Miss Klara. She reaches for the amber shard. Her eyes flash amber. She makes the dancers vanish with one gesture. This is her in her element — absolute control over her environment.

**The Amber Shard (Elisabeth's pendant):**
Found in the Master Suite jewelry box. This is the key that controls the Flesh Mound — placing it in the ritual chamber niche opens the secret passage. Presenting it to the Flesh Mound and speaking "Walter" allows one command (DC 13 Charisma/Intimidation, see plot.md).

**The House Collapse:**
When the party attempts to leave the Reliquary (basement), Elisabeth's spirit manifests as the final obstacle — distorted, furious, blocking the exit with her presence. She does not fight them directly. She is the signal that triggers the house's collapse.

> *"A guttural moan ripples through the air — and a terrifying apparition manifests: the spirit of Elisabeth Durst, her once-beautiful features grotesquely distorted. She fixes you with a feral grin — and then dissipates into thin air, leaving only echoes of spiteful laughter as the house rumbles, shifts, and groans."*

She is not killable here. She is a trigger. Run for the door.

## The Durst Family Web

| Person | Relationship | Fate |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Gustav Durst (husband) | She had him killed by the cult | Ghost/poltergeist in the basement |
| Miss Klara (nursemaid) | Locked her in the attic to starve | Spirit trapped in a mirror |
| Walter Durst (infant) | Her husband's child by Klara; she had him sacrificed to the Flesh Mound | His corpse is the Mound's heart |
| Rose & Thorn (her children) | Died in the house; she never acknowledged causing it | Ghost children in the attic |

Everything that is wrong with Death House traces back to Elisabeth Durst deciding that control was more important than any of the people in her life.

## DM Notes
- Never let Elisabeth feel like a monster. She was a person who was genuinely betrayed and responded by becoming the thing she wanted to destroy.
- The party never gets a conversation with her. She is all consequence, no redemption.
- If a player tries to talk to her during the collapse: she doesn't hear them. She hasn't heard anyone in three hundred years.
- Her amber shard is the mechanical key to the Flesh Mound — make sure the party finds it in the Master Suite before the basement
