# Elder Burebis
**Type:** Ancient Storm Giant (Dying); no combat — he cannot stand  
**Role:** Keeper of the Fane Lore; Final Exposition; Death Scene  
**Appears:** Month 4, Week 1 (Soldav, the hidden mountain village)

---

## Appearance
Enormous even seated — a storm giant shrunken by incredible age, wrapped in blankets and furs that still don't look sufficient. His skin is the color of thunderclouds. His eyes are the color of lightning — still. He has a stillness that suggests he has been sitting here so long the mountain has grown around him.

He is clearly dying. He knows it. He is at peace with it. He has one thing left to do.

## Personality
Vast. Patient. He speaks slowly because he has had a very long time to think about how to say things. He is not slow — he is considered.

He has watched over the valley since before Strahd arrived. He watched the Ladies Three be worshipped. He watched the Fanes be built. He watched Strahd desecrate them. He has been waiting for someone capable enough to receive what he knows.

**Voice:** Deep, slow, resonant — thunder turned gentle. Each sentence is a considered gift.

**Key Lines:**
- "I have been waiting for someone worth telling."
- "The Ladies Three. You know their names. You know their Fanes. You do not yet know what the Fanes are — truly. Sit."
- "The gems must be planted at the Fanes. We must reconsecrate them. But to reach the Swamp Fane — Baba Lysaga guards it. We can't move without dealing with her."
- "The man in the dark castle — he was not always what he is. Remember that. In the last moment, when you stand before him — remember that. It may matter."
- *(final words, closing his eyes)* "My work is done."

---

## What He Reveals (The Fane Lore)
This is the campaign's strategic exposition scene — the party learns, for the first time in full, the mechanics of what they must do.

**The key points:**
1. The Fanes are not just sacred sites — they are anchors of the Ladies' divine power in the valley.
2. Strahd desecrated all three and bonded himself to them. He is unkillable while that bond holds — even if his body is destroyed, the land regenerates him.
3. To sever the bond: reconsecrate each Fane using the appropriate artifact (Weaver's gem, Huntress's spear, Seeker's gem).
4. Each reconsecration weakens him in the final battle (lair actions lost, land regeneration gone).
5. All three completed: the Heart of Sorrow becomes his only remaining power source.

**He also reveals the Strahd warning:** "He was not always this." This is the campaign's moral thesis stated plainly. The party now has the answer to a question they may not have known they were asking.

---

## His Death
He dies quietly before morning. There is no drama in it — he was ready. The mountain folk of Soldav mourn with dignity.

**DM note:** Don't rush past his death. Give it a moment. The party has just received the most important tactical information of the campaign from someone who held it for centuries waiting for them. Let that settle.

The mountain folk build him a cairn on the slope above Soldav. Ravens from the Keepers of the Feather land on it as the party prepares to continue to the Amber Temple. This is not explained. It doesn't need to be.

---

## Soldav (The Hidden Village)
Burebis's home. The mountain folk who live there are isolated, secretive, and the last practitioners of the old faith that hasn't been corrupted by the druidic cult at Yester Hill.

**Key villagers:**
- **Kavan** — Weapons-master, suspicious, warms to respect
- **Helwa** — Keeper of oral history; knows the Ladies' lore from a different angle than Burebis
- **Diegia, Oroles, Como** — Village leaders; cautious but welcoming of people Burebis trusts

They can provide a safe long rest, supplies, and knowledge of the mountain pass to the Amber Temple.
