# Walter Durst — The Innocent Heart
**Location:** Death House Basement — inside the Flesh Mound (the boss's weak point)  
**Role:** Mechanical boss weak point; the tragedy at the center of everything  
**Session:** Month 1, Week 1  
**Classification:** NPC (deceased infant) — not interactive, not hostile

---

## Who He Was
An infant. The illegitimate son of Gustav Durst and Miss Klara the nursemaid. He never had a name publicly — Elisabeth refused to acknowledge him. Rose's diary refers to "the baby" with the carefully neutral language of a child who has been told not to ask questions.

He was sacrificed by Elisabeth's cult when he was still an infant. He never spoke a word. He is the most innocent person in Death House and the center of everything wrong with it.

---

## His Role in the Flesh Mound Fight

Walter's preserved infant corpse is suspended inside the Flesh Mound's rib cage — the creature's literal heart.

**Innocent Heart (Weak Point Mechanic):**
- AC 15, uses the Flesh Mound's ability scores
- Every point of damage dealt to Walter's corpse deals **twice** that amount to the Flesh Mound
- Finding the heart requires either destroying the mound's outer layers until it's exposed, or triggering the **Wail** action (which briefly exposes it)

**Elisabeth's Amber Shard Command:**
When a character presents Elisabeth's amber shard and speaks the name *"Walter"* within 30 feet of the Mound:
- DC 13 Charisma (Intimidation)
- On success: the Mound must use a reaction to follow one command — move, stop, release someone it has absorbed
- It will not obey a command directly harmful to itself
- This works because the Mound is, at some level, still Walter — and still responding to his mother's voice

---

## The Emotional Weight

Walter did nothing. He was born, he was killed, and his body was used as the anchor of a horror that has existed for three hundred years. The amber shard that controls the Flesh Mound is the same amber shard Elisabeth used to have him sacrificed.

If any party member realizes what they're looking at inside the Flesh Mound:

> *"Inside the rib cage, hovering in the center of the creature — an infant. Preserved, not rotted. Small enough to hold in one hand. Eyes closed. The Mound built itself around him, and has been built around him for three hundred years."*

Give the party a moment with this. Don't rush it.

---

## After the Fight

Walter's corpse is released when the Flesh Mound is destroyed. It is small. It is quiet. It has the quality of something that has been waiting to be found.

**If a player picks it up:**
There is no magic, no ghost, no voice. Just the weight of a very small body and the understanding that this is what was at the center of everything.

**What to do with it:** The party can leave it, bury it in the yard, or carry it out. There is no mechanical effect to any choice. The right choice is whatever feels right to the player holding it.

If buried outside the house before it collapses: Rose and Thorn's spirits, watching from the doorway, see it. Rose nods once. It means something to her even if she cannot say what.

---

## DM Notes
- Walter is not a character — he is a revelation. The fight with the Flesh Mound is viscerally mechanical until the party realizes what the weak point is.
- Don't hide what they're seeing. When they find the heart, describe it clearly. Let it land.
- The amber shard mechanic is your "escape valve" if the fight is going badly — make sure the party finds Elisabeth's shard in the Master Suite before reaching the basement
- Walter's burial, if the party chooses it, is one of the most quietly powerful moments available in Session 1. Let it happen at its own pace.
