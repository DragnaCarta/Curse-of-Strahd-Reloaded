# Vallaki Townspeople — Minor NPCs
Supporting cast of Vallaki. No combat stats unless noted.

---

## Gadof Blinsky
**Role:** Toy-maker; Blinsky Toys; unwilling Izek commission artist  
**Appears:** Month 2, Week 2 (Blinsky Toys shop, Vallaki)

### Appearance
A small, sad man in a jester's hat and coat — his "professional uniform." The hat has bells that don't ring anymore because they've been worn smooth. He looks like a man who chose a cheerful profession and has been outlasted by it.

### Personality
Perpetually melancholy, genuinely talented, harmless. He makes the best toys in Barovia. He is also deeply unhappy in Barovia. His professional motto — "If it's not fun, it's not Blinsky's" — sounds increasingly ironic the longer you talk to him.

He makes the Ireena-lookalike dolls because Izek pays him and Izek frightens him. He asks no questions. He regrets this.

**Key Lines:**
- "Welcome, welcome. Blinsky says: if it's not fun, it's not Blinsky's." *(jingle bells, defeated tone)*
- "The dolls? He comes with charcoal sketches. He wants them to look exactly right. I ask no questions. Izek has no sense of humor about questions."
- "I would very much like to move to a different city. Any city."

**The Toy Shop:** Full of impressive mechanical toys, puppets, and marionettes. The Ireena dolls fill one shelf. A clockwork monkey in the window waves its arm every thirty seconds regardless of whether anyone is watching.

## Henrik van der Voort
**Role:** Coffin-maker; Volenta's unwilling host; Arc D key NPC  
**Appears:** Month 2, Week 1 (Coffin-maker's shop, Vallaki)

### Appearance
A nervous middle-aged man with the dark circles of someone who hasn't slept since a vampire moved into his attic. He answers the door, takes one look at the party, and immediately tries to close it.

### Personality
Terrified. He is not bad — he is a man who made one bad decision (accepting payment to store "some crates" for a woman with too many teeth) and has been paying for it in pure fear ever since.

**He is not Volenta's accomplice.** He is her hostage. She threatened his family. He believed her. He was correct to.

**If the party is kind to him:** He breaks almost immediately. He WANTS to tell someone. He's been alone with this for days.

**After the threat is resolved:** He is a useful source of information about coffin-and-crypt related services (someone has to build coffins in Barovia). He has a certain black-humor practicality about his profession.

**Key Lines:**
- "Please. Please don't — she'll — my children —"
- *(after Volenta is driven off, giving the key)* "I didn't know what the bones were for. I thought it was just a joke. She makes horrible jokes."

## Milivoj
**Role:** St. Andral's Church groundskeeper; stole the bones out of desperation  
**Appears:** Month 2, Week 1 (St. Andral's Church)

### Appearance
A teenager — seventeen at most. Guilt written across his entire face from the moment the party speaks to him. Dark circles. He has been unable to eat since he did it.

### Personality
Desperate and honest. He stole the bones of St. Andral and sold them to the coffin-maker for a handful of coins. His parents are dead. He has seven younger siblings. He needed the money for food. He did not know what the bones were for.

He breaks with almost no pressure — he has been waiting for someone to confess to.

**Father Lucian's response:** "You were afraid and hungry. That is not a sin I can condemn." He is immediately, genuinely forgiven. Milivoj cries.

**After the arc:** Father Lucian gives Milivoj a real wage from the church's small treasury. He becomes a reliable groundskeeper. By Act III he is running minor charitable work out of the church on Lucian's behalf.

**Key Lines:**
- "I just — the children were hungry — I didn't know —" *(can barely get words out)*

## Ernst Larnak
**Role:** Lady Wachter's spy and go-between; everywhere, remembered by no one  
**Appears:** Month 2, Week 2 (Wachterhaus dinner; seen in various Vallaki locations)

### Appearance
Middle-aged. Forgettable face — average height, average coloring, the kind of person you don't retain in memory after he leaves a room. This is professionally useful.

### Personality
Professional, watchful, completely loyal to Lady Wachter. He has no apparent personal life or opinions. If he has them, they are private.

**Role:** He is everywhere. If something happens in Vallaki, Ernst knows within hours. He feeds information to Lady Wachter. He handles the logistical side of her plans — the notes, the arrangements, the payments.

**Key Lines:**
- *(at the dinner, mostly)* *(watching)*
- *(if the party spots him in public)* "Good evening." *(continues moving)*
- *(if confronted)* "I run errands for Lady Wachter. Everyone in Vallaki runs errands for someone."

## Arturi Radanavich
**Role:** Wandering Vistani; Van Richten connection; the curse-giver  
**Appears:** Month 1, Week 3 (Tser Pool camp, briefly)

### Appearance
Middle-aged Vistani man, missing two fingers on his right hand. Travels alone. The kind of person who carries their history visibly.

### Personality
Quiet, weighted. He is not hostile. He has been through something that stripped the hostility away and left a more complicated residue.

He cursed Van Richten. The Radanavich clan — after Van Richten's actions led (unintentionally) to their destruction by a monster — cursed him: everyone he loves deeply dies by a monster's claws. Arturi is the last Radanavich. He placed the curse himself.

He does not regret it. He also does not feel better. The curse made sense as grief. It doesn't undo anything.

**Key Lines:**
- "Van Richten. You know that name." *(flat, not a question)*
- "He destroyed my clan. Not on purpose — but what's the difference to the dead?"
- "I have carried this long enough to know it doesn't get lighter. You just get stronger."

**Campaign Relevance:** Arturi's curse on Van Richten explains why Van Richten pushes people away. If the party can arrange a genuine conversation between them (possible in Act III or IV): the curse can be lifted. This is not required. It is meaningful.

## Cyrus Belview
**Role:** Eldest mongrelfolk at the Abbey; most articulate resident  
**Appears:** Month 3, Week 1–2 (Abbey of Saint Markovia)

### Appearance
Too many features — the Abbot's experiments gave him functional animal traits alongside his human ones. He has worked out a kind of dignity about it.

### Personality
Dry, observant, unexpectedly philosophical. He has had a lot of time to think about what it means to be what he is.

**Key Lines:**
- "The Abbot means well. The road to the Abyss is paved with good meaning, is it not?"
- "We are what happened when someone tried to fix things with the wrong tools. There are worse origins."
- *(on leaving the abbey after the campaign)* "Cyrus Belview is going to see what the world looks like now. He's heard it has a sky."
