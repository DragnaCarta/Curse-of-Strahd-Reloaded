# Lady Fiona Wachter
**Type:** Human Aristocrat / Cult Leader (Stat Block: Mage, CR 6)  
**Role:** Vallaki's Political Operator; Strahd Loyalist → Pragmatic Ally  
**First Appears:** Month 2, Week 2 (Wachterhaus dinner)  
**Inspiration:** Cersei Lannister, Lady Macbeth, a Cersei who has a moment of grace

---

## Appearance
Impeccably dressed in black silk — it always looks like armor. Her face gives nothing away unless you watch for the moments when she looks at her daughter Stella. Those moments reveal everything.

## Personality
Calculating, precise, cold in her professional dealings, capable of warmth in private. She is a pragmatist so thorough it has become its own kind of ideology: the practical path is always correct, which has led her to conclusions that are morally uncomfortable.

She is not evil. She is a person who has decided that survival in Barovia requires bending the knee, and has committed to that decision completely — which makes her both a useful ally and someone who will compromise in ways the party may find appalling.

**Voice:** Measured. Every word placed deliberately. She does not raise her voice.

**Key Lines:**
- "Baron Vallakovich is insane. That alone might be forgivable. He is also *dangerous*."
- "I want to lead this city, and I want to lead it *well*. Those are not incompatible things."
- *(looking at Stella)* *(says nothing for a moment)*
- *(after Stella is restored)* "Thank you." *(two words, full weight of the world in them)*
- *(at the war council)* "You cannot be here when the walls break. You must be at Ravenloft."

## The Cult of Ezra
Lady Wachter runs a cult — the Cult of Ezra, devotees of a deity called Ezra who is actually (unknown to the cultists) the Dark Powers in a soft-focus form. The cult preaches acceptance of Barovia's reality: cooperate, endure, survive. The cultists are ordinary Vallakians who have been given a community and a theology that helps them cope. They are not evil. The theology is wrong.

Lady Wachter knows exactly what she is doing. She believes it is the most compassionate option available.

---

## Stella Wachter
Lady Wachter's daughter (early twenties) was left catatonic by Victor's spirit mirror experiment. Her soul was scattered in the Ethereal Plane. Lady Wachter has been unable to reach her or help her.

When the party rescues Stella (Arc H, Month 2, Week 4): the reunion is Lady Wachter's single moment of complete vulnerability. She holds Stella and says nothing. The cultist, the political operator, the woman who asks parties to commit murder for pragmatic reasons — all of that is in the room, and the only thing that matters is her daughter's face.

---

## Arc F: The Assassination Request
Lady Wachter asks the party to kill Izek Strazni. She provides:
- Odorless poison for his wine
- A burlap sack "for proof"
- 200 gp per party member + safe passage

**Her reasoning is correct:** Izek is a monster and Baron Vallakovich will eventually trigger Strahd's mass violence. Her method is morally uncomfortable. This is the arc's point.

---

## Campaign Arc
- **Act II:** Dinner, political manipulation, assassination request.
- After Izek's death: takes power in Vallaki. Governs better than the Baron, though not perfectly.
- **Act III:** Present in Vallaki; provides political support.
- **Act IV (Three Fanes):** Forced to choose between Karl (her son, held hostage) and the Mountain Fane. If Karl is rescued: she plants the gem without hesitation.
- **War Council:** Organizes Vallaki's defense. Her finest moment.
