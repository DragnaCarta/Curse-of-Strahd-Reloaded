# Argynvost
**Type:** Silver Dragon (Ghost; no combat stats — communications only)  
**Role:** Argynvostholt's Soul; The Beacon; Symbol of Hope  
**Appears:** Month 3, Week 3 (Dragon Gargoyle, Argynvostholt chapel)  
**Arc:** Skull must be recovered from Castle Ravenloft and returned to the mausoleum to relight the beacon

---

## Appearance
In life: an enormous silver dragon who took human form to walk among the valley's people. His statues throughout Argynvostholt show a dragon of noble bearing — not monstrous, but majestic in the way of very old, very wise things.

As a ghost (communicated through the dragon gargoyle): a presence rather than a form. When he speaks through the gargoyle, the temperature drops slightly and the air smells of old stone and open sky.

His skull has been mounted in Castle Ravenloft's Hall of Bones for 300 years — a trophy.

## Personality
Noble, patient, sorrowful. He has been a ghost for three centuries, waiting for someone capable enough and willing enough to restore his resting place. He does not demand. He asks.

He speaks the way very old, very tired things speak — without urgency, but with weight. Every word he says has had three hundred years to settle.

**Voice (through the gargoyle):** Deep, resonant, oddly warm despite the cold air. Slow.

**Key Lines:**
- "I fought for this valley. I failed. I have had three hundred years to make peace with both of those things."
- "My skull rests in the devil's hall of trophies. I do not ask you to risk yourselves for my dignity. I ask because the beacon cannot burn without it. And the beacon matters."
- "Bring me home. That is all I ask. Bring me home and light the fire."
- *(if asked why he stayed)* "Because someone had to keep hoping. Even if the hope was only a gargoyle in an empty chapel."

---

## The Vision (Triggered by Touching the Gargoyle)
Players see the battle three centuries ago: Argynvost fighting Strahd's forces on a clifftop, losing, buying time for the last of his Order to escape. The dragon falls. Its last thought: *the beacon. Light the beacon. Let there be hope.*

This is not a fight scene — it is a tragedy, watched passively. The players cannot intervene.

---

## What the Beacon Does
When Argynvost's skull is returned to the mausoleum and the beacon is lit:
- Silver fire rises from Argynvostholt's peak, visible across all of Barovia
- All characters actively resisting Strahd gain **+1 bonus to AC and saving throws** while the beacon burns
- Strahd shifts from the **Gentleman** to the **Tyrant** persona — the beacon proves the party is a real threat
- The surviving revenants of the Order find peace and dissolve into light
- Vladimir Horngaard finally releases his 300-year-old hatred and dies

---

## The Silver Dragon Egg (Epilogue)
After the victory, a silver dragon egg is found at Argynvostholt — laid by Argynvost before his death, preserved by his lingering magic. It begins to crack as the sun rises. This is a campaign epilogue gift: the Order of the Silver Dragon will continue.

---

## Sir Godfrey Gwilym (The Revenant Knight)
Argynvost's most loyal surviving knight. A revenant who has walked the Svalich Road for 300 years protecting travelers.

**Appearance:** Full plate armor, tarnished silver, face gaunt and hollow as all revenants. Clearly dead and clearly aware.  
**Personality:** Patient, sad, honorable to the point of inflexibility. He has been waiting for the party for three centuries without quite knowing it.  
**First Appears:** Month 1, Week 4 (on the road to Vallaki)  
**Voice:** Creaking, slow, formal.

**Key Lines:**
- "Do you come to serve the devil, or to oppose him?"
- *(on being asked why he still walks)* "Because no one has given me a reason to stop."
- *(when the beacon lights)* He kneels. He does not speak. He dissolves into silver light.

**Stat Block:** Standard Revenant (MM) — CR 5. He does not attack the party unless they declare themselves Strahd's servants.

---

## Vladimir Horngaard (The Bitter Commander)
The Order's former commander. Unlike Godfrey, Vladimir's hatred has consumed him completely.

**Appearance:** Massive. Ancient plate armor rusted onto a frame that doesn't need to breathe. He sits in Argynvostholt's great hall and stares at nothing.  
**Personality:** Consumed by hatred — hatred of Strahd, but also hatred of the party for daring to hope where his Order failed. He is not an enemy. He is simply wrong in a way that makes him useless.  
**Key Lines:**
- "You will fail. Every hero who has come to this valley has failed. I watched them fail."
- "If by some miracle you do not — bring our lord home. Let that much be undone."

**Stat Block:** See `Campaign/NPCs/monsters/vladimir_horngaard.md` — Death Knight, CR 17. He will not fight the party unless they defile the manor or declare themselves Strahd's servants. Do not fight him. He is a tragedy to witness, not a monster to defeat.  
**At the beacon lighting:** His three-century scream. Then dust. Then peace.
