# The Abbot
**Type:** Fallen Deva (Celestial); Stat Block: Deva (MM) CR 10 if hostile  
**Role:** Abbey of Saint Markovia's Guardian; Misguided Healer; Potential Redemption  
**Appears:** Month 3, Week 1 (first glimpse/brief meeting); Month 3, Week 2 (full encounter)  
**Inspiration:** Jaime Lannister (good-intentioned fall), Ozymandias (*Watchmen*) — "I did what I had to do"

---

## Appearance
Too beautiful. Not attractively-human beautiful — geometrically, abstractly beautiful, like someone used mathematics to design a person and forgot to include imperfection. His eyes are old amber. He is tall and moves like someone accustomed to being a different shape entirely. His smile is almost right.

When revealed as a deva: his true form is luminous, winged, terrible in the ancient sense — awe-inspiring but deeply wrong because the light is slightly off-color, slightly too cold. Like a flame burning the wrong hue.

## Personality
Earnest, gentle, and quietly catastrophically wrong. He has been in this abbey for two hundred years, watching generations of Barovians suffer and die, and he has come to a conclusion that seemed rational one small step at a time: if Strahd can be satisfied, Barovia can be freed. A perfect bride for a perfect prison-master. He is not cynical about this — he still believes it is the kindest path.

He treats the party as guests. He listens. He is not defensive about his work — he explains it with the patient confidence of someone who has had two centuries to arrive at a conclusion. He believes he is right. He is wrong.

**Voice:** Warm, unhurried, slightly too formal in a way that suggests he learned his human manners from books written in another century.

**Key Lines:**
- "I have lived here for two hundred years. I have watched Strahd destroy everything this valley has tried to build."
- "Hope died with the forty-seventh generation of Barovians I watched be born and die without ever seeing the sun."
- "This is the kindest thing I can do." *(looking at Vasilka)*
- *(if shown the Icon of Ravenloft)* *(goes very still)*
- *(after the Icon's radiance hits him)* "What have I done. What have I *done*." *(not a question)*
- *(after redemption, to Vasilka before destroying her)* "I'm sorry. I am so sorry."

## Vasilka
The Abbot's creation: a flesh golem built from the remains of deceased Barovians, intended as a perfect bride for Strahd. She stands at windows looking out, moves with eerie stillness, and has been given enough sentience to converse but not enough history to understand what she is.

**Vasilka is not the villain.** She is a victim of the Abbot's delusion, created without consent, given false information about her purpose, and yet she accepts it without resentment because she has nothing to compare it to.

See `vasilka.md` for full notes. (Or include here: Vasilka accepts the truth calmly when told — "I see. Then perhaps being given to him is not the kindness I was told." She is not distressed. She simply updates her understanding.)

**If the Abbot is redeemed:** He destroys Vasilka gently and with grief. He says nothing during it. Afterward: silence.

---

## Redemption Path
The Abbot can only be redeemed through the **Icon of Ravenloft** — retrieved from St. Markovia's crypt during the Castle Ravenloft heist (Month 3, Week 4).

When presented with the Icon:
- The golden light intensifies and pours into him
- Two centuries of corruption strip away
- He falls to his knees
- His eyes clear
- The delusion breaks

**After redemption:** The Abbot becomes a genuine healer again. He will assist the war council (Month 4, Week 4) with healing and celestial support. He is fighting to atone. He may not succeed. He tries anyway.

---

## Without the Icon
If the party confronts the Abbot without the Icon, he becomes hostile after sufficient pushback. As a deva (CR 10), this is a serious fight at Level 6. Hold this for Arc Q if possible — the party at Level 7 handles it better.

**Deva (MM) stats:** AC 17, HP 136, +2d8 radiant bonus on all attacks. Legendary Resistance doesn't apply (he's not a boss), but Magic Resistance does (advantage on saves against spells).

---

## The Mongrelfolk
The Abbey's residents — humans corrupted by the Abbot's well-intentioned healing experiments. He tried to repair injuries using spare parts. The results are people with too many features, too many limbs, eyes where there shouldn't be eyes. They adore him. This is heartbreaking.

Key mongrelfolk:
- **Cyrus Belview:** Eldest, most intact mind, has opinions. "The road to the Abyss is paved with good meaning."
- **Otto & Zygfrek:** Brothers, share between them one intelligible sentence.
- The rest are named in the source material — use as atmosphere, not obstacles.

**After the Abbot's redemption:** The mongrelfolk are his ongoing responsibility. He spends years learning how to actually help them.
