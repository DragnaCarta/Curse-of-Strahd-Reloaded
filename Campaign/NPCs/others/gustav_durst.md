# Gustav Durst — The Broken Patriarch
**Location:** Death House Basement — blocks the exit from the Reliquary  
**Role:** Reluctant obstacle; tragic figure; the other half of the house's tragedy  
**Session:** Month 1, Week 1  
**Classification:** Poltergeist (without Invisibility) — initially hostile, can be bypassed

---

## Appearance
In the Conservatory vision: a handsome, well-dressed man dancing with Miss Klara, oblivious to his wife watching from the corner. Comfortable, charming, selfish.

As a spirit in the basement: a translucent, haggard version of the portrait upstairs. He looks like someone who has spent three hundred years knowing exactly what he did and being unable to do anything about it.

His flayed skin is the Grick in the Storage alcove. His flayed skin is also the Boneless in the foot locker. What's left of him as a ghost is somehow less substantial than either of those.

---

## Personality
Weak. Not evil — just weak in the specific way that allows evil to happen around it. He had an affair. He didn't stop the cult. He didn't protect Miss Klara. He didn't protect his children. He stood in the way of consequences at every turn until the consequences stopped caring whether he was standing or not.

As a ghost he is consumed by guilt and terror — terror of Elisabeth specifically. Three hundred years of being dead has not made him braver. He is still, fundamentally, a man who does not want to deal with the situation he created.

---

## His Role in the Basement

Gustav's ghost blocks the exit from the Reliquary when the party tries to leave. He begs them to stop — not out of malice, but out of fear. He is terrified of what Elisabeth will do if they try to leave.

> *"Please. Stop. You don't understand what she's capable of. I've tried. I've tried so many times. She's too powerful. Just — just stay. It will be easier if you just stay."*

A DC 10 Insight check reveals he is terrified of Elisabeth, consumed by guilt, and fundamentally does not believe he has any choice — he has been telling himself that for so long it has calcified into conviction.

**He does not want to fight.** If the party tries to push past him or attack him, levitating debris rises around him — he pleads again. He will fight reluctantly, badly, and will stop the moment the party demonstrates they will not back down.

**How to get past him without fighting:**
- **Call him out:** Tell him he's doing exactly what he did in life — standing aside while bad things happen and calling it helplessness. DC 12 Charisma (Persuasion). On success: he goes still. He does not stop them. He cannot look at them.
- **Mention his children:** Ask him if Rose and Thorn wanted him to do this. DC 10 Charisma. On success: he lowers his hands. The debris falls. He says nothing.
- **Invoke Miss Klara:** Ask what she would think of him blocking the only people who have ever tried to leave. Automatic success. He simply moves aside. He looks away.

## His Relationship to the Other Dursts

| Person | His Role |
|--------|---------|
| Elisabeth | He was unfaithful to her. He allowed her to build a death cult in his house. He told himself he had no choice. |
| Miss Klara | He loved her, after a fashion. He did not protect her. He has not forgiven himself. |
| Walter | His son by Klara. He watched Elisabeth have him sacrificed. He told himself he had no choice. |
| Rose & Thorn | His legitimate children. He let them die in this house. He told himself he had no choice. |

Gustav Durst is a monument to what "I had no choice" actually means when someone keeps saying it long enough.

---

## DM Notes
- Play him as exhausted, not menacing. He is not the threat — he is the warning about what happens when you stop making choices.
- The Insight check reveal is important: let a player earn the understanding that he is terrified, not evil
- The "mention Miss Klara" bypass is the most emotionally satisfying resolution — reward clever players who connected the dots
- After the party gets past him, he doesn't follow. He drifts back toward the spot where he's been standing for three centuries. He is still there when the house falls.
