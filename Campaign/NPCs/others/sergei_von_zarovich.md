# Sergei von Zarovich
**Type:** Ghost (Prince); Stat Block: Ghost (CR 4) — nonhostile  
**Role:** Strahd's Murdered Brother; Sunsword's Soul; Forgiveness Incarnate  
**Appears:** Month 4, Week 2 (Amber Temple — summoned to empower the Sunsword)

---

## Appearance
A young man, early twenties, translucent and faintly golden — like sunlight filtered through water. He is Strahd's brother. The resemblance is there, but where Strahd is angles and calculation, Sergei is open and direct and warm. He looks like what Strahd might have been if things had gone differently.

He is wearing the wedding clothes he died in.

## Personality
He is not bitter. Three hundred years of being dead and he has reached a kind of peace that is both genuinely admirable and slightly unnerving to those who haven't. He knows what Strahd did. He knows why. He has made his peace with it in a way Strahd never has.

He is ready to pass on. He has been waiting for someone to ask him — not just to use the sword, but to ask *him*, to acknowledge what was taken.

**Voice:** Young, clear, warm. He sounds like morning.

**Key Lines:**
- "I have been waiting for someone brave enough to ask. Not for the sword — for *me*. To acknowledge what was taken from me."
- "Will you use this to free Barovia? Will you use it for vengeance?"
- *(when given the right answer)* *(the hilt blazes)*
- "Tell my brother I forgive him. Whatever good that does either of us." *(fades)*

---

## The Right Answer
Sergei asks the party what they will use the Sunsword for. The right answer is not "to kill Strahd" — the sword will respond, but weakly, and without the full empowerment.

The answer that calls the blade fully is **sincere and focused on others**:
- "To free Barovia"
- "To end the suffering"
- "To bring the light back"
- "To give these people their lives back"

Any genuine version of this — even imperfect words — activates the full empowerment. Sergei can feel sincerity.

**If the party gives a vengeance-focused answer:** The sword still functions (it responds to opposition to undead) but the radiant +2d8 bonus and sunlight radius are halved. Sergei seems sad rather than angry.

---

## Sergei and Tatyana
Tatyana was his betrothed. He loved her. He watched Strahd watch her during the months of wedding preparation and felt something he could not name.

He does not speak of her to the party unless asked directly. If asked:
> "She was wonderful. She was kind to everyone, always. She didn't deserve any of what happened to her. Neither did Strahd, in the end — he was just the one who chose wrongly."

---

## What He Knows About Strahd
He has been watching, in the Mists, for three hundred years. He knows his brother better than anyone alive.

If the party asks: "Can he be saved?" Sergei says: "I don't know. I hope so. I forgave him. Whether he can forgive himself — that I don't know."

---

## The Sunsword (Fully Empowered)
- Blade of pure sunlight extending from the hilt
- Bright sunlight in 15-foot radius (vampire mechanics apply — no regeneration, disadvantage on attacks/checks in sunlight)
- **+2d8 radiant bonus damage** against undead when fully empowered
- Strahd's regeneration does not function in its light
- Wielder can suppress the light as a bonus action
