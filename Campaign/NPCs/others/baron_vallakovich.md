# Baron Vargas Vallakovich
**Type:** Human Noble (Stat Block: Noble, CR 1/8)  
**Role:** Vallaki's Tyrant Burgomaster; Comic Villain Turned Pathetic  
**Appears:** Month 2, Week 1 onward (Vallaki)

---

## Appearance
A man in his fifties who dresses as if performing the idea of authority — golden trim on his coat, a signet ring too large for his finger, a wig that is slightly askew. He beams with the fixed smile of someone who has decided that smiling means everything is fine.

## Personality
He is genuinely insane, but in a specific and somewhat pitiful way: he has convinced himself that if he holds enough festivals, Strahd will eventually be satisfied, the fog will lift, and everything will be fine. He is not wrong that morale matters — he is catastrophically wrong about the method and increasingly disconnected from reality.

He is not cruel in the way Izek is cruel. He is cruel in the way a man is cruel when his anxiety has become ideology: punishing people for pessimism because their pessimism threatens his framework for coping.

Victor Vallakovich is his son. He doesn't understand Victor and gave up trying years ago.

**Voice:** Loud, confident, cracking around the edges. He ends statements with "All is well!" delivered like a prayer.

**Key Lines:**
- "ALL IS WELL. All festivals strengthen our resolve against the darkness. All is WELL."
- *(when challenged)* "You are being pessimistic. Pessimism is dangerous. Guards!"
- *(to Victor)* "When are you going to come out of that room and do something useful?"
- *(when removed from power by Lady Wachter)* He sits in his room and stares at the wall. He looks very small.

---

## The Festivals
Baron Vallakovich has held a festival every week for years. The current one — the **Festival of the Blazing Sun** (Neyavr 7) — involves a wicker sun effigy and mandatory citizen attendance. If it rains, he declares it still a success and punishes anyone who disagrees.

**Festival history** (posted in the town square): "The Festival of the Rusty Nail." "The Festival of the Tired Horse." "The Festival of the Almost-Nice Weather." Citizens have developed a gallows humor about them.

## Stat Block (Noble, CR 1/8)
He does not fight. If cornered, he calls for guards (2d6 guards arrive in 1d4 rounds).  
**AC:** 15 (breastplate) | **HP:** 9  
**Rapier:** +3, 1d8+1 piercing (he has one; he has never used it)

---

## Lady Lydia Petrovna (His Wife)
The Baroness watches everything from windows and doorways. She has long since accepted that her husband is unwell and has been quietly managing what she can. She is exhausted. She is not cruel. She is complicit by omission.

If Izek is killed and Lady Wachter takes over, the Baroness quietly gathers her husband and moves him to a smaller house. She does not argue. She has been ready for this for years.

---

## After Removal from Power
Baron Vallakovich is confined to his room (by Lady Wachter's order) and declared "unwell." He is not harmed. He sits quietly and, occasionally, hums the tune to one of his festival songs. He looks, stripped of his title and his certainty, like someone's sad grandfather.
