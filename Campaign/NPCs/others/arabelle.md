# Arabelle
**Type:** Human Child (Divine Seeker Bloodline); Non-combatant  
**Role:** Luvash's Daughter; Seeker's Heir; Grand Conjunction Control Mechanism  
**First Appears:** Month 2, Week 2 (in Van Richten's wagon)

---

## Appearance
Approximately twelve years old. Dark eyes that look older than twelve. She was sitting cross-legged in Van Richten's wagon reading a book when the party found her.

## Personality
Calm, observant, occasionally unsettling in her precision. She accepts strange things with equanimity — she has been seeing things since she could walk. She is not naive; she is young in years and very old in ways that matter.

**Voice:** Quiet, direct. She speaks like Madam Eva would speak if Madam Eva were twelve.

**Key Lines:**
- "Hello. I know who you are. You're the ones from Madam Eva's vision. She told me you might come."
- "I had a dream last night. I dreamed the sun came back to Barovia. I think it has something to do with you."
- "Beware of the man with the silver arm. He is not the monster he appears, but he is dangerous to my father's brother."
- "Beware the pastry woman on the hill — her kindness is hungry."

---

## The Seeker's Bloodline
Arabelle is a direct descendant of the Seeker goddess — specifically, through the Seeker's mortal vessel line, of which Madam Eva is the current holder. This divine bloodline makes Arabelle extremely valuable to Strahd.

**Why Strahd wants her:** For the Grand Conjunction, Strahd needs a "control mechanism" — someone of divine bloodline to channel and direct the Fane power. Arabelle, as the Seeker's heir, fits this requirement precisely.

**She doesn't know this yet.** Her parents don't know this. Madam Eva knows, and has chosen not to tell anyone until necessary.

---

## After Her Rescue
Arabelle returns to her father Luvash. She is not traumatized by her stay in Van Richten's wagon — she found him interesting and they talked about monster lore for three days. She carries a copy of one of his journals that he gave her.

She appears briefly in Act IV when the Grand Conjunction threat becomes clear. The party must ensure she is nowhere near Castle Ravenloft during the final assault.

---

## Arrigal's Secret
Luvash's brother Arrigal is Strahd's spy within the Vistani camp. He has been feeding information to Strahd about the camp's activities, including Arabelle's location. He knew Van Richten had her and told Strahd. He chose not to intervene in the rescue — this is his one line he wouldn't cross. Harming Arabelle.

**If the party exposes Arrigal:** Luvash's grief and fury. Arrigal will not deny it — he will explain himself (he believed cooperating with Strahd protected the camp). He is not entirely wrong. He is also not entirely right.
