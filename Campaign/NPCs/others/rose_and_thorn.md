# Rose & Thorn Durst
**Type:** Ghost Children (no combat stats unless possessing)  
**Role:** Death House lure; tragic guides; first heartbreak of the campaign  
**Appears:** Month 1, Week 1 (Death House, Daggerford fog border)

---

## Appearance
**Rose (Rosavalda Durst):** A girl of about ten. Dark hair in braids, wearing a nightgown that is slightly too clean for the situation. She holds her brother's hand and does the talking.  
**Thorn (Thornboldt Durst):** A boy of about eight. Quieter than his sister. Tends to look at the floor. Occasionally stares at something no one else can see.

In their ghost forms (when revealed or triggered): translucent, flickering, still wearing the same clothing but subtly wrong — their feet don't quite touch the floor, their edges blur in bright light.

## Personality
**Rose:** Precise, determined, older than her years. She has been repeating this for a very long time and has settled into a patient desperation. She is the one who approaches the party, who asks for help, who steers them toward the house.

**Thorn:** Quieter, more obviously frightened. He follows his sister's lead. He is the one who looks sad when he thinks no one is watching.

**The truth:** They are already dead. They have been dead for years. They do not know this. Their bones are in a toy chest in the children's bedroom on the third floor. They have been haunting the entrance, luring people in, because they are trapped and the house will not let them go until it is destroyed.

When they find out they are dead (if the party discovers the skeletons and tells them): Rose goes very still. Thorn starts to cry silently. Rose then says, quietly: "Oh. We've been waiting a long time, haven't we."

---

## Key Lines
- **Rose:** "Please. There's a monster in our house. Our parents are asleep. We can't wake them."
- **Rose:** "The monster lives in the basement. Our parents said not to go there."
- **Thorn:** *(when asked if they're cold)* "Yes. Always."
- **Rose (ghost form, at the toy chest):** "Don't open it. Please. I don't want to see."
- **Rose (if told she's dead):** "Oh. We've been waiting a long time, haven't we."
- **Rose (if a player lets her possess them):** "Can I stay? Just for a little while? I'm so cold."

---

## The Possession (Rose Only)
Rose can attempt to possess a willing player character. If a player agrees:
- Rose uses the body gently — she explores the house, shows the party things she knows, looks out windows.
- She is not controlling or threatening. She is just a child who is cold and wants to feel warm.
- She will leave when asked. Immediately and without resistance.
- After leaving: she thanks the player by name. She has a first name for each character now, having used their body.

**Mechanics:** Treat as voluntary possession. Rose has no combat capability and will not use the body to fight. If the character is attacked while possessed, Rose immediately releases.

---

## The Skeletons (Third Floor, Toy Chest)
The children's skeletons are in the toy chest in their bedroom. Finding them causes:
- Rose screams (a child's scream, brief, quickly suppressed)
- Thorn reaches for Rose's hand
- If the party has been kind to them: the children can accept it, given time. Rose will tell the party that their parents' cult in the basement is what killed them — locked them in the attic to keep them safe from the monster, but the monster found them anyway.

---

## Resolution
When Death House collapses:
- The children's voices call out one final time: "Run. Please. *Run.*"
- They do not escape with the party. They cannot leave the house.
- If the house collapses fully: Rose and Thorn are finally freed. A faint warmth brushes past each party member as they exit — like the feeling of a small hand briefly held.

---

## Notes for DM
- Play them as children, not as horror devices. They are victims, not monsters.
- The emotional impact of Death House depends almost entirely on the party caring about Rose and Thorn before the skeletons are found.
- Do not rush their reveal. Let the party get attached first.
- If anyone in the party has a child NPC backstory, Rose will find them and stay close to them specifically.
