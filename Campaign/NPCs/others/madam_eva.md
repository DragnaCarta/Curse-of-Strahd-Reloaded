# Madam Eva
**Type:** Vistani Elder (Avatar of the Seeker Goddess); CR 10 if combat ever needed (never intended)  
**Role:** The Tarokka Reader; Avatar of the Seeker; Oracle  
**First Appears:** Month 1, Week 3 (Tser Pool camp)  
**Inspiration:** The Ancient One (Marvel), The Fates (mythology), Trelawney at her most genuine

---

## Appearance
Ancient. Not frail — simply old in the way that implies witnessing rather than decay. Her eyes are dark and calm as deep water. Her face is mapped with decades. She dresses simply. Her tent is layered with meaning — herbs, woven tapestries of stars and wolves, a brazier.

## Personality
She does not guess. She knows. She has been waiting for the party since before they were born, because she exists somewhat outside of time. She speaks slowly, with long pauses. She is not ominous — she is sorrowful. She has seen this play out before. She hopes this time is different.

**Voice:** Slow, deliberate. She never rushes a word. Pauses last two or three beats.

**Key Lines:**
- "I have been expecting you. I have been expecting you since before you were born."
- "The cards do not lie. They never lie. They simply show the truth before you are ready to hear it."
- *(on Strahd)* "A man who loved once, and lost, and made the worst bargain imaginable to have that love again. He has been paying ever since."
- *(when asked if Strahd can be killed)* "He can. But first you must understand him."
- *(when asked about the Grand Conjunction)* "You are not ready for that answer. Come back when you've read the book."

## The Tarokka Reading (Month 1, Week 3) ⭐
This is the campaign's central expository scene. Madam Eva draws five cards:

**Card 1 — Tome of Strahd:** "This card tells of history — knowledge of the ancient. The master's secrets are hidden in [LOCATION]."

**Card 2 — Holy Symbol of Ravenkind:** "This card tells of a powerful force for good — its voice is heard in the chirp of birds. Seek it where [LOCATION]."

**Card 3 — Sunsword:** "This is a card of power and strength. It tells of a weapon of sunlight. It slumbers in [LOCATION]."

**Card 4 — The Ally:** "This card sheds light in the darkness. An ally of flesh and blood whose fate is entwined with yours. Look for this one in [LOCATION]."

**Card 5 — The Enemy (Final Card):** "This… is the card that matters most. The one who will stand in the darkness's path. That is you. All of you. If you are willing."

> **DM NOTE:** Card locations must be determined BEFORE Session 1 (Week 1 plan.md). See the official *Curse of Strahd* Tarokka deck placement rules in the Appendix for all possible locations by card draw. Record the results and never change them.

## What Madam Eva Actually Is
She is the avatar of the Seeker goddess — the third of the Ladies Three (autumn, foresight, wisdom, harvest). The Seeker gave her sight to a mortal vessel centuries ago. Madam Eva has lived far beyond normal human years because the goddess sustains her. She is tired. She is ready to pass this role on when the time comes.

**Arabelle** (her grand-niece) carries the Seeker's divine bloodline — which is why Strahd wants her.

## Answering Player Questions
| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| "Who are you?" | "A very tired old woman. And something older than that." |
| "Can we trust you?" | "You can trust that I have no interest in deceiving you. Whether that is trust, you must decide." |
| "What will happen to Barovia if we fail?" | *(pause)* "What always happens. More centuries of this." |
| "Do you know Strahd?" | "I have known him since he was young. He was not always this." |
| "What is Arabelle?" | "She is herself. That is sufficient." *(will not elaborate)* |
