# Stella Wachter
**Type:** Human (Catatonic / Recovering); no combat stats  
**Role:** Lady Wachter's Daughter; Victor's Catalyst; Arc H McGuffin with a Soul  
**Appears:** Month 2, Week 2 (catatonic in a chair at Wachterhaus); Month 2, Week 4 (rescued)

---

## Appearance
Early twenties. Before the accident: people who knew her say she was vibrant, opinionated, quick to laugh. Now she sits in a chair in the corner of the Wachterhaus sitting room and stares at nothing. She breathes. She blinks. She does not respond to her name.

After her rescue: the first thing she does is take a gasping breath and say "Mama?" Then she blinks at her mother as if she doesn't quite believe what she's seeing. She is confused, thin, and slightly translucent around the edges for a day or two as her soul fully reintegrates.

## What Happened
Victor Vallakovich — using forbidden mirror-magic from his father's library — accidentally shattered Stella's soul during an experiment. Her consciousness was scattered through the Ethereal Plane. Her body has been sitting in that chair ever since.

Victor has been trying to fix it for weeks. He couldn't. He needed a heartstone, a full moon, and backup, and was too proud to ask for any of it.

## Personality (Post-Rescue)
She is, genuinely, her own person — not a reflection of what happened to her. She is warm, direct, with a dry humor she clearly didn't get from her mother. She recovers faster than anyone expects because she spent weeks watching the Ethereal Plane and has processed a great deal.

She does not resent Victor. This surprises people, including Victor. "He made a mistake. He was trying to help. People make mistakes when they're trying to help." She has, somehow, more grace about it than Lady Wachter does.

**Voice:** Warm, slightly hoarse at first (the body has been mostly still), quick to recover into wry.

**Key Lines:**
- *(first moment of consciousness)* "Mama?"
- *(to Victor, when she sees him across the room)* "You look terrible." *(pause)* "Thank you for not stopping."
- *(to the party, days later)* "I don't know exactly what you did, but I'm told it involved a night hag and a ghost, and I've decided not to ask for details."

---

## Lady Wachter's Reaction to the Rescue
Lady Wachter crosses the room in four steps and holds her daughter's face in both hands. She says "Stella. Stella." She says nothing else. She does not cry where anyone can see her. She turns away once to compose herself.

This is Lady Wachter fully exposed. Every calculation, every political move, every moral compromise — all of it lives in that room, and it weighs nothing against her daughter's face.

---

## Victor and Stella
Victor loves Stella. He does not know how to say this. He has known her since they were children (their families have been rivals/neighbors in Vallaki for decades) and has been working up to saying something for approximately three years. He watched her soul get shattered because of his mistake. He spent weeks trying to fix it.

When she is restored and he sees her from across the room: he says nothing.

In the epilogue: he finally says something. It comes out badly. She laughs. This is the best possible outcome.

## Campaign Arc
- **Month 2, Week 2:** Catatonic in Wachterhaus — players see her, are told about Victor's accident.
- **Month 2, Week 4:** Rescued via the Ethereal Plane ritual. The reunion.
- **Act III onward:** Recovering. She begins to assert herself in Wachterhaus. Lady Wachter listens.
- **War Council:** She attends. She volunteers to help with civilian coordination. Her mother doesn't argue.
