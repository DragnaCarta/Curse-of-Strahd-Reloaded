# Curse of Strahd — Story Synopsis

> *A group of adventurers is pulled into a fog-shrouded valley ruled by the immortal vampire lord Strahd von Zarovich, who seeks to escape his prison by sacrificing the soul of a woman he has loved — and lost — across three hundred years of death and reincarnation. To survive, the adventurers must forge alliances, recover ancient weapons, and confront not only a monster, but the tragedy that made one.*

---

## Background

Centuries ago, a brilliant military commander named **Strahd von Zarovich** conquered a remote mountain valley for his aging father, founding the realm of Barovia. Among the people of the valley, he met a young woman named **Tatyana** — and fell in love with her for the first and only time in his life. Tatyana, however, loved his younger brother **Sergei**. Strahd, who had given his entire life to war and duty while his brother grew up soft and beloved, watched Tatyana choose Sergei. On the day of their wedding, consumed by jealousy and a desperation he had never allowed himself before, Strahd made a pact with dark forces known as the **Dark Powers** — immortality in exchange for his soul. He murdered Sergei at the altar. Tatyana, in grief and terror, threw herself from the castle battlements. She did not survive.

The Dark Powers fulfilled their promise. Strahd became a vampire — the first vampire. But he did not escape grief. Tatyana's soul has reincarnated repeatedly across the centuries in the valley, always appearing as a young woman who looks exactly like her — always eventually dying before Strahd can keep her. He has filled journals with their names. He has lost count of the attempts.

The valley itself is now a closed demiplane — surrounded by impenetrable fog called the **Mists**, from which no one escapes. Barovia's people live under permanent grey skies, in poverty and fear, generation after generation, with no way out. Strahd rules from **Castle Ravenloft**, high on a spur of the Pillarstone of Ravenloft, overlooking a valley that is essentially his prison as much as theirs.

He has a plan to end this. He calls it the **Grand Conjunction**.

---

## Act I — Into the Mists

A group of adventurers from elsewhere in the world — walking a familiar road, minding their own business — steps through the Mists without warning and finds themselves on the **Old Svalich Road**, in a forest that is not their forest, under a sky that is not their sky. The fog is behind them. Moving into it just puts them back where they started.

They reach the **Village of Barovia** at the end of the road: a cluster of stone houses, shuttered windows, no light in the streets, the faint sound of someone weeping somewhere they can't locate. The village has just endured a siege by Strahd's undead — called every night as punishment for the villagers' refusal to give up their burgomasters' daughter, **Ireena Kolyana**, to Strahd's brides. The burgomaster **Kolyan Indirovich** died of a heart attack during the siege. His son, **Ismark**, needs the party to help bury his father and escort Ireena to the safety of the walled town of **Vallaki** before Strahd comes for her again.

The adventurers also stumble into **Death House** — a haunted manor whose ghost children, **Rose** and **Thorn Durst**, lure them inside. The house is a trap that once belonged to a cult that worshipped Strahd, and its basement contains a ritual chamber around a monstrous creature called the **Flesh Mound**, formed from the cult's victims. The party must survive the house, defeat the creature, and escape before the building collapses. Strahd observes all of this with interest from a distance.

On the road west toward Vallaki, the party passes through the **River Ivlis Crossroads**, where Strahd appears — not as a threat, but as an observer, dressed in fine clothes, politely curious. He has seen their kind before. He watches.

At **Tser Pool**, they encounter **Madam Eva** — an ancient Vistani woman (and secretly the avatar of the Seeker, one of the valley's three old gods) who reads their fortunes with a Tarokka card deck. Her reading tells them what they need to know: the location of three ancient weapons that can harm Strahd, and a foretold ally who will join their cause. These readings are genuine. Everything she tells them is the truth.

## Act II — The Shadowed Town

Vallaki is a walled town presided over by **Baron Vargas Vallakovich**, a delusional tyrant who forces his subjects to attend mandatory "festivals of happiness" on pain of imprisonment, convinced that if Barovia's people simply pretend to be happy, Strahd's power will break. The current festival is called the **Festival of the Blazing Sun**, scheduled for Neyavr 7. The Baron's enforcer — **Izek Strazni**, a man with a devil's arm that throws fire — keeps the population in line.

At **St. Andral's Church**, Father Lucian discovers that the bones of Saint Andral — relics that power a magical ward keeping the church safe from undead — have been stolen. The groundskeeper **Milivoj** sold them to the coffin-maker **Henrik van der Voort**, who was coerced by **Volenta Popofsky**, one of Strahd's vampire brides, who has taken up residence in his shop with a nest of vampire spawn. The party must recover the bones before Volenta uses their absence to massacre the church's congregation on the feast day.

Elsewhere in Vallaki, a young Vistani girl named **Arabelle** — secretly the granddaughter of Madam Eva and the Seeker's chosen vessel — has been kidnapped. Her father **Luvash** is frantic. Her uncle **Arrigal** is hiding something. The party discovers she was taken and must recover her safely. They also begin to understand that Arabelle is specifically important to Strahd's plan: her blood carries the Seeker's divine essence, which is a required component for the Grand Conjunction.

**Lady Fiona Wachter**, a Barovian noblewoman, approaches the party with a proposition. She wants to overthrow the Baron — replacing him with someone who will keep Vallaki's people alive by not provoking Strahd. She is pragmatic to the point of darkness, a Strahd appeaser, but she is right that the Baron's festival obsession is going to get people killed. Her daughter **Stella** is catatonic — her soul has been shattered and scattered across the Ethereal Plane, a side effect of magical experiments by her son **Victor**, who was trying to escape Barovia. Victor, horrified by what he did, has been trying to fix it, aided by the ghost **Erasmus van Richten** — the son of the vampire hunter Van Richten, killed years ago and trapped in Barovia's Ethereal Plane. Rescuing Stella requires the party to enter the Ethereal Plane and reassemble her soul.

**Rudolph van Richten** — the famous vampire hunter — has been hiding in Vallaki disguised as a carnival performer called **Rictavio**. His cover is eventually blown. He is a brilliant, driven, grief-hollowed man who blames himself for Erasmus's death and has been hunting Strahd alone for years. He becomes an ally, if a difficult one.

Izek Strazni's obsession with Ireena — he has been having Blinsky the toymaker create dolls in her image for years, not understanding why — turns out to be the result of a forgotten sibling bond: Izek and Ireena are long-separated brother and sister. This revelation complicates any fight with him and offers a potential resolution that doesn't end in death.

The Festival of the Blazing Sun becomes the pressure point of the act. Whether through the party's direct action or Lady Wachter's scheming, the festival collapses — and so does the Baron's grip on power. Wachter takes control of Vallaki. Strahd watches this too. He is pleased that someone sensible is in charge, briefly.

The party eventually passes through or helps the village of **Krezk** — a fortified village whose burgomaster, **Baron Krezkov**, is hiding his own secret (his son Ilya is being held by **Baba Lysaga**, the swamp witch of Berez, as leverage over Krezkov's cooperation). They reach the **Abbey of Saint Markovia**, where they meet the **Abbot** — a fallen deva who has been isolated in Barovia for two centuries, slowly going mad without divine guidance, who is constructing a flesh golem bride named **Vasilka** to give to Strahd in the hope of appeasing him. He is sincere. He is wrong. He can be redeemed.

## Act III — The Broken Land

The **Wizard of Wines**, a winery outside Krezk run by the Martikov family (wereravens who serve as Keepers of the Feather — Strahd's secret resistance), has been invaded by a druidic cult that worships Strahd as a nature god. The druids have stolen one of three magical gems from the winery — gems that once anchored the divine presence of the valley's old gods and still mysteriously accelerate the vineyard's growth. They have carried the gem to **Yester Hill**, a standing-stone summit, where they are using it to animate a massive tree blight called **Wintersplinter** that will destroy the winery entirely. The party must fight through the winery, pursue to Yester Hill, and stop the ritual.

In the forests, Strahd's werewolf pack — led by the aggressive **Kiril Stoyanovich** — is terrorizing Barovians and corrupting the pack's original purpose. The pack's true leaders, **Emil** and **Zuleika Toranescu**, want Kiril gone. Zuleika holds the **Holy Symbol of Ravenkind**, a golden sunburst artifact of the old gods, and will give it to the party in exchange for Emil's rescue from Castle Ravenloft's dungeons.

The party receives an invitation to dine with Strahd at **Castle Ravenloft** — a formal dinner, elegant, genuinely civil, with food and wine and Strahd's full charm on display. His brides attend. Ireena is there. Strahd plays the gracious host all evening while making absolutely clear that he considers himself the party's inevitable superior. Before leaving, one of his brides — **Sasha Ivliskova**, quiet and resigned and tired — slips the party an iron key to the castle's east crypt passage. She says nothing else. She doesn't have to.

Armed with the key, an alliance with the Martikovs, and Zuleika's deal, the party undertakes a heist of Castle Ravenloft. They have five objectives: recover **Argynvost's skull** from the Hall of Bones, retrieve the **Spear of the Huntress** from King Dostron's crypt, recover the **Icon of Ravenloft** from Saint Markovia's crypt, rescue **Emil Toranescu** and **Gertruda** (Doru's betrothed) from the dungeons, and escape without triggering a full castle response. Sasha's key gets them in. A sympathetic dungeon guard lets Emil out. Strahd knows they are there — and lets it happen, because he is curious about how far they'll get.

The heist concludes with the party returning Argynvost's skull to **Argynvostholt**, the ruined manor of the Order of the Silver Dragon — a knightly order that opposed Strahd 300 years ago and whose members, as revenants, have been trapped in the ruins ever since by their own guilt and the bitter rage of their commander **Vladimir Horngaard**, who blames Argynvost for the Order's failure. With the skull returned and the revenants' unfinished business addressed, Argynvost's ghost finds rest enough to light the **Beacon of Argynvostholt** — a lighthouse visible across the valley, a signal that resistance is possible. It means nothing to Strahd practically. It means everything to Barovia's people.

Strahd shifts. The Gentleman disappears. The Tyrant begins: nightly attacks on the party's rest, psychological pressure, direct harassment. He is no longer curious. He is annoyed.

## Act IV — Secrets of the Ancient

The path to the campaign's final objectives passes through **Tsolenka Pass** — a mountain road in winter, guarded by a gatehouse fortified with Strahd's servants. Beyond the pass, in the mountains, is the village of **Soldav**, home to **Elder Burebis**, a dying storm giant who is the last keeper of the valley's oldest lore. He tells the party what Madam Eva's reading implied but could not fully explain: the **Three Fanes** — the Forest Fane at Yester Hill, the Swamp Fane at Berez, and the Mountain Fane at the foot of Mount Ghakis — are the original sacred sites of the valley's three old gods (the Huntress, the Weaver, and the Seeker). Strahd desecrated all three when he first arrived, binding their power to himself, giving him control of the land itself — which is why the fog persists, why crops fail, why the people cannot leave. Reconsecrating all three will strip him of his land-bond and his lair actions in the final battle.

At the **Amber Temple** — a ziggurat built into the peak of Mount Ghakis, carved from amber, ancient beyond memory, once a prison for the dark entities called vestiges — the party meets **Exethanter**, the ghost of the archmage who built the temple. He has lost his memory over millennia. Restoring it reveals that he taught Strahd his cipher, among other things. He helps the party decode the **Tome of Strahd** and shows them the Sunsword's hilt, which lies inside the **Vampyr's** amber sarcophagus.

The Vampyr — the dark entity whose pact Strahd accepted — still exists in the temple as a spirit. It still whispers. It offered amber shards to the party as dark gifts: real power, real cost. Patrina Velikovna's spirit guards the Sunsword's hilt; the party must defeat her in the amber chamber. To empower the hilt into the full **Sunsword**, they must summon the spirit of **Sergei von Zarovich** — Strahd's murdered brother — from the Mists and ask for the blade with a sincere purpose. Sergei, pacific and sorrowful, will empower the sword and ask only that his message be delivered to Strahd: "Tell him I forgive him."

The party reconsecrates all three Fanes in sequence. With the **Spear of the Huntress** driven into the Forest Fane's stone circle, the first Fane is freed. With a life-gem planted at Berez — with Davian Martikov carrying it himself, fulfilling five generations of his family's promise — the Swamp Fane is freed, requiring first the defeat of Baba Lysaga, who guards it. With the third gem planted at the Mountain Fane by Lady Wachter, completing her redemption from Strahd's appeaser to active resistance, the final Fane is freed.

Strahd shifts again. The Monster emerges: no more theater, no more personas. Pure efficiency.

The party conducts a **war council** in Vallaki — gathering every ally they have made: the Martikovs, Van Richten, Ezmerelda, Lady Wachter, the freed werewolf pack, the revenants of Argynvostholt, Father Lucian, Baron Krezkov. They plan the final assault. That night, in sleep, the **Dark Powers** offer the party a bargain: leave Barovia. The fog will part for them and only them. Barovia's people will continue as they are, but the party will be free. The right answer is no. The dawn begins immediately after this refusal.

The final assault on Castle Ravenloft. The **Heart of Sorrow** — a massive crystal heart beating at the castle's highest spire, the device that has been harvesting Barovian souls for the Grand Conjunction — must be destroyed first. Rahadin, loyal without reservation and without comprehension of what Strahd has planned for him, defends the castle. Strahd himself waits in the throne room.

The fight is brutal. Strahd is fast, cunning, genuinely dangerous, and has three hundred years of combat experience against vampire hunters. The Sunsword suppresses his regeneration. The Holy Symbol turns his spawn. The consecrated Fanes have stripped his lair actions. Every choice the party made — every ally forged, every artifact found, every arc completed — matters here.

Strahd dies. Not as a monster. As a man who made a terrible choice three hundred years ago and has been paying for it, and making others pay for it, ever since. If the party delivers Sergei's message: Strahd receives it. He says nothing. Whether it means anything to him, in those last moments, is left for the table to decide.

The fog lifts. For the first time in living memory — in many lifetimes of living memory — the sun rises over Barovia. The people step outside and look up, not entirely sure what to do with it.

Ireena Kolyana — who is, without anyone having confirmed it in so many words, the latest reincarnation of Tatyana — is herself. Not Tatyana. Her own person, with her own life, finally free to live it. She does not remember the other lives. She does not need to.

## Themes

**Curse of Strahd** is, at its core, a tragedy about a man who had everything — intelligence, competence, loyalty, achievement — but could not bear that one thing he wanted was given to someone else. His evil is not mysterious evil. It is recognizable evil: jealousy, entitlement, the refusal to let go. Three hundred years of immortality have not given him perspective. They have only given him more time.

The campaign is also about Barovia's people — not just Strahd. Every major NPC has a life that exists independent of the party's presence. Father Lucian holds a congregation together under impossible circumstances. Lady Wachter made a deal she cannot undo and lives with it. Ismark carries his father's memory and his sister's danger. Davian Martikov tends a vineyard that keeps failing, season after season, because three gems that belong to the land were stolen.

The adventurers do not save a town. They save a world — one person at a time, one act of stubbornness at a time — by refusing to accept that this is simply how things are.
